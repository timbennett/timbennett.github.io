Sky Ghetto

By ReneSchaefer 2009-03-16T00:00:00+00:00

Acid-fried folk meets warped electronica in a riot of intro-extroversion. Sexy beats for sexy beasts.

Cougar Flashy used to be Fulton Girls Club, but he isn’t anymore. Cougar Flashy sings in an American accent because that’s where he’s from (Illinois, to be precise), but he lives in Melbourne in a permanent state of hyperactivity. He’s got a million projects all going on at the same time. He draws comics, writes songs, raps, travels, collaborates with other artists and musicians and basically spreads his demented genius and good cheer wherever he goes.

Some of Sky Ghetto sounds like it was recorded by stoned munchkins, some of it sounds like Lou Barlow jamming with Daniel Johnston on ?shrooms. All of it is home-spun, hand-drawn, simple but dense. It’s an incoherent but totally captivating private universe full of in-jokes, wistful interludes and tokens of a life lived to the fullest. Unlike a lot of this kind of music, none of it seems contrived just to be ?weird?. Cougar Flashy doesn’t have to try – this strange light just pours out of him.

It could all fall flat; the dinky percussion, the cut-ups, the funny voices and odd gibberings. Somehow it all gels into this mega-artwork, especially when taken in combination with Flashy’s new comic Suck My Scab*, which is *Sky Ghetto?s visual analogue – every page crammed full of mystical hybrid creatures, talking clouds, disembodied spirits and disjointed pop culture narratives.

No matter how crazed things get though, one thing that shines through is Cougar Flashy’s intelligence and musical talent, which he employs in action painting style by throwing everything at his canvas and seeing what shapes and patterns will emerge. As he states on his MySpace page, his ambition is to “make stuff, grow, fuck expectations, build forts”. As a life philosophy, I think he’s onto something there.
