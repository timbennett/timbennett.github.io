Track By Track: The Stevens

By Mess+Noise 2013-05-13T14:00:00+00:00

Clocking in at just 13 minutes, The Stevens? miraculous self-titled debut EP has been re-released by Chapter. All its filthy secrets are revealed below, thanks to guitarist-singers ALEX MACFARLANE* and *TRAVIS MACDONALD plus ?someone called Steven who represents all Stevens past present and future.?

?Alone?

Travis: This song is about people who are proud of being left alone, so much so that it attracts attention. This song isn’t about anyone in particular; I just thought it would be something interesting to write about. People tell me that this song has a memorable chorus.

Alex: Since going through puberty I cannot hit the high notes.

Steven: Initially when I heard this song, I thought the lyrics were ?The girls I harass will be no less than 10 years old.?

Alone by The Stevens

?I Look Back?

Alex: I reckon I’ve been a dick to a lot of people, so this is a general apology to everyone who’s come into contact with me. I found an older version of this that I recorded where the lyrics are ?Don’t look back, don’t feel sad,? which is much worse and much less accurate.

Steven: Sometimes we all look back and feel bad. My trick is to play some Fu Manchu and rub myself while thinking about all the love that I missed out on at university.

I Look Back by The Stevens

?Teenage Satellites?

Travis: I wrote this song with 21st-century communication methods in mind. I have found that mobile phones and social networking has made it very hard for people to be natural strangers. I feel I know an unnatural amount about things and people I would never interact with otherwise. I like sci-fi theme music; I ?borrowed? some melodies from the Star Trek theme song.

Steven: This song was never called ?Million Miles?. I think people assumed this title because the rest of the songs on this EP are cleverly named after their most repeated lyric.

Teenage Satellites by The Stevens

?Weaving?

Alex: Most of the songs on this EP are actually quite old now, a few years old. The name of this one came about when Matty Mac (my father) came into my room to deliver Melbourne Bitter stubbies to me and Travis while we were trying to write some songs for the newly formed Stevens. When he heard the opening riff of this song he said, ?This sounds like two guitars weaving a basket.? The song is about not feeling good, and making people around you feel not good also.

Weaving by The Stevens

?Out of a Bag?

Alex: This one is about people moving overseas and doing the same things but with different scenery, then moving back home and doing the same things again. It’s also about my buddy Miles Leonard], who I was in a band with called [Breaker Morant. He moved away and I didn’t want him to because he is a good guy. He’s coming back though, so it’s all OK.

Out Of A Bag by The Stevens

?Fast Cars?

Travis: I wrote this song about fast cars. I like cars. I have noticed that a lot of small people tend to be passionate about cars. They are the modern equivalent of horses. Horses were used to hunt for food, and were symbols of power and even worn as clothing. Today people use cars for the similar purpose of hunting for bank and freedom. Like horses, they have names. I chose to mention GTO and Plymouth because they are iconic. I try and sound like Joey Ramone when I say GTO sometimes.

Alex: The guitar solo is real good.

Fast Cars by The Stevens

##?The Stevens? is out now digitally and on 7? vinyl through Chapter Music. It launches in Melbourne this Friday (May 17) at The Tote with Love of Diagrams and Full Ugly. It’s $10 on the door from 9pm.
