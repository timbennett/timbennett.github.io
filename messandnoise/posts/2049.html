Nothin

By ReneSchaefer 2009-08-01T00:00:00+00:00

The debate over ?originality? in rock’n?roll has raged ever since rock stopped being a teenage dance craze and was (rightly or wrongly) elevated to the level of art, sometime in the mid-1960s. Ever since, there has been a tug-of-war between rivaling philosophies, ranging from the reactionary ?keep music stupid? purists to those who believe that the form needs to mutate and develop in new directions to remain relevant. There is nothing new to this back and forth and it is precisely this friction between conservatism and experimentation that keeps the music culture stew bubbling along.

In among all the critical chest-beating though, a large proportion of bands really just want to rock. Let’s remember that at its most basic level rock’n?roll will always be teenage music and the youngsters don’t really give a flying fuck what some puffed-up ?critic? thinks of their racket. They also don’t really worry about sounding derivative or mimicking their heroes, because that’s just what you do when you start out as a band.

So Bendigo natives Mother And Father play grunge rock like it was invented yesterday. They get very excited about doing this because they are young and weren’t around when Mudhoney first plugged in their Big Muff pedals, so it all sounds very new to them. Their press release is smart enough to acknowledge this by namedropping everyone from Kurt Cobain to The Pixies to The Beatles. It’s true, Greg Kerslake (guitar, vocals), Andrew Tolley (bass) and Andy Howard (drums) write great genre songs. On their debut album guitars rage, drums pound and vocal cords are shredded. Songs like ?Overtired? brim with an enthusiasm that sounds fresher than anything silverchair ever put out.

Nothin? is only the second release on Z-Man Records, following on from Witch Hats? new EP, and as such it tests the waters for the fledgling Melbourne indie. I have no idea whether banking on yet another grunge revival is on the money or not. Arguably, the genre never really went away. It’s easy to understand its appeal to white middle-class youth who are sussed enough not to adopt the trappings of other imported pop fashions such as emo or hip-hop, and prefer to ?keep it real? by pulling bongs in their parents? garage and raiding their older siblings? record collections.

Maybe grunge is as good an entry point into rock culture as any. If a band like Children Collide can outgrow their early grunge leanings and become hugely popular, why not these guys? On songs like ?Oh No?, ?Ignored? and ?Keeping, Sleeping?, Mother And Father prove that they are certainly capable of evolving into songwriters with a great ear for catchy melodies and sharp pop hooks. Maybe they could have benefited from waiting a little longer before putting out a release and developing the subtler side of their music in the meantime, but Nothin? is a decent starting point.
