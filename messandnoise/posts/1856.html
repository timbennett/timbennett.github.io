Romance and Adventure

By Trevor 2008-11-05T00:00:00+00:00

This album is fucking awesome. Buy it. That’s it. Short, sweet, and it’s written in the sort of language that the Ooga Boogas would appreciate. But that won’t do.

From the gatefold cover art to the romantic opening line, to the final echoing feedback squawk, Romance and Adventure – the Oogas Boogas debut LP – is great. How great? Take the pedigrees of the band members, who collectively hail from the Onyas, Eddy Current Suppression Ring, the Sailors and a fistful of impossibly obscure Swedish garage bands. Take the song titles; the fucked-up running order, which sees ‘Ooga Booga II’ open side one, and ‘Ooga Booga I’ close it; the way it was made, locked in a remote farmhouse for a drunken long weekend; and most importantly, the way it hangs together as some kind of sonic monument to the art of getting fucked up and loose while still keeping it tight.

No one involved looks like the angry political sort who is out subvert the dominant paradigm – and yet they do it all the same. At a time when many whinge and fret about the future of music distribution and the profitability of live acts, the Ooga Boogas seem to have hit on some answers. Like launching their album in the afternoon at a Fitzroy pizza shop instead of a showcase at a mid-sized venue, for instance. Like going to the US to play Gonerfest after only doing three dates at home. (They were asked back a year later to do it again). Like running their own record label and issuing their own records. All this from a bunch of guys whose founding motto was ?no ambition?.

But what about the music? There is a great opening line here, ?I’m an Ooga Booga/ And my cock’s made of wood?, which Stacky sings over a raucous mass of fuzz guitar that sounds like it’s coming out of a Marshall stack full of blowflies. With a few octopus and squid references thrown in, this basically sets the tone and the pace for the whole album. It’s basic, beat-driven garage rock all the way. ECSR fans looking to hear Mikey Young’s distinctive sound replicated here will be disappointed – he left it at the door. Sailors fans looking for a dose of leering boy/girl chat will be pleased though.

This vinyl-only release is limited to 500 copies, which will probably have all been sold by the time you read this. There are noises about a ?proper? re-release, but it’ll most likely be a few CD-Rs. Move quick.
