I?ve Got a Friend Called Emily Ferris

By Doug Wallen 2012-04-01T00:00:00+00:00

Courtney Barnett isn’t the first person to record songs in a lounge room with some friends. And while the self-described ?slacker garage pop? of her first EP has a shambling, psych-waggled continuity that makes the six songs blend into one winding road, the music isn’t the reason for sticking around. It’s Barnett’s lyrics that set her apart, right from the opening line: ?I masturbated to the songs you wrote.?
But she’s not going for shock value, or for naked confession. She’s more like US ?anti-folk? songwriters Jeffrey Lewis and Kimya Dawson, sighing off lyrics that couch their insights in fleeting thoughts and deadpan punchlines. ?You should probably call me more,? she sings offhandedly on ?Canned Tomatoes (Whole)?, after citing a routine that includes coffee on the garden wall, Monday morning laundry and sifting through vaguely threatening mail (?Pay your rego by the 4th?) for the song’s absent subject. Barnett’s voice is dry and chalky, like a rough outline.

Courtney Barnett: Canned Tomatoes (Whole) by -gaga

At nearly eight minutes, ?Are You Looking After Yourself?? is the standout. It takes a worried parent’s point of view in places, asking, ?Are you working? We’re so worried? and similar queries. ?I don’t want no 9-to-5/Telling me that I’m alive,? Barnett answers. The back-and-forth continues with just as much disconnect: ?Have you got some money saved up for those rainy days?/You should start some sort of trust fund just in case you fail.? But Barnett is content to be unambitious and in good company: ?My friends play in bands/They are better than everything on the radio.?

Familiar sentiments, admittedly. But Barnett is wittier than most and seems to find both absurdity and reason in the song’s sparring lifestyles. Lurching wobbles of electric guitar consume the last few minutes, without disrupting the easygoing gait sustained for most of the EP. Likewise, some psych gnashing elsewhere and piano accompaniment and self-harmonising on ?Porcelain? don’t disrupt the folky ramble.
Despite the EP’s homespun nature, Barnett is no amateur. She plays in the duo Cottonbed with Immigrant Union singer Bob Harrow, the bluegrass band Merri Creek Pickers and the surf-ish act The Olivettes. She also plays slide guitar in Immigrant Union, whose Brent DeBoer (also of the Dandy Warhols) drums for her. She may be radiating slacker vibes with the doodled cover art and no-frills recording, but Barnett’s songs are much more studied than their shrugging exterior.
