The Saddest

By JodyMacgregor 2009-11-01T00:00:00+00:00

Extrafoxx is Conwae Burrell and The Saddest is his bedroom project. It’s the sound of one man shut in his room, presumably after a breakup, confronting all of his demons one-by-one. It’s like one of those medieval bestiaries only with neuroses instead of griffins. If the title of the album doesn’t give you enough of an idea what a cheery ride you’re in for, here are some of the track titles: ?Phoney?, ?Appreciate Me?, ?I Lied? and ?If You Can’t Be Nice?. Every song features droning, simple guitar lines and mopey lyrics with lots of repetition. Burrell talk-sings as if he’s so laconic – lying back on his bed eating the wine gums of despair – that he can’t be bothered singing. And it’s glorious.

Burrell’s vocals are echoed; doubled-up (thanks to GarageBand) so that he’s his only accompaniment. From this lonely world he delivers lines like, ?I’m the saddest on Saturday?, over and over again until it can’t help but be a bit funny. It’s such an exaggerated version of sadness that it becomes hysterical. But is Burrell sharing these smiles or is his unhappiness genuine? It’s the tears of a clown dilemma: ?You don’t appreciate me at all? he sings in ?Appreciate Me?. Oh, but I do, Extrafoxx, I do. I’m smiling right now.

Even when he becomes playful for a minute or two – few of the songs last much longer than that – Burrell’s voice never picks up and there’s no confirmation that he’s in one the joke: ?You’re top shit, believe it,? he sings on ?Song 4 My Friends?, ?and if anybody tries to tell you otherwise, I will make them burst into flames.? The delivery is entirely earnest, which only makes it better. ?A Story? is about his attempts to write a book about a hero and heroine struggling through the darkness; it’s repeated in its entirety three times over the course of the album’s 35 tracks. It never changes and he never gets to the end.

Listening to the blues is always a good way to pick yourself up because it puts all of your trivial problems in perspective by comparing them to someone else’s. It doesn’t matter whether they’re real and sincere or not, that’s exactly what Extrafoxx provides with his lo-fi monologuing. It’s schadenfreude rock. May he never cheer up.
