Track by Track: Bad Family

By Mess+Noise 2014-08-12T14:00:00+00:00

Melbourne quartet Bad Family write breathy, jangle-laden pop that recalls The Smiths and Material Issue as well as lesser-known bands like Kincaid and Aden. Below, members LUC DAWSON*, **ADAM MADRIC**, **LACHIE RANKIN** and *HARRISON SMART dissect their self-titled debut LP.

?Lilac Mouth?

LD: A friend told me of a woman she saw outside Centrelink huffin? paint from a shopping bag. The paint was lilac coloured and I found that contrast interesting. It is inspired by the same friend.

Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?Playing Spoons?

LD: This was written for a dear friend of mine, whom I owe a great deal to. It’s also about regrettable behaviour.

Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?Off to Bed?

LD: When the best part of your day is going to bed again.

Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?Proximity?

AM: Sook song. Was feeling pretty helpless in a relationship. Nothing else mattered and I found it ironic that the more time spent together, the further apart we were becoming. We’re cool now though. <3 Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?15?

LD: Home.
AM: Home.
LR: I’ve had more beers at this house than anywhere else. And I’ve had a lot of beers?
HS: We also recorded the album here.

Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?Kate & Tony?

HS: It’s an observation of drug-addicted lovers. Luc and I witnessed a lot of this whilst working together at Grindhouse in Balaclava. We had friends there who were drug dependent; they were really nice. Nicer than the people that frequent frozen-yoghurt franchises.

Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?Dusty?

LD: Written in a time of more or less constant ?recreating?.
AM: I remember this song changed a million times. Pretty sure it was the first too. We put heaps of brainpower into this one. Dunno if it shows.
HS: Someone said it reminds them of 90210.

Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?Weak to Week?

LD: Everybody feels a little flat and disenchanted with the way things go sometimes. It is important to keep your head up or things can get unhealthy.
AM: The outro is real dreamy. ~8~

Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?Bleak?

AM: About accepting a hollow feeling that was lurking for too long. Everything I looked at was charmless and bleak. But I also knew it was coming to an end; there was light on the horizon when I wrote it. That feeling lives in the past now. Mostly.

Bad Family by BAD FAMILY

?Old Habits?

LD: I guess the album has recurring themes of disenchantment, escapism and detachment and their ramifications.
AM: Yeah, I think this song encapsulates the essence of the record. Dual harmonies are fun too.
