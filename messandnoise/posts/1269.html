Track by Track: Panoptique Electrical

By Mess+Noise 2014-10-12T13:00:00+00:00

JASON SWEENEY marries glacial ambience and classical majesty with his project Panoptique Electrical. His gorgeous new album charts the aftermath of a broken heart.

?Said?

That moment you hear that dreaded phrase ?we need to talk.? Kind of like suspended animation, everything freezes or moves in a very slow motion. What follows is a confession you don’t want to hear or a statement of finality that you can do nothing about. Each word spoken is like a bullet through the flesh. Your heart just sinks and sinks some more until it threatens to fail altogether.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical

?Pain?

The pain that follows. The pain that feels like it will go on forever. You are now fully immersed in the ensuing stages of grieving.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical

?Fury?

Then the anger, seething, almost murderous. You recall the words said, analysing each one, all through the sleepless nights.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical

?Lost?

You walk the streets in an endless daze. You cannot work. What once was pleasurable has no appeal. You’re empty.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical

?Alone?

No matter what words of comfort find their way to your ears from friends or family, nothing will ease that feeling of disorienting isolation and despair. At this moment this feeling will go on forever.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical

?Action?

Until something breaks. It’s time for change, a shift, to take matters into your own hands. From out of the ether an energy suddenly propels you forward. Maybe this pain won’t actually last for eternity.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical

?Quiet?

A sort of resting point, a calm. Even sleep.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical

?Love?

And then it appears. Just when you thought you’d never have the capacity to feel again. Your heart begins to break open.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical

?Done?

What once was pain, fury, loss and loneliness is now only a dull sense, a blur, a thing of the past.

Love Lost in a Storm by Panoptique Electrical
