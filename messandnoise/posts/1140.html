Cellular Bits #4: Kane Ikin, Power Moves, HTML Flowers

By lawsonfletcher 2014-02-11T13:00:00+00:00

LAWSON FLETCHER rounds up the latest Bandcamp, SoundCloud and YouTube streams from the fringes of local electronic music.

Basenji
?Dawn?
(track, independent)

This young Sydney producer dropped two tracks on his SoundCloud in late 2013, ostensibly a homage to Cashmere Cat (?Tribute to the Cat?) and Cosmo’s Midnight (?Speak With a Dofflin?) respectively; the latter of whom, along with Wave Racer, has provided him with sage production mentorship. These artists give you a good sense of the kind of territory Basenji traverses, this giddying, instrumental-trap-redone-as-nursery-rhyme school of colourful, loopy electronics. But Basenji is also carving out a niche all of his own, yet more apparent in everything from the faux-regimented drums that open his latest freebie ?Dawn? to the bizarrely apposite dog-bark sample midway through; personal touches that speak to an ever more refined production sensibility in this gleefully heavy-but-light sound.

Amateur Dance
?what i had 2 do?
(track, independent)

Little more than a SoundCloud with less than 50 followers and no contact details, and a single, solitary, beautiful track. ?what i had 2 do? is a slowly unfurling piece of resampled piano house, deep on ambience and distinguished in its latter half by a completely unexpected repossession of a speech by bodybuilder CT Fletcher, his words spun into a far more emotionally complex tone than that of ?do u even lift??.

Kane Ikin
Hold Tight
(mini EP, //This Thing//)

An intensely beautiful and difficult pair of tracks to listen to if you know the context, Kane Ikin recorded ?Hold Tight? and ?Fallen on Strings? at his family home in summer of 2013 whilst spending time with his mother, who was suffering from cancer that would lead to her death only months later. Provided to //This Thing// and released as an online, price-flexible download, they stand both outside and within Ikin’s sonic trajectory, seemingly lighter to the touch but also hinting at an immense sadness underneath the foam of ambience and subtle guitar loops. An intimate slice of grief in the making.

Wave Racer
?Rock U Tonite? (Sable remix)
(From Re:Thinks Pt.2 // Waveracer, Diehigh Records)

Diehigh have their finger on the pulse of the Australian contingent of this glorious emerging neon club sound, and perhaps one of the most fruitful things to come from the label so far is Perth producer Sable?s remix of Wave Racer’s bubblegum rush ?Rock U Tonite?. Whereas the original goes into barely-in-control warp speed with its heavily vocoded lead, Sable strips back the vocal and reassembles the track into a just as exciting but somehow even more catchy post-trap beast, with a undeniable repeating synth melody. I’m feeling love in this club. Grab the whole collection of ?Re:Thinks?, and don’t sleep on CRNKN and Slumberjack’s takes either.

(Re:Thinks Pt.2 // Waveracer) by Waveracer

Power Moves
Psycho Shower Scene
(EP, Dream Damage)

Sydney producer Austin Buckett’s EP as Power Moves traverses the same kind of ?blinged- and bugged-out? aesthetic that Venezuelan producer Arca) has elevated to the level of fine art, perfectly capturing the always partial, garbled mistranslation of rap into other sonic and cultural contexts. More than happy to stray off the grid, and splattered with uncanny field samples and repurposed concrete sounds, Psycho Shower Scene is both absurd and kinda thrilling. It takes the standard, post-Dilla ?beats? template into a much-needed and far more exciting weird territory; a beat tape for the spirits haunting Hologram Tupac.

Psycho Shower Scene by POWER MOVES

Oneohtrix Point Never
?Americans? (NULL remix)
(track, independent)

Trawling through the SoundCloud of restless Melbourne producer NULL (n’e Rachel Haircut, n’e BKind2Me, n’e BK2M) furnishes a lot of gems. And not to discount his originals (the Reserve* EP in particular), but this refix of Oneohtrix Point Never’s ?Americans? is possibly pick of the bunch. *R Plus Seven always seemed destined for the club in some weird way – tracks like ?Zebra? are basically stomping house or maybe even hip-hop heaters simply stripped of beats – but NULL has gone one better here. Grafting a backing rhythm and heavily processed Janet Jackson sample onto the track (not to mention field-recording bookends) retrofits its sources into an inspired vaporware soft jam. One senses Dan Lopatin would appreciate the gesture. Listen out for it piping from the elevator speakers of the virtual plaza.

Tink
?HUNIDN1?
(track, independent)

Not to be confused with the US rapper of the same name currently blowing up Chicago (peep her vocal on ?Wanna Party? by Future Brown and be schooled), this Tink is flying the trap flag for the city of churches. Taking things into that oh-so-sweet 100bpm zone (hence the title), ?HUNIDN1? has that same finger-snapping crest perfected by US producers like Falcons and RL Grime, and keeps the ?OMG HERE COMES THE DROP? histrionics relatively subdued, favouring maintaining a groove over one-time wig-outs. She’s helping spearhead the post-trap scene in Adelaide as resident of the regular Trapped nights, but if she stays on this tip wider horizons beckon.

HWLS
?001?
(track, independent)

HWLS is the collaborative project of Perth powerhouses Ta-ku and Kit Pop, long-time friends and conspirators. In its current incarnation, HWLS is a semi-shady operation, with nary but a few numerically-labelled ?bass jawns? (read: killer instrumentals) up on SoundCloud to tide over the hungry hoards until something more tactile materialises. All tracks appear to be further collabs, like the first, ?001?, with US producer B.Lewis, an absolute trunk-rattler. ?003? may have arrived by the time you’re reading this; expect big things.

HTML Flowers
?ICANTHEARYOOOOOO ;) ;) ;)?
(track, independent)

Grant Gronewold (one half of Brothers Hand Mirror, with Oscar Key Sung) has thrown up this little gem under his HTML Flowers moniker, once reserved for his raps but now branching further into the production realm. What first seems like a SoundCloud sketch in the broken beat vein soon metamorphoses into a much more beguiling beast – a kind of granular, ambient footwork, or ?FOOTWART? as Gronewold tags the track, as if its frenetic beats were trying to escape the pull of its melody. A fragment of a journal one hopes he keeps penning ideas into.

Rainbow Chan
?Milk? (Cassius Select remix)
(From Long Vacation Remix EP, Silo Arts)

We know that Silo Arts has plenty of aces up its sleeve, but one can pretty safely assume that this will be the best cut from the forthcoming remix EP of Rainbow Chan’s breakout debut, due next month. Cassius Select has given ?Milk? the kind of remix treatment that crafts an entirely new composition out of the original’s stems, taking the slightest melodies and vocals from Chan’s bedroom pop and assembling a warm, driving house-techno chimera, shifting between sun and shade. An absolute delight.
