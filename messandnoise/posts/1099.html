Retrospective: Lost & Lonesome

By Mess+Noise 2013-11-19T13:00:00+00:00

To celebrate 15 years of his label Lost & Lonesome, MARK MONNONE (Monnone Alone, Last Leaves, The Lucksmiths) pinpoints some Aussie highlights of the back catalogue ahead of an all-day Melbourne Music Week showcase.

L&L001 – The Foots ?The Foots?

The first release for the label, from my erstwhile label buddy Jane McCracken’s band, which also featured Eva [Sommerfeld] from Hurdy Gurdy/Slurper. They have a new band together now called Wind Cheaters, who I can’t wait to see play their first show on Saturday!

L&L005 – Kirsty Stegwazi ?Jailbirds?

Kirsty Stegwazi was in The Bedridden, who should really be a household name. Also, Kirsty Stegwazi should be a household name. She played guitar and sang just like Cat Power, only more powerful and cat-like.

L&L007 – Lacto-Ovo ?Tsunami Pop? CD

Not saying the ?90s was the place to be, but somewhere between Poncherelli (whose drummer wore a CHIPS souvenir motorcycle helmet) and The Capgras Family, Lacto Ovo appeared and blended pulsing Stereolab-like exotica with Sonic Youth wiggy guitar stupidity. And it was cool.

L&L010 – Sleepy Township ?All These Records? CD

Sometime in the mid-?90s, in my opinion Sleepy Township were the best, sloppiest pop band in the land. Back when Guy Blackman was still interested in strumming the shit out of a guitar.

L&L022 – The Zebras ?Worry a Lot? CD

These ramblin?, janglin? Brisbane tequila fiends polished off this second album before relocating to Melbourne 10 or so years ago. Every song on this album is the best song ever. Well, until their new record comes out next year.

L&L037 – Fred Astereo ?Don’t Break My Heart?

Stanley Paulzen only ever wanted to get his music played on a golden oldies station, and this collection of tunes proves he’s well pitted against his heroes Perry Como and Frank Ifield, if not Jonathan Richman and Stephen Merritt.

L&L043 – The Smallgoods ?Down on the Farm?

These dudes proved there’s more to Port Fairy than folk music, whaling and bongs. This album deserves to be rediscovered in 20 years and filed alongside your favourite Elephant 6 records.

L&L047 – Mid-State Orange ?Odds?

As with many of the Lucksmiths titles on L&L, this album was taken in as an orphan when Candle Records closed up shop. Louis Richter is an friendly yet formidable guitarist and songwriter, and I know for a fact that the elusive second album is just around the corner!

L&L054 – The Lucksmiths ?First Frost?

The Lucksmiths? final studio album, recorded by the ace Chris Townend in his wild-man shack in the foothills of Mt Wellington in Hobart. It was snowing and the room was kept warm by Chris? potato and leek soup on the woodfire stove and the warm glow given off by a fortnight-long marathon of Lucksmiths wisecracks.

L&L071 – Francis Plagne ?Tenth Volume of Maps?

I loved Francis? earlier bedroom pop and musique concrete* tinkerings, so this album, full of gorgeously arranged strings and vast spaciousness, was a real surprise. Francis still loves a pop song though, and this album owes as much to *Nilsson Shmilsson* as it does *Ara’a Azul.

Francis Plagne – Spun Six Circles by Lost And Lonesome

+

####Lost & Lonesome 15 runs from 1 to 11pm this Saturday (Nov 23) at the Arts House as part of Melbourne Music Week’s Labels Series. Playing are Bart & Friends, Captain Coach, Footy, Great Earthquake, Tim Guy, The Icypoles, Last Leaves, Mid-State Orange, Milk Teddy, Monnone Alone, Tim Richmond, The Sublets, The Zebras and Wind Cheaters. Also check out a Lost & Lonesome sampler featuring new songs from many of those bands.
