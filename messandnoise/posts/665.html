New Noise: Frowning Clouds, Sand Pebbles, Margins, Ernest Ellis

By Darren Levin 2011-08-08T06:00:00+00:00

Our regular round-up of the best new music on the internet, featuring Geelong retro-fetishists Frowning Clouds, Melbourne psych collective The Sand Pebbles, instrumental outfit Margins and Sydney’s Ernest Ellis and his newly christened band, The Panamas.

? There might be a ?sly hint of surf? (and a titular nod to Lionel Richie) on the new single for Geelong retro fetishists Frowning Clouds, but it still sounds like some long lost cut off 1960s psych-garage comp Nuggets*. (And that’s just the way we like it, guys, so don’t ever change.) ?All Night Long? is the first sample from the five-piece’s upcoming second album, the follow-up to last year’s *Listen Closlier, which we described as penetrating ?the heart of a classic era of playing and songwriting?. No surprises to hear that ?All Night Long? and its b-side ‘Dame A Dozen’ were recorded to four-track cassette in the loungeroom of Owen Penglis (The Straight Arrows).

The Frowning Clouds – All Night Long by singhalamusic

The Frowning Clouds – Dame A Dozen by singhalamusic

? Is Sand Pebbles singer Tor Larsen channeling Siouxsie Sioux on the band’s new song ‘Occupied Europe [Take Me Across The Water]’? Well, that’s what guitarist Ben Michael reckons anyway – even though it’s supposed to be a love song told from a male perspective. The track comes from the band’s fifth album Dark Magic, which will be out on August 26 via Dot Dash/Remote Control. It features contributions from Dean Wareham (Galaxie 500), Britta Phillips (Luna), Tim Holmes (Death In Vegas), Will Carruthers (Spacemen 3, Spiritualized) and Malcolm McDowell.

Sand Pebbles – Occupied Europe by 00remotecontrol

? Like Ned Collette and Jack Ladder before him, Ernest Ellis has decided to give his long-suffering backing band a name and equal billing on new track ?Great Sky?. Backed by ?The Panamas? – Ben Morgan (bass, guitars and vocals), Mathew Gardner (drums, vocals), Russell W (keys) and Thom McAlister (guitars) – the track is reportedly the kind of thing he’s always wanted to record. It’s described as ?huge, blissful, heartbreaking and at the same time optimistic?. In other words: he’s been listening to The National a lot. Second album due out through Antelope/Inertia on September 23.

Ernest Ellis – Great Sky by Mess+Noise

? Slow and steady wins the race. This is Margins approach to making records – and it’s how they craft their songs, as well. All clean tones, tempo changes and dynamic shifts, ?Ice Station? is the slow-building first cut from new album Divide, which has been two years in the making. It’ll finally see the light of day in October this year through Casadeldisco Records (Harmony, Oh Mercy, The Nation Blue). The band – comprising members of The Nation Blue, Ricaine and Blacklevel Embassy – are currently on tour. Dates here.
