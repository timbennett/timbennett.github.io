New Noise: Boulet/Dennison, Oliver Tank, Hello Morning, Toot Toot Toots

By Edward Sharp-Paul 2011-11-22T13:00:00+00:00

Our latest installment of the best new sounds on the internet featuring Jonathan Boulet and Alyx Dennison “versus” Lamb, an offering from newcomer Oliver Tank, and returns for The Hello Morning and The Toot Toot Toots.

? Ahead of the release of new album 5*, long-running British electronic pop duo Lamb have commissioned a remix EP for lead single ?Build A Fire?. As well as Evil9, Gaudi and Lamb’s own Andy Barlow (under the moniker LOWB), there are contributions from Domeyko/Gonzalez, and an effort by Jonathan Boulet** and ky??s *Alyx Dennison. The latter, renamed ?Build A Fier?, is probably the pick of the lot – including the original. Boulet and Dennison do away with the xylophones and climactic choruses, and replace them with a synth burble and successive layers of gauze. Boulet offers a more subdued lead vocal than Lou Rhodes? slightly overwrought original, while Dennison’s impassioned yowling is an unsettling but welcome addition.

Lamb – Build a Fire (Jonathan Boulet Remix) by FBi Radio

? Sydney bedroom auteur Oliver Tank probably thought that no one would notice him lifting a lyric from Don McLean’s ?American Pie?, as long as he did it over a dreamy, post-coital dubstep backing. On ?Up All Night?, Tank does just this, asking, ?Can you teach me how to dance real slow?? as a barely-there bass drone descends in accompaniment. ?Up All Night? is taken from his triple j-approved debut EP Dreams, which is available on a pay-as-you-feel basis from Yes Please Records? Bandcamp page.

Oliver Tank – Up All NIght by Yes Please

?Melbourne’s The Hello Morning are back with a new single. Having secured a distribution deal with MGM, ?Stone Cold Lover? bears testament to the alt country-leaning act’s growing ambitions. It’s basically a wide-screen fist-pumper, with more than a hint of the Boss in the organ and in Steven Clifford’s delivery. After recording their debut in a rural cottage, their forthcoming, self-titled album was recorded with Jimi Maroudas at Sing Sing Studios, and is due for release in February 2012.

Stone Cold Lover by The Hello Morning

? ?Let Lead Rip/Gomorrah Fields? is the first offering from The Toot Toot Toots since their 2010 EP, Curse The Crow. The double A-side – if such a thing still exists – is taken from the band’s upcoming, as-yet unnamed ?spaghetti western rock opera, based on the Australian gold rush?. Implausible though it may sound, that description is pretty much spot on. With a smattering of horns and some truly hammy delivery, singers Danny Eucalyptus(!) and Giuliano Ferla tells a story of a shootout at a saloon, in which a grieving father knocks off 12 blokes, before carving his daughter’s name in their chests. Needless to say, it’s pretty full-on.

Let Lead Rip / Gommorah Fields by The Toot Toot Toots by showoffservices
