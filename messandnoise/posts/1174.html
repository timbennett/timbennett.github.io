Track By Track: East Brunswick All Girls Choir

By Mess+Noise 2014-04-28T14:00:00+00:00

From stupid drum fills to his parents? home address, MARCUS HOBBS unpacks the long-awaited first album from East Brunswick All Girls Choir – complete with guitar tabs.

?West Brunswick?

I wanted a really stupid drum fill to start the album; I think we got it. Josh Lobley] false-starts on the organ. [Negative Orgones.

It was written a while before recording. I was living in the depths of West Brunswick and had to get out for the Melbourne winter. Nothing new was happening; it was a sushi train with no new items. There’s only so much pickled ginger one can eat.

I think if you play this song at a different speed it would sound very much like some Bob Dylan thing.

C Dm G C->Am F G for those playing at home. G7 somewhere for good luck.

Seven Drummers by East Brunswick All Girls Choir

?Mon Repos (Charlie’s Jam)?

Topical hit about aeronautical adventures resulting in death. Tried to keep it to two chords, failed miserably and added a few more. When in doubt turn up Rob [Wrigley]?s guitar.

Am G for those playing at home. Some other chords in the middle.

Seven Drummers by East Brunswick All Girls Choir

?Dirty Bird?

Number about a friend or enemy who loves to hunt the ladies. Falls in love too easily. Maybe the ladies hunt him; I should have asked. Always best to assume though.

Capo 2 – Em to E for those playing at home. Am – G and some other stupid chord for the other part of the song.

Seven Drummers by East Brunswick All Girls Choir

?Golden Ninne?

Not far from the address of the track following this, there is a bluestone grave that housed a mother of two. I used to ride my bicycle around it and trod on top of where she lay. This may have then resulted in a few haunting experiences I can’t be bothered to go into detail about, but feel free to drop me a line and I’ll tell you some stories. Spooks all around.

Capo is on the 7th fret; Rob doesn’t use one though, so I’m not sure what he’s up to here. C to Em here and then some other chords in the singalong part for those playing at home.

Seven Drummers by East Brunswick All Girls Choir

?14 Clay Gully Court?

This is my parents? address: pay them a visit. They have a really nice outdoor entertaining area with a fireplace. Dad will weld anything you require and Mum will probably try and feed you.

Two chords, F to C, if you’re playing along at home.

Seven Drummers by East Brunswick All Girls Choir

?Darius?

Being away from home and meeting people from home. Tiresome activities. There’s no story here, just an idiot who learnt how to play a little bit of guitar and sang some words at the same time as the chords.

G Am C for those playing at home.

Seven Drummers by East Brunswick All Girls Choir

?Red Top?

There’s a guitar solo in this song – my guitar teacher would be rolling in his mud-brick shack in Elphinstone. Real-life grave.

A little number about living in Berlin. Did I tell you I lived in Berlin? I lived in Berlin for a while. I was in Berlin and saw things that you can only see in Berlin. Berlin.

Mostly a G, some kind of Dm and a C for those playing at home. Slide the Dm up a few frets when the time is right. You’ll know what I mean.

Seven Drummers by East Brunswick All Girls Choir

?Aeroflot?

I decided to gamble and fly with Aeroflot. I’d heard stories and seen some docos. I already hated flying so there wasn’t much point going for something luxurious. Turns out Aeroflot are alright.

I got a little scared when everyone clapped as the plane landed, soon to find out this is a normal habit. Alex Dal Santo told me the Italians are into this tradition also. Alex Dal Santo was going to put out this record but then decided he’d rather buy a house and get married. Real-life grave.

Start on C for those playing at home. An octave chord: move the first finger around and you’ve got action.
