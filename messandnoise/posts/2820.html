Off By Heart

By Doug Wallen 2013-10-07T00:00:00+00:00

Darren Sylvester is best known as a visual artist, particularly in the fields of photography, video and sculpture. His music is an extension on that, whether it’s his self-produced self-titled debut – made to accompany an exhibition of his – or Off By Heart* four years later. He plays every instrument and painstakingly cracks the precise sonic cues of his soft-rock heroes, from *Avalon*-era Roxy Music to Robert ?Simply Irresistible? Palmer. As echoed by the album cover, *Off By Heart is the classic rock-star fantasy rendered as an immaculate museum piece.

All canned thrum and overheated romance, these songs beat with a lustful heart through arteries of neon. With its posh bass pulse, glitzy keys, ticking drum machine and oh-so-sensual guitar licks, the opening ?Toni, How Did You Get So Cynical?? could be subbed in for the theme song of Silk Stalkings. Ditto the guitar licks of ?Not This Time?, every bit as steamy as a well-placed sax solo. ?Dream or Something Like That? lopes through the fevered night, while the vocals and lyrics of ?Salesgirl? are almost secondary to the slinky instrumental percolations.

Some will write this off immediately as kitsch or ironic, but it’s really proof of a fawning dedication. Sylvester takes his work – and his daggy influences – quite seriously. There’s by necessity some degree of tongue-in-cheek involved, as with his soft-rock brethren in Montero, Geoffrey O’Connor and Donny Ben’t, but it isn’t a joke. In fact, it’s not far removed from the fist-pumping, hyper-real portraits of youth found in TV Colours. Everything is heightened, from the triumph of most tracks to the acoustic-guitar melancholy of ?Hurts to Say?.

There are twinges of subversion, too. ?I rue the day to hell I didn’t give you head,? goes ?Fresh Face?, recalling the unexpected chemo line in the last album’s ?That’s a Nice Haircut?. ?I never thought a bad thing about you/Well, I almost did,? backtracks Sylvester at the start of ?If You Only Knew’s propulsive synth-pop, which showcases his hesitant falsetto and exaggerated croon. He can try his hardest to inhabit a super-specific bygone aesthetic, but something is always going to be slightly off. And rather than dodge that inevitability, he embraces it on certain tracks.

Darren Sylvester – Off By Heart by Darren Sylvester

Sometimes the songs? mechanics can distract from the actual songs; the scene-setting drum-machine rhythms are so appealing to me that I can find myself fixated on them at the expense of everything else.

Co-produced with Super Melody’s James Cecil, Off By Heart is an ?80s-cheese art project smeared in the Vaseline fog of nostalgia. ?Where is the trigger that makes you remember all of your life?? sings Sylvester on ?Boys Die Young?. This whole album is a trigger that makes us remember; remember a past we never actually experienced – and that never actually existed. Somehow, that makes it even more powerful.
