Damn Terran

By Tim Fitzpatrick 2013-12-22T00:00:00+00:00

Think about your Magic Dirt, your Tumbleweed or, even better, go to YouTube and type ?ABC Recovery? (or something along those lines). Dig through a Salvos store and a Tumbleweed album will jump out, if it’s somehow slipped through the cracks to make it there in the first place, largely because of the ?90s cartoon-psych cover. Damn Terran know this. They know the impact of the visual. Op-art posters, single/album covers and general band iconography seem to make out that they do, anyway.

But enough about imagery and peripheral junk. Does the music stand up? On songs like ?Provokasia? and ?Wrong Things?, definitely. There’s some pretty decent substance from the start of the album, with ?Provokasia’s echoed tremolo intro before the general tempo of the album kicks in with a stunted charge. Kinda Kyuss-ish, but brief. Good brief.

Damn Terran by Damn Terran

?Lost? comes in next with its isolationism: ?I am in a dark place, I am losing what is left of my mind, and I know that I’m leaving alone tonight, there I go.? Not so much angst-y or ambivalent, more uncomfortable, with the peak-refrain-peak dynamic. This kind of build-up definitely works on ?Simon’s Song?. Peak-refrain-peak, then charging off again with ?A Killer?. But then this brings us back to Recovery*. Blues Explosion’s appearance on the show is probably played on *Rage at least a dozen times a year, while YouTube is probably the only place you’ll see any evidence of 311 playing the show.

Damn Terran seem like they’re more on the Magic Dirt side of the Recovery spectrum. There’s longevity in the music for sure; maybe not so much in the lyrics right now, but the sounds stand out. ?Wrong Things? is probably the best example of this. A desert riff churning away and burning any ghosts of 311?s past. On ?Rebels? the churning speeds up then – ?Where have the rebels gone. Say they’re everywhere? You know you’re a liar, I can’t see them here.? The vocals are great though.

Damn Terran by Damn Terran

DZ Deathrays? Shane Parsons? appearance on ?Pills? midway through the album is the first time the refrain is dropped for a constant drive, which works for the most part. With the intro to ?Downtown?, it feels like the band could be running with this for a bit longer, but the ol? breakdown comes back into the equation and carries over to ?Burlesque Dancer?. ?Uncanny Valley’s Neu!-like beginnings are the first distinctive departure from the norm on the album. Constant cymbals keep it grounded in the band’s style, though, and tie it down with the album’s general theme.

Finally there’s ?In Your Dreams?. Recovery turns to static on the screen. Tumbleweed have reunited and whatever Damn Terran do next should be pretty interesting.
