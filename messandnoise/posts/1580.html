Arctic Blast

By Ben 2007-04-23T12:00:00+00:00

Arctic Blast is the collaboration of two respected Melbourne musicians: Ben Andrews (a.k.a. Blarke Bayer) and Robert McManus (a.k.a. Black Widow). When not scraping kitchen utensils, tapping cymbals, altering pianos and driving feedback through speaker cabinets – as they do on this experimental project – Andrews and McManus play in thoughtfully heavy bands: the former in My Disco and Agents of Abhorrence; the latter in Grey Daturas. Arctic Blast is the pair’s co-signed document of an afternoon in a Clifton Hill warehouse, charting three gratifyingly different approaches to long-form, improvisational music.

The grinding feedback hum of ?Maria of Puero Montt? – low, buzzing, distorted – is an electro-magnetic force at the track’s centre. Over fourteen minutes, other sonic elements tumble off this centrifuge, like sparks from an angle grinder. It links up the band careers of the two protagonists, capturing the heaviness, but pushing it through to a punishingly relentless point. The ten-minute ?Alone in Tierra Del Fuego? is a sparser track, yet the silences tremor just as ominously. It’s a dark composition. An out-of-tune piano plods portentously in the bass register – its notes, frantic clusters and deep single tones, linger and bloom like bruises. The piano’s upper reaches have the clanging gamelan quality of prepared piano.

?Trujillo’s Last Request? sees the two guitarists spar for seven minutes. It’s a melancholy and minor-key piece – less fraught than the other two, more resigned. It’s the most accessible thing here, but after the notable focus of the preceding tracks, it veers closer to the meanderings of post-rockers. Although misstepping into orthodoxy with ?Trujillo’s? – which is almost entirely redeemed by some wonderful closing moments of electrical pulses – this is a great first collaborative release from two inventive artists.
