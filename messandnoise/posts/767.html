New Noise: Wolf & Cub, Pond, Bearhug, Harpoons

By Darren Levin 2012-02-07T13:00:00+00:00

An all-killer, no-filler edition of New Noise featuring tracks by Wolf & Cub, Bearhug, Pond and Harpoons that will surely whet appetites for forthcoming full-length releases.

? After the red cordial buzz of 2008?s Chris Colonna-produced Science & Sorcery, Adelaide’s Wolf & Cub have noticeably taken it down a notch. Showcasing the dubby (?See The Light?) and the Krauty (?All Through The Night?) sides of the band, a new double A-side single precedes the band’s forthcoming third album, which was produced, recorded and mixed by Burke Reid at Sydney’s BJB Studios. Out now through iTunes and Bandcamp, the single will be launched in Adelaide, Melbourne and Sydney in March/April. Dates here.

See The Light // All Through The Night by wolfandcub

? Reportedly written in the midst of a Springsteen obsession (though we’re hearing more of summery US west coast vibe), Bearhug’s new single ?Angeline? is the first lifted from their forthcoming debut, Bill, Dance, Shiner*. Recorded with Tim Whitten, it’ll be out through Spunk on March 23. The album follows two EPs, *Cartoon Islands* and *To Anything, which features popular single ?Grapefruit?.

Bearhug – ‘Angeline’ by SpunkRecords

? ?Moth Wings? is more batshit crazy psych-rock from new Modular signings Pond, which features Tame Impala alumni Nick Allbrook and Jay Watson, along with Joseph Ryan. It’s the second single from soon-to-be-released fourth album Beard Wives Denim, following ?Fantastic Explosions Of Time?, which you can listen to here. The album was recorded over two weeks in a ramshackle farmhouse in Western Australia, with ?Moth Wings? reportedly breaking the band’s ?mid-recording cabin fever?.

Moth Wings by POND

? Bec Rigby puts in another star vocal turn in ?Walk Away? (not to be confused with Craig David’s ?Walking Away?), the new single from modern R&B pop group The Harpoons. Along with its excellent predecessor ?Keep You Around?), which [premiered on M+N* late last year, the song bodes well for the band’s Nick Huggins-produced debut (out this year through Two Bright Lakes/Remote Control). In a Citizenship Test interview with *M+N, Rigby described it as a ?a ball of barely sentient flesh clicking its tiny fingers to the Michael Jackson greatest hits its mum is listening to?.

The Harpoons – Walk Away by twobrightlakes
