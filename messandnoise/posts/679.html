New Noise: Francis Plagne, Amaya Laucirica, Plastic Palace Alice, The Hondas

By Darren Levin 2011-09-04T14:00:00+00:00

The next installment of our regular round-up of the best new music on the internet, featuring the long-awaited return of Francis Plagne and Plastic Palace Alice and a “protest song” from Little Red’s Taka Honda.

? There’s always been a baroque pop heart beating beneath Melbourne sonic innovator Francis Plagne, and it really comes to the fore on ?Spun Six Circles?. The track is the first taste of third album Tenth Volume Of Maps, which reportedly shifts away from Plagne’s early reliance on field recordings and tape splicing to a more ?focused live-in-the-studio feel?. It features contributions from the likes of Robbie Avenaim, Ned Collette, Anthony Pateras and Joe Talia, and will be out through Lost And Lonesome on October 7.

Francis Plagne – Spun Six Circles by Lost And Lonesome

? Last year’s Early Summer* may’ve been an ?album marked by understatement and restraint?, but there’s none of that on display on Amaya Laucirica’s fuzzy new single ?No Excuses?. It’s one of two unreleased songs from new EP *Anywhere There’s You*, which comes bundled with two live versions of tracks from *Early Summer. Laucirica is one of 80 artists performing at this year’s BIGSOUND Live. She’s also touring in October. Dates here.

No Excuses by showoffservices

? ?My Johnny?, the first single from Melodie Nelson’s debut album Meditations On the Sun, has been given the remix treatment once again. Following Collarbones? ?indistinguishable? take on the track, which was partly written in the car, comes a sprightly electronic spruce up courtesy of Alex Nosek (Hammocks and Honey, ii, Oblako Lodka). The remix comes bundled with new single ?Orpheus, She’s Gone?. Melodie Nelson (aka Lia Tsamoglou, one half of now defunct Sydney duo Moonmilk) will perform this Saturday (September 10) at the Grace Darling in Melbourne.

My Johnny (Mt Mutombo’s LD re-mix) by Melodie Nelson

? ‘Heart Weighs A Tonne’ marks the return for Melbourne pop collective Plastic Palace Alice, who certainly haven’t lost their penchant for Arcade Fire-like melodrama in the intervening years. (Look out for the double-time twist at 1.55.) The song comes from new album Like A Light*, which will be launched at Phoenix Public House in Melbourne on October November 5. It’s the follow-up to 2008?s *The Great Depression.

Heart Weighs A Tonne – Plastic Palace Alice by showoffservices

? Little Red’s Taka Honda is not gay, but he has a song called ?I Am A Homosexual?, is partial to cross-dressing and reportedly doesn’t ?get laid that much?. Go figure. The debut single from The Hondas is a protest song about accepting difference within society. It’ll appear on new album From Tokyo to Melbourne Town, which will be out in early 2012 through fledgling indie Small Empire Records. ?I Am A Homosexual? will be launched on September 23 at The Grace Darling Hotel.

I Am A Homosexual by The Hondas
