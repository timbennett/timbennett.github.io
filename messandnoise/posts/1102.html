Track By Track: The Ancients

By Mess+Noise 2013-11-25T13:00:00+00:00

Ancients leader JONATHAN MICHELL looks to Todd Rundgren, Christopher Cross and Kermit the Frog for guidance on the band’s third album, ?Night Bus?.

?Chaos and Futility?

This is a jaunty song with some psychedelic touches. The lyrics are downbeat but the music is optimistic. This much you can tell from listening to it. I feel that chaos and futility are in their ascendancy in the 21st century. To try and resist being engulfed by either will be a great struggle for the majority of people. But you can’t stop trying to enjoy and improve your life.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?Green Face?

Green Face is a character from the story Journey to the West*, on which the TV show *Monkey) was based. I found this out after I had named the song and had written the lyrics. This didn’t stop me from trying to imbue the song post-hoc with greater significance than I had dreamt it had while making it. I don’t know why but I feel like this song could be popular in Taiwan.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?She Lives in a Tent?

There was a time not so far back when gangs of shorthaired boys roamed the suburbs of the north looking to get their share of what was going. They knew that life was just a gamble so they lived from day to day without a care. Across the river there were boys with longer hair. They were treacherous and mean. Their braggart’s boasts rang out in the night. Everyone was strutting around. It was a bloody strange time.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?Hey Now?

One day last year we were in the band clubhouse, which is situated at the back of the Darebin tip. It’s where we jam and hang out, and we also use it as a base for a private investigation business that we run on the side to supplement our music income. I had this catchy riff I wanted to play but the other guys were sifting through surveillance photos and scrunched-up pieces of paper and were ignoring me. A rich lady’s prized St Bernard dog had gone missing and she had hired us to get it back. Anyway, they wouldn’t listen and I lost my temper and I yelled out, ?Hey guys!? ?Guys hey!? ?Hey now guys!? Julian [Patterson, drummer] said to Georgie [Ward, bassist/singer]: ?Hey now what’s that sound?? They laughed but kept ignoring me. Only later did Julian realise when I played the completed song to him that he had given me the title and the chorus all in one shot. None of that is true.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?Molokai?

Todd Rundgren has written some of my favourite songs ever. He lives on the Hawaiian island of Kauai in a house he acquired after Meatloaf bought him out of future royalties from the album Bat Out of Hell, which he produced, played on and funded. Don’t hold that against him! Anyway, I thought he lived on Molokai, another Hawaiian Island. ?Molokai? was partially a tribute to Todd.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?Night Bus?

I was on an overnight bus journey through a strange land and behind me an aggressive idiot was conversationally victimising a reasonable stranger. My non-enclosed headphones weren’t loud enough to drown out the fool’s attempts to insinuate himself into the stranger’s life and a stalemate played out painfully over what seemed like hours. Why can’t people just be quiet? For most of the trip some low-level classic hits had been playing. Christopher Cross’s ?Ride Like the Wind? came on and seemed to shut this guy up and change the mood generally. What a song.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?Hamster?

Georgie wrote this song. The danger of being the most prolific songwriter in a band is that when occasionally one of the others throws one in, it will be better and more popular than all of yours and you’ll feel less grand than you might have. It remains to be seen whether that will be the case with this song, but it is certainly worthy I think.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?House of Cards?

Musically I wanted this song to find a place somewhere between ?Rainbow Connection? by Kermit the Frog and ?Junk? by Paul McCartney. The lyrics were partially inspired by Les Murray the poet, who was on Radio National one day. I’m wary of poets but I listened to him talk about pastures and Presbyterians for a while and remembered I had a copy of Fredy Neptune that someone had dumped outside the Northcote Police Station – I’m guessing it hadn’t belonged to a police officer. So I skimmed it and found a line I liked, which was paraphrased into the opening line posing the question ?how to be human.?

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?Bad Weather Report?

Julian, who plays drums in The Ancients, is one of my favourite guitarists. In Mum Smokes and Minimum Chips he did some pretty amazing chord changes and melodies. Pat Ridgewell from Small World Experience is another favourite and this song represents, musically at least, an attempt to write something like those guys might.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients

?Jenny Street?

The title of this song was a placeholder indie-song name that became the real thing. I’m trying my guts out here to pull off the falsetto and I like the guitar solos. Not enough songs start with a guitar solo and then repeat the same solo later in the song. C’mon guys, clearly this needs to happen more.

The Ancients – Night Bus by The Ancients
