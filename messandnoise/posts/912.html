A Dead Forest Index: Europe Tour Diary Pt 2

By Mess+Noise 2012-12-05T13:00:00+00:00

As SAM* and *ADAM SHERRY of A Dead Forest Index wrap their jaunt with Heirs, they snooze through an organ recital and embrace England.


After our short interlude in Berlin, without finding a harmonium and driving through some cordoned-off streets in the middle of a bomb scare and hearing a bomb sound (where the instrument shop would be) – after THAT we settled to roam around Kruetzberg. The traveling by that point and lack of sleep had taken effect, so we resigned to the bunks early to rest, in preparation for Prague the next day.

So we drove to Praha. Brent, Adam and I got there some time before the troupe and found this beer hall with ?the world’s strongest beer.? After this very wine-y beer interlude, we bought tickets to an organ recital and made haste to get there, back over the Charles Bridge of sculptures (including one to bring luck – Saint Jan Nepomuk).


Organ recital: the liquor had taken effect by the first drone. Whether it was this or the lighting or the heavy ornamentation, we slept well for the duration, except for Adam, who was steadfast. The next night, onwards to play the K4 club with the LA band Ides of Gemini. Again, just over the Charles Bridge, in a complex positioned very near and underground to the Estates Theatre. We saw Anna Chromy’s sculpture ‘II Commandatore’ in memory of Mozart’s Don Giovanni, which premiered at this opera house in 1787.

Then to Vienna, Dresden and Hamburg, where we played our last show with Soror Dolorosa before Denovali’s Swingfest in Essen. Hamburg was very interesting for us; really stood out amidst our travels. We stayed directly above the St Pauli Football club’s headquarter pub, a famed anti-fascist, anarchist club [with] skull and crossbones emblem. In the midst of after-show and end-of-tour celebrations and – after much unrecognisable vodka – I realised I had to stumble away from a St Pauli lock-in.


Denovali Swingfest is a yearly festival held by our label and was an honour to be part of. It was the perfect way to end the tour. We played, followed by Heirs, then A Winged Victory for the Sullen. All very memorable. After a month on the road with Heirs, we parted ways as Adam and I went onwards to London and Bristol for a short tour with some friends we hadn’t seen for years. It had been four years since I left for Melbourne and five years for Adam. We revisited many places and old walks, centred around the East and the Thames. One thing I’ve really missed greatly is just drifting and scouring the Thames banks for archaic bones, ceramics and porcelain when the tide’s out.

Jack Brennan’s new project Ectopia create improvised vocal, cello and electronic works with a current inspiration of horror film soundtracks. Dynamic spoken word rises and falls set against drum machine and the patterns of cello. Martin Aloysius Brignall is a phenomenal instrumentalist, playing very delicate pianistic guitar lines, but with a very old folk nature and beautiful yet sometimes coarse voice. We ended our tour at the Cube Cinema in Bristol, with the help of Lina B Frank, curator and performer at Ausform. An amazing venue.
