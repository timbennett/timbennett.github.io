New Noise: Regurgitator, Sailor Days, Donny Ben?t, Hit The Jackpot

By Darren Levin 2011-06-23T06:00:00+00:00

Our regular round-up of the best new music on the internet, including Regurgitator, Donny Benet and the new outfit for Biddy Connor (Kes Band, Laura Jean).

? ?One day we’re all going to die? is the cheery opening line of Regurgitator’s new single ?One Day?. While the band had initially planned to disseminate tracks as they ?bobbed to the surface?, we’re pleased to learn the song is part of an actual album called Superhappyfuntimefriends*. Recorded in Ben and Quan’s respective Melbourne dwellings and put together in the midst of an enforced creative blizzard, it’ll be released in time for the band’s August tour with New Zealand electro-pop act Disasteradio in support. As an aside, Regurgitator’s *Unit was recently voted #13 on triple j’s Hottest 100 Australian Albums industry poll, topping the personal lists of Muscles and Art vs Science.

ONE DAY (free download from http://www.facebook.com/regurgitators and bandcamp)” by Regurgitrax

? Violinist to the stars Biddy Connor (Kes Band, Laura Jean, Magic Silver White) has finally started putting out some compositions of her own. New outfit Sailor Days features Connor on viola, voice and organ, with Laura Jean bandmates Jen Sholakis (drums and bells) and Laura Jean Englert (bass). To whet appetites for a self-titled debut, Sailor Days have a new EP out featuring first single ‘Species Counterpoint’, along with remixes by Magic Silver White, Super Melody and Alarm Birds. Recorded and mixed by Joe Talia (Ned Collette + Wirewalker), Sailor Days be released physically on biddotcon records and digitally through Gaga Digi on June 24. It’ll be launched on Saturday (June 23) at Bella Union at Trades Hall in Melbourne.

Sailor Days: Species Counterpoint by -gaga

? Like Biddy Connor, bassist-for-hire Donny Ben’t (Jack Ladder and the Dreamlanders) has decided to forge his own path, inking a deal with Rice Is Nice (Seekae, Straight Arrows, Seja) and preparing for the July 8 release of his self-titled solo debut. A frenzy of Moog and slap bass, the title track is influenced by motivational speaker Anthony Robbins, and was reportedly penned while killing time at a Las Vegas Greyhound bus terminal. The result: motivational music you can dance to. Did we mention his dad is a famous Italian disco accordionist? Or maybe it’s just a ruse?

Donny Ben’t – Don’t Hold Back – Donny Ben’t by Rice Is Nice

? Holiday*, the new EP from Adelaide’s Hit the Jackpot, is a bit of a landmark release for Chapter Music, being the first cassette the iconic indie has released since 1996. The follow-up to 2008?s *Soul Money Gang Vibe (also released on Chapter), the EP was recorded at Jessica Thomas and Kynan Lawlor’s Adelaide home studio, with contributions from prodigal member Sebastien Calabretto, who now lives in the UK; new drummer Stephanie Crase (Batrider, No Through Road); and multi-instrumentalist Scott O’Hara, who’s moved to Melbourne. The EP will be launched in Melbourne at the Workers Club on July 8 and back home in Adelaide at the Metro on July 9.
