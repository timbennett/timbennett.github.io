Track By Track: Terminal Sound System

By Mess+Noise 2013-10-01T14:00:00+00:00

?A Sun Spinning Backwards? is the epic 11th album as Terminal Sound System from Victorian musician SKYE KLEIN. It spans doom, dub, jazz, post-rock and drum ?n? bass, with themes steeped in bleak sci-fi and even bleaker reality.

?Deep Black Ash?

Prologue: a great big dirty wave of electromagnetic fallout. It’s a fragment from a corrupted transmission snatched out of the air, contextless, a sign of things to come or maybe a remnant flying outward at near light speed, partially decoded.

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System

?Oceans?

Opening to imaginary film. Big sci-fi, big doom riff. You’ve got to open with a big song, right? A friend thought I was mad opening with the previous track, but whatever. This track basically sums up the entire album, both in theme and production. Hope, foreboding, aspiration, despair. Partially inspired by a weird field recording I found online, apparently some kind of construction noises in a housing estate but sounded like the sounds of impending doom/monster attack from Cloverfield.

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System

?Clearlight?

Changes brought about by manipulations of the body. Chemical, maybe psychological. Definitely physical. Either way the end result is the same. Building a better human? Normalising the genome? I’m not sure. Not sure either whether it was intentional or indirect side effects. I’m leaning towards the latter, in this story arc. Named after an imaginary nanotech/psychotropic course of ?treatment.?

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System

?Theme for Scorched Earth?

Probably the most literal TSS song I’ve ever written. The title says it all, really – it’s part one of a two-part lament for a broken planet. The ?earth? in the title should actually be lowercase, not necessarily (but not necessarily not) referring to our Earth.

I was tinkering on the piano and, quite uncommonly for me, came up with something that resembled an actual song for piano. A perfect invitation to write a song about runaway nanotechnology and planetary infertility.

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System

?To the Sun?

One of the bigger influences on the past few TSS albums has been the ?good half? of the film Sunshine. The scene of the omnipotent, all-destroying pure sun rising over the horizon of the spacecraft while the dude is stuck outside has merged into my own personal mythology/theology/paganistic archetype lookup tables.

This song is kind of about that, but also all of them kind of are. It’s about rushing forward, actually plummeting, without worry or care or any other intent but to move. The second part is my Bladerunner moment, one of my favourite bits on the album.

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System

?Silver Ships?

The earth is well and truly fucked. Preparations are made, children are loaded into massive bulk transports and sent into space. They will be cared for by machines. We have only an incomplete idea where they are headed, and no time to complete the research. We don’t know if they will make it. Certainly the first generation won’t, but their children might, or their children’s children.

All we have are probabilities now, and anyway nobody wants to think too hard about all of that, and it’s not like we have any other options. This song actually makes me quite sad, but I think it’s my favourite on the album.

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System

?Suns We’ve Killed?

The chillingly sinking feeling that, as we look for safe places to retreat to, they have all already been rendered null. Still, we push onward and outward. I think the riff at the end is possibly the best bass tone I’ve ever recorded.

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System

?A Perfectly Reflecting Sphere?

The second part of the lament begun by ?Theme for Scorched Earth?. I had a very clear mental image of this track before I even began it, a view from space of a planet with 100% cloud cover, similar to visualisations of Venus. Albedo 1, which means all sunlight is reflected back into space. You can think about the implications of that.

A couple of my filter boxes were malfunctioning, and from there comes the buzzy broken-signal drones.

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System

?What Will Come?

This song is for my daughter. The title is both a question and a statement. I have such mixed feelings about the future. I try to maintain hope; people can do some pretty amazing things when they put their minds to it. Probably equal to the amount of harm they can do when they don’t.

I get to splurge most of my favourite TSS indulgences in one song – fast jazzy/jungle drums, gnarly analogue bass, melancholy piano, metal. The second part was written in my old house in the middle of summer on some horrible fucking 40+ day. The wall-size window in that room soaked up heat and radiated it back at like 300% intensity, or so it seemed.

A Sun Spinning Backwards by Terminal Sound System
