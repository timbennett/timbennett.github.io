Track: ‘Birds’

By lawsonfletcher 2009-07-01T00:00:00+00:00

If you really want to cement a band’s canonisation, then it makes sense to do what new label Feraltone did when assembling Brand Neu!, a compilation saluting the influence of seminal Krautrock duo Neu! and released a few months ago overseas. The tribute album standard of inviting contemporary bands to revamp classic cuts was jettisoned in favour of curating a collection of songs that sound like they were influenced by Neu! ?Dare not reinvent your idols,? Feraltone seemed to be saying to today’s musicians, ?for we will reveal your indebtedness to them!? Here, the history of music plays in reverse, it is the reconstruction of influence rather than the progression of departure, and it smacks of conservatism.

One wonders how Neu! themselves might feel about this decision, seeing as their project (embodied in their very moniker) from the beginning, when they emerged in the ?70s, was to deconstruct rock cliche and forge a new direction. They did this by mixing a uniquely German outlook with remade psych motifs and other sonic ephemera, privileging driving metronomic beats and drones with subtle textural changes over the traditional verse-chorus-verse. Neu! are a band to be remembered for their ideas as much as their sound, but Brand Neu! seems to reduce ?influence? to little more than a set of stylistic signifiers, with the perilous risk of turning into a trite and – even worse – nostalgic, sonic pastiche.

Enter Dappled Cities: a band well rehearsed in covers, versions and remixes, who are among a handful of Australians, including Wolf & Cub and Decoder Ring, invited to contribute to the local digital release of Brand Neu! They’ve chosen to reinterpret their otherwise swift and effusive live favourite ?Birds? by lashing it with typical Krautrock signifiers: a more deadpan motorik beat, glitchy keyboard scribbles, lethargic echo-vox, all stretched to a super-indulgent eight minutes.

Unlike the majority of international acts featured, it’s a plus that Dappled Cities recorded this version specifically for the compilation, which also signals that it all might be a tad more self-conscious in its homage. Yet one wonders if the original was already sufficiently Neu!-influenced in its metronomic percussion and elfish vocal experimentation, and I’m at a loss as to what this all amounts to: an interesting tribute, a trite mongrel of their own song and another’s style, or even a vaguely offensive aberration that reduces the idea of Neu! to a few sonic stereotypes?

Somehow, though, as it peels through rise and fall, you realise that ?Birds? has fidelity and infidelity in happily equal measure. Maybe it’s because Dappled Cities seem to have followed Neu!?s more overarching sonic project: to craft something indescribably atmospheric from elements that otherwise read like a recipe for monotony, while still retaining the originating pulse and passion of their own song. In the end, the track is a well-realised excursion in finding beauty in the harsh and abstract. However, it’s bloody difficult to shake the feeling that it’s all a little too tongue-in-cheek.

As this once-revolutionary duo are marshalled into rock myth – an almost obligatory name to drop for a growing number of bands of all stripes – perhaps being true to the spirit, rather than the letter, of Neu! would be the greatest possible tribute. Honouring their boundary-breaking experimentation and dedication to sounding fucking alien might involve daring to venture as far away as possible from the blueprint. As it stands, Dappled Cities manage a mildly engaging appropriation that is awkwardly neither here nor there.
