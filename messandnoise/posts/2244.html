Chrome Dome

By Shaun 2010-04-01T00:00:00+00:00

This album is as cheap as a Dean Koontz paperback: cringingly addictive while it lasts, but forgettable once its 17 miserly minutes are up. Melbourne’s Chrome Dome emerged last year with their debut single ?Negative Vibes?, a tastefully imbecilic release that married the current mood of underground ?punk? – disenfranchisement, boredom, self-loathing – with synth melodies and beats you could dance to.

But a formula that can barely withstand 17 minutes is a dubious one, and while not without some charms, this LP is mostly just annoying. Worst still is that it sounds uncommitted: every available trope is executed to make this sound dark, but it never actually is. Shaun South’s lyrics unflinchingly plunder stock footage from the goth punk canon: people get killed, bitches break hearts, and a general sense of depraved gutter ennui prevails. The songs sound unloved, like spontaneous ideas cast immediately in stone. South and fellow vocalist Andrea Blake impart reverberated horror set pieces (?he’s dead in the woods, there’s blood on my hands?) over thick buzzsaw synth lines, with what sounds like a drum machine accompanied by a live drummer (Bryce Sweatman). You really need to not be concentrating for this to sound good.

Because instrumentally, this record is fun. The dull mock-despondent vocals however, are horribly awkward. They sound like the first-person narration from a bad horror movie. ?CMA? is the one unconditionally lovable song on the record: the pace settles, and Blake’s otherwise shouted vocals relax into a drugged and minimal coo, an unsettlingly pretty sound redolent of HTRK. It shows that Chrome Dome isn’t a bad band per se (their ?Negative Vibes? single is great) but one that would benefit from fleshing out their ideas. And applying more distortion to the vocals, maybe.
