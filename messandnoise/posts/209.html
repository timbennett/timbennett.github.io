West End Riot

By A.H. 2009-03-24T13:00:00+00:00

A.H. CAYLEY* reports from day two of the My Filthy Riot festival at the Annandale Hotel in Sydney’s inner-west last Sunday. Photos by *JEZ HEYWOOD.


What a great little festival. Walking through the side alleyway between the Annandale and next door’s ever-abandoned lot, under a cardboard sign and gold tinsel, I see a ?Dale I’ve never known: sofa beds, comfy chairs and old leather recliners sit against all the outside walls. At the end of this, a barbecue is set up, advertising a $4 sausage sizzle (a bit pricey but it hit the spot later in the day). I never knew that wall was painted yellow. I’ve never seen it in the daylight. The early arrivals make themselves at home on the comfiest seats, lounging in the midday sun while the first band, Dusty Duets, soundchecks. It seems black is the new black for these carefully nonchalant identities. I feel garish and showy in my high-necked red dress. At least I’m smoking the right brand of tobacco. I convince myself that I really am cool enough to be here, and walk inside.

The whole day feels as relaxed as those first punters. The three rooms across the venue – even the pokies lounge – host mostly Australian artists (the exception being New Zealand’s Popolice*), and for the most part, local Sydney bands such as **Sherlock’s Daughter**, **The Laurels** and the frankly amazing *Aleesha Dibbs. The punters wander through the rooms as they please, catching a bit of what they want between cigarettes, face-painting and Pub Cha (the Dale’s take on yum cha) out the back. It’s a wonderful vibe, even if our photographer had issues with the effect the rarely-changing lights – blue in one room, orange in the other – will have on the overall appearance of his work. The fragile little flower.

I find myself questioning if this will be a financial success – it seems everyone here is either a band member, a plus-one or a media guest – but it certainly is a success in all other aspects. I would love to see something like My Filthy Riot happen more frequently at the Annandale and in Sydney in general. Like-minded music fans spending a day in a great live venue, experiencing an independent local line-up in a fun and relaxed atmosphere.

A lot of effort has obviously gone into creating a great day – and it certainly paid off.
