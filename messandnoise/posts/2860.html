Regional Curse

By Freya Zaknich 2014-01-11T00:00:00+00:00

Regional Curse is but one of Stacey Wilson’s guises, having also generated work in solo projects Rites Wild and Comfort Zones and her most recent band, Terrible Truths. The recordings collected under Regional Curse make it the longest-running facet of Wilson’s output, beginning in 2008 while she was still operating in Adelaide.

In comparison to the aforementioned, Regional Curse appears to be the darker performance vehicle. Wilson shows a predilection for naming songs with a sinister/sardonic dualism, and I find myself tracing through her entire discography to arrange them in a significant way, with little ease or conclusions able to be gained. Unease, then, is the order of business.

?Queer Scum? opens the album, with two notes that glisten and stretch for some minutes before minimal percussion gives slight momentum to the piece. When it appears the vocal line reverberates, precise and direct in its articulation. And yet the whisper-voice is buried by a mash of frequencies, and purposefully unintelligible. This, in coordination with a reeling synth progression, decorate a vivid, paranoid walk through I know not what.

Without words or insight, to try and tack meaning onto ?Conversion Therapy? would be ridiculous. I find some similarities between it and ?Simple Detachment?, in that there are equal parts drone and groove set to heavy paces and punctuated by metallic and rhythmic snaps and clangs. Much of Regional Curse is evocative of a surreal film soundtrack. The tension is exacerbated tenfold by way of dragging tempo and thickened layers of sound, producing enough weight to broil the mind in a state of heightened sensitivity.

?Sensual Full Time? is the longest track of the album, and with length comes a full demonstration of the dynamics available in the cyclical growth of Wilson’s work. For the main part it’s all cold oscillation before a beautifully distinct maneuver about the keyboard bleeds through, then receding back into a low growl.

Regional Curse sees a tightening of technique in Wilson’s dedication to dense, dank atmospherics. Her recording and production acumen are revealed in the creation of generously spacious planes that are often lost in less finely finished electronic schemes. Overall, the effect produced is one of rich tonal swathes dressing stark, unknown landscapes. The songs unfurl, enveloping and drowning all they contain.

Regional Curse by FORMAT Records
