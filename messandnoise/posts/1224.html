Track by Track: Tralala Blip

By Mess+Noise 2014-07-22T14:00:00+00:00

Mixed-ability NSW ensemble have created a rich and cosmic new album in ?Aussie Dream?. Band members MATHEW DAYMOND*, **LYDIAN DUNBAR**, **RANDOLF REIMANN** and *ZAC MIFSUD weigh in on its six tracks.

?Soccer in Space?

Mathew Daymond: Two things that I like are soccer and space. I also like my PS4 but that’s another song. I started writing a song just about soccer but it was kind of boring, so I thought what it might be like to play soccer in outer space, maybe on a space station with the heavenly lights of stars and planets all around. I imagined the ball moving slowly through space and so we made the music a bit slow-motion.

AUSSIE DREAM by Tralala Blip

?I Wanna Leave Home?

Lydian Dunbar: I wrote this song about two years ago. It is about living in Bangalow with my mum and sister. When they had fights I wanted to move out. In the song I say I wanna live in Brisbane with my new girlfriend. I love Brisbane because Tralala Blip has friends there and there are good bands like Scraps and Primitive Motion. Scraps sings a song about Devo and I love Devo. My sister lives in London now and I am still in Bangalow.

AUSSIE DREAM by Tralala Blip

?If You Love Me I’ll Love You Back?

Zac Mifsud: This song is about taking my girlfriend to the movies. I can’t remember much about the movie because I was looking at my girlfriend most of the time. In this song I use the Frostwave SpaceBeam. It is my favourite instrument. I think it (SpaceBeam) was made in Melbourne.

AUSSIE DREAM by Tralala Blip

?In My Natural Way?

Randolf Reimann: The lyrics to this song were written by Leanne Butt. Leanne has many volumes of poetry written about the Australian bush and the creatures that inhabit it. ?In My Natural Way? was written after one of Leanne’s morning constitutionals.

AUSSIE DREAM by Tralala Blip

?Dog Boy Soldier?

Mathew Daymond: ?Dog Boy Soldier? was inspired by an illustration I saw at Lismore Regional Gallery. Tralala Blip were to perform at the gallery and I thought I would write some lyrics about the art that was being exhibited there at the same time as our performance. We used an MFB drum computer, MFB Microzwerg and a vocoder to make the song.

AUSSIE DREAM by Tralala Blip

?The Sweetest Girl?

Lydian Dunbar: I wrote this song about a girl named Jess. I was in love with her but she just wanted to be friends. But I could not just be her friend because I loved her and it hurt. So I don’t see Jess anymore.

AUSSIE DREAM by Tralala Blip

+
