Adrian Deutsch: Out Of The Red

By Darren Levin 2009-11-11T13:00:00+00:00

With his defiantly unfashionable solo album ‘Help You!’, ex-Red Rider Adrian Deutsch exhibits a world where it’s hip to be square, writes DARREN LEVIN.


After six years chasing the zeitgeist with Sydney’s Red Riders, Adrian Deutsch has embraced his inner-dag. The 26-year-old’s debut solo album Help You! was created in a self-imposed bubble, where major-seventh chords are celebrated, three-part harmonies are the norm and it’s OK to like Todd Rundgren and Steely Dan.

?I wasn’t listening to much modern music, definitely not anything fashionable,? the Canberra-born singer says from Sydney, his home for the past eight years. ?If all you do is listen to the hottest band right now, by default you’re going to make runner-up music. I just focused on living in my own bubble and not really worrying about anything outside.?

Of course, it wasn’t always like this. Emerging full of promise from the same fertile scene that produced The Vines, Red Riders – the band Deutsch formed with Alex Grigg while living out the ?art-school cliche? in 2003 – never really scaled the heights of their Ivy League peers. They’ve been persistently dogged by an identity crisis, and you can hear it on their debut Replica Replica, an awkward album that stammered into the world in 2006 to muted fanfare and acclaim.

?It was quite easy initially. It was youthful enthusiasm and anything we wanted to try we’d do it,? says Deutsch of the band’s beginnings. ?But then there came a point when we started second guessing ourselves about what might or might not sell, or what would be popular. That’s when things changed.?

It was only when the band went into the studio to record what would later become Drown In Colour – their second LP – that Deutsch knew he had to leave. The album was eventually released in July this year with Brad Heald from The Vines replacing Deutsch on guitar.

?Obviously I was moving to a different songwriting direction. I was bringing songs to the band, they weren’t digging it and that would create all sorts of tension. We’d all just get frustrated with each other which is pretty unhealthy because that’s the whole point of being in a band and playing music together.?

?If all you do is listen to the hottest band right now, by default you’re going to make runner-up music. I just focused on living in my own bubble and not really worrying about anything outside.?

Help You! was co-produced by Philadelphia Grand Jury’s Berkfinger at BJB Studios in Sydney with a cast of players that included workmate Leighton Arnold on bass, Adam Foreman (ex-Sparkadia) on piano, Robin Dixon on lap steel and Paul Andrews from Sydney pop stalwarts Lazy Susan on drums. Deutsch met Andrews at a mutual friend’s party, where the pair shared a mutual admiration for The Band’s singing sticksman Levon Helm. ?I’m still trying to get him to sing and play at the same time,? Deutsch laughs.

Despite its pop flourishes and occasionally syrupy sentiments – ?Her light was bold/It shot to another planet/That’s why you had to have it,? on the unashamedly MOR-ish ?Her Light? – Help You!* also showcases a more serious side. ?The Partisan?, for example, is dually inspired by Primo Levi’s *If Not Now, When?, a novel that details the Jewish resistance’s fight against the Nazis, and the legacy of Deutsch’s Holocaust survivor grandparents. ?It’s definitely something that’s always meant a lot to me, but it’s only recently that I’ve been able to articulate that,? he says.

Stylistically, the album is illustrative of the wide gulf that opened up between Deutsch and former cohort Grigg. A nod to classic ?70s pop, Help You!* is confident, mature and anything but self-conscious – there’s no way you could pull off those Wes Montgomery octaves on the title track if you cared about what people think. On the other hand, *Drown In Colour, with its modern affectations and wistful Smiths-isms, still sees Grigg shooting for the stars.

?If you look at it [Help You!] on paper, it’s not a model for modern commercial success,? jokes Deutsch. ?Then again, it’s still a pop record and pop music wants to be liked. Maybe it might attract an older crowd.?

So an ARIA nomination for Best Adult Contemporary Album, a dubious category that runs the gamut from The Panics to crooner David Campbell, isn’t something to be baulked at then?

?Frankly, I’d consider that a compliment,? Deutsch admits. ?Actually, adult contemporary? I don’t even know what that means. Pop music for a more mature audience? If that’s what it is, sign me up baby.?

+

Adrian Deutsch will launch Help You! at Oxford Art Factory tonight (November 12) with Canvas Kites and on November 14 at The Front Bar in Canberra with Paul Andrews (Lazy Susan).
