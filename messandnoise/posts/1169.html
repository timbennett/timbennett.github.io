New Noise: Lace Curtain, Die! Die! Die!, Atolls, Shards

By Mess+Noise 2014-04-14T14:00:00+00:00

It’s all about pop of the synth-, noise- and slacker- variety in this week’s roundup of new music.

? Fresh off news of a second Total Control album, that band’s dance-centric sibling Lace Curtain has debuted a video for ?I Can’t Wait?, a track from their third EP. Which, incidentally, is called The 3rd* EP. It came out in late February on digital and 1000-run vinyl in silkscreened jackets via Mexican Summer. ?I Can’t Wait? is a surprisingly straight synth-pop number that’s not such a far cry from *Zonoscope-era Cut Copy and actually sidles up nicely alongside Total Control’s debonair coldwave curveball ?Flesh War?. Also taken from the four-song EP, the six-minute ?Lovers Fade? is a total unabashed ballad that eschews the anxious, club-wrought momentum of the first and second EPs. Three EPs in the space of a year is no small feat considering how many other projects Mikey Young and company are involved in. And hey, put them all together and you’ve got yourself a Lace Curtain album.

? Dunedin trio Die! Die! Die! haven’t even released their fifth album yet – or even firmed up a release date – but there are already two singles and a whole mess of touring. The band were just here in Australia last month, and now they’re finishing a national NZ tour before touring Europe next month. Following on from the dreamy yet corroded ?Crystal?, the new tune ?Sister? shows off the band’s longstanding knack for combining the noisy, poppy, punky and shoegaze-y all in one cohesive thought. That fifth album, entitled S W I M, is due out sometime later in the year.

? Atolls have been cranking out ?90s-laced indie rock anthems for a while now, culminating last year in the Hair Machine EP. A follow-up EP hits this Thursday (April 17) as a free download via Bandcamp, and it’s heralded by the joint singles ?Love Forlorn? and ?Jump In?. The former swaps in some Pavement-y bits for the band’s past Dinosaur Jr. worship, while the latter is a crunchy yet melodic duet with Big Scary’s Jo Syme. Compared to leader Lucas Skinner’s work with King Gizzard, this isn’t so much druggy overdrive as post-grunge sweetness. The EP launches on release day (April 17) at Shebeen with The Demon Parade and the coyly named Local Group. It’s $10 from 8pm, with more info here.

? Melbourne band Shards do the slacker-pop thing well on their self-titled debut album, which is up on Bandcamp for any price. But then they spike it with fuzzed-out guitar runs and throw in enough sneaky Krautrock and shoegaze cues to keep it from straight dolewave. You’re more likely to hear traces of Doug Martsch or Stephen Malkmus than Dick Diver or Twerps, in other words. Recorded in a Coburg home studio, the album already has a video for opener ?Play? and coincides with a few low-key local gigs in the coming weeks.
