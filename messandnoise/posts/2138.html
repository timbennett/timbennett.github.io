Freemason?s Hall/Sunbathing in Squalor

By timscottt 2009-11-01T00:00:00+00:00

Alongside Kitchen’s Floor’s debut record Loneliness is a Dirty Mattress, this 7? from three Perth malefactors prove that it doesn’t require a whole lot of musical knowledge or chops to release a quality record in 2009. Taco Leg are not pretending to be anything they’re not. They would be the first to admit to have done some plundering of most of the Kill Rock Stars and K Records back catalogue and probably play drunker than they should. In fact, they’re most probably drunk right now.

Released on Baltimore label Fan Death, who have put out records by the likes of Clockcleaner, Drunkdriver and Pygmy Shrews, the single is a good introduction to the band’s shambolic lo-fi sound. A-side ?Freemason’s Hall? – a re-recording of a track that appeared on their now sold-out Spider in the Mouth cassette tape – is a song about, you guessed it, a now demolished Freemason’s hall in Perth. Only this time around it takes on a slightly more aggressive swagger. Like Matt Kennedy from Kitchen’s Floor, Andrew Murray’s vocals shift easily from a slur to a shout. They’re both a defence and a celebration of inane suburban boredom.

The slightly poppier (though not my by much) ?Sunbathing in Squalor?, about the heat of Perth in summer, was recorded on a 40-degree day in drummer Richard’s bedroom. When Murray shouts, ?Stuck to the bed/Stuck in my bed?, over loose tom beats and a broken-down guitar line you can almost smell the perspiration and warm beer.

Taco Leg will draw haters, for sure. This is loose, primal punk rock that some will consider nothing more than barked vocals over noisy semi-tuned guitars. For those people, there’s always the Philly Js. For others, who like things a bit more backward and demented, there’s Taco Leg.
