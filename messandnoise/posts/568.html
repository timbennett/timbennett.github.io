Graney On The Hottest 100: ?Be Amazed At My Ignorance!

By Darren Levin 2011-01-26T13:00:00+00:00

In his first column of 2011, DAVE GRANEY picks out 13 songs from triple j’s Hottest 100 that he actually has an opinion about.


Dr Mess+Noise asked me to cast a lazy (?) or perhaps jaundiced (?) or perhaps angry (?) eye over this list. I don’t really seek any conflict and at first declined but then thought, why not?

Firstly let me say that it seems like a great idea to have a top 100, and it’s good that there’s such a thing in a world where music, and any interest in it, is soundly mocked at any turn. Mocked and trivialized by both young and old, might I add. Look at the presentation of music on television (the little there is) and say it ain’t so.

Also, this list is new music released in the calendar year, and compare that to any other commercial stations miserly commitment to contemporary music. Shame. (Actually, the occasional discussion about file sharing and stealing of online music, where it’s argued that it allows people to hear more music, is made quite redundant when you bring up the silly idea that that’s what radio is supposed to do. Like so many things in this needlessly crude world, it’s a given that commercial radio is totally made up of hits of the 20th century.)

“I must admit I never tune into this broadcast. It’s a bit of an event I hear, but it’s on Australia Day and I make it a point to ignore most events on that date.”

Now, to this list. I must admit I never tune into this broadcast. It’s a bit of an event I hear, but it’s on Australia Day and I make it a point to ignore most events on that date. Also, I live in Melbourne and we don’t get the JAYS here.(I love to use that term – it’s so daggy). By the term ?we? I am being recklessly inclusive, feel free to opt out of my random hug. Un-friend me – please! Also, the term ?get? has a couple of meanings. Something may be available but you still refuse to ?get? it. Dig?

Anyway, I’m no critic and I wanna show you here how dumb I am but also how a fellow like me, who is older but kind of contemporary, might engage with this constructed list, this portal, this window into some kind of a cultured room with many ceilings. I actually seek out and hear a lot of contemporary music. I guess I would have avoided a lot of this anyway. I find Australian music to be the most exotic and interesting. I am commenting on the ones I have something to say about. The others, I am pretty clueless about to tell you the truth. I probably will remain so. I also have my precious and private prejudices, which I enjoy intensely. Be amazed at my ignorance!

Triple J’s 2010 Hottest 100

1. Angus & Julia Stone – Big Jet Plane.
Congratulations!I’ll hear Angus and Julia someday. It’s amazing in this world how niched everything is and how things can be so popular but so controlled in their frequencies. Why aren’t they on TV?

2. Little Red – Rock It
I heard this when it came out and it sounded very lacking in dynamics to me. six months later they were performing it on morning TV. It sounded quite good. It’s great when a sound makes it through the business somehow. Past all the fecking ?experts?. What does anybody know?

3. Ou Est Le Swimming Pool – Dance The Way I Feel
4. Birds Of Tokyo – Plans
I have never heard this band but the drummer is an act called The Sons of Rico, who I absolutely love. Their album Reactions* is superb. Along with Black Brown Green Grey White from Kes Trio, *Jane Dust* by Jane Dust and The Ancients 2 by The Ancients, it’s my album of the year!
