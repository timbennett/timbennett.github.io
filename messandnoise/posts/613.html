New Noise: Panel Of Judges, YesYou, Phia, The Singing Skies

By Darren Levin 2011-05-08T14:00:00+00:00

A regular new column, where we pillage Soundcloud for the best new music on the interwebs so you don’t have to.

? A new double A-side from Melbourne noise-pop institution Panel of Judges, whose fourth album Moods On The Move* is due July 22 on Mistletone (vinyl only with a download code). ?Coconut Trees? is a driving pop number with some wonderfully off-key sax, while the more downbeat ?Dainty Vagabond? features some fuzzy blasts from new guitarist Martin Frawley (The Twerps). Panel of Judges recorded the new album at Head Gap Studios with Jack Farley. It follows 2008?s *Bad Vibrations, which we described as ?their most fully-realised [release] to date?.

Panel of Judges – Coconut Trees by Mistletone

Panel of Judges – Dainty Vagabond by Mistletone

? YesYou are a couple producers from Brisbane who you’ll no doubt be hearing a lot more of, probably on triple j. The pair have been drafting in a range of different collaborators for their debut ?eclectic dance-pop record?, including The Boat People’s James O’Brien and the St Margaret’s Anglican Girls’ School Choir, who urge you to ?raise your glasses? on new single ?Half Of It?.

Half Of It by YesYou

? Imagine Eddy Current’s ?Which Way To Go? performed by Sally Seltmann and you’ve got ?Indecision?, a sprightly pop number from Garden Of Youth, the debut EP from Melbourne musician Phia (aka Sophia Exiner). Exiner, a classically-trained jazz pianist, describes herself as ?an analytical sort of girl?, while kitchen utensils, saucepans and triangles clatter and crash around her. Phia launches the EP in both solo and band mode at Melbourne’s Grace Darling this Friday (May 13).

Indecision by showoffservices

? The Singing Skies is the songwriting project for Sydney musician Kell Derrig-Hall, who’s collaborated with Jack Ladder, Rand and Holland and Seaworthy, but is probably best known as one half of Moonmilk with long-term partner Lia Tsamoglou. On debut album Routine and War*, he trades that act’s experimental impulses for plaintive vocals and acoustic guitar. ?Acting Fine? is its poignant first cut with lilting harmonies by Tsamoglou and strings by Biddy Connor, (Laura Jean, Grand Salvo). *Routine and War was recorded by Simon Grounds (Underground Lovers, Grand Salvo) in Melbourne. It’ll be out through Preservation on June 10.
