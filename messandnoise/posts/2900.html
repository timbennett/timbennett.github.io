Spent The Afternoon

By timscottt 2014-05-08T00:00:00+00:00

Nathan Burgess sings in a high, nasally voice. He also has a tendency to sometimes sing out of tune. But his high, nasally and out-of-tune singing seems perfectly suited for the sweet, loping pop of Full Ugly.

As the title suggests, their debut album is ideal listening for a relaxed weekend afternoon, whether at home making pumpkin soup or out strolling through the local golf course. Tracks like ?Nervous? and ?Oh Daddy? – with their bouncy lead guitar and Burgess? laconic, almost confessional delivery – have a homely warmth. Monday morning is still a ways away.

What started as a side project for Burgess and his then Love Connection bandmate Michael Caterer (now in New York playing in Scott & Charlene’s Wedding and Shorts) has developed into an all-star cast that includes Thomas Mendelovits from Milk Teddy, Zach Schneider (Great Outdoors, Totally Mild) and Nathan McFarlane filling in for Caterer.

Recorded by Jack Farley back in in 2011, prior to Caterer’s move to New York, the album is only now being released but brims with poppy observations of living in Melbourne as a mid-twentysomething. First single ?Drove Down? tells of Burgess working a dead-end job at a big hotel and looking for love. He drives down the highway late at night on his way to find something new. A real simple narrative but a real pretty song.

On ?Hilly Street?, a modern coming-of-age tale, he sings of a childhood where ?the neighbors were friends? then climbing ?the cold ladder of growing up.? He now sports a sore shoulder from working hard but croons, ?If I be myself I will last?. This last phrase is a good representation of Burgess and Spent the Afternoon. Despite some limitations with delivery, honest songwriting shines brightly throughout the album.
