New Noise: Total Giovanni, Schlager Music, The Bonettes, Sewers

By Mess+Noise 2014-11-11T13:00:00+00:00

From dance romantics and girl groups to Krautrock and grotty punk, this week’s roundup of new music has it all.

? Melbourne’s retro dance romantics Total Giovanni are on a roll. The word-of-mouth attraction of their live shows precedes them, and they’re bringing their sweaty magnetism to Golden Plains next year. They’ve also just linked up with local hit factory Two Bright Lakes for the debut single ?Human Animal?, which has racked up more than 4,000 plays in under two weeks. The tune unabashedly looks up to Talking Heads, from the talky David Byrne-style vocals to the squiggly synth and funk guitar. There’s whiffs of pure novelty about it, but it’s well-constructed enough to make us take these guys seriously. Catch them this Friday (Nov 14) along with Architecture in Helsinki and Rainbow Connection DJs as Melbourne Music Week takes over the Vic Markets. Tickets here.

? Schlager Music crank out synth-driven Krautrock that sounds cobwebbed over from decades neglected in storage. They’re only doing a token run of 10 hand-cut copies of their self-titled debut 7?, but you can grab both sides digitally for just a dollar total. The Melbourne trio enlist the genre-blind Orlando Furious to sing on ?Schlager F’nf?, while the instrumental B-side ?Stump Juice? lopes alongside the buzzing afterburn of a chugging guitar. The 7? was recorded and mixed by Damian Clarkson (Autoportraits), who played additional guitar on both songs, and mastered by Mikey Young.

ST/EP 7″ by Schlager Music

? Touted as a ?band built on beehive wigs, custom dressmaking, killer choreography and hits,? The Bonettes do indeed look to the glory days of classic girl groups for just about every aspect of their sound and look – including the black-and-white video for opener ?I’ll Be Lovin? You?. The vocal team-up of three Sydney singers, with a backing band that includes members of Sounds Like Sunset, The Handlebars, El Mopa and Kid Cornered, The Bonettes cut their self-titled five-song EP with Belles Will Ring’s Liam Judson, who’s produced the likes of Cloud Control and Caitlin Park. It’s taken the ladies six years to make their proper recorded debut, but it’s pretty well worth the wait for the excitable, horn-and-reverb-gifted pop goodness of this EP. If it’s easy to pick the precise influences from track to track, that’s just part of the fun. The Bonettes launch the EP as a live nine-piece ensemble this Saturday (Nov 15) and next Sunday (Nov 23) at The Imperial Hotel’s Cabaret Room.

The Bonettes by The Bonettes

? M+N scribe Patrick Emery described Sewers? debut album last year as ?dark, dirty and not all nice?. That description fits just as well for ?Chain of Command?, the grotty, punky A-side for the Brisbane band’s new 7?, which comes out this Friday (Nov 14) through Tenth Court. Recorded by the band’s own Josh Watson, who also did the above cover art, it’s raspy and rumbling but packs a lot of gnashing guitar melody too. And finishing at 2:21, it’s over too soon. But don’t listen to us; here’s what Matt Kennedy (Kitchen’s Floor, Eternal Soundcheck) had to say about the 7?: ?Only an infuriatingly indifferent place like Brisbane could give rise to these disillusioned guitar-slingin? goon sackers, and the sonic nihilism on display here is a glorious call to arms for the disaffected.?
