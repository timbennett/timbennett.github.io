New From NZ: Winebox Inquiry, Fazerdaze, Mongo Skato, The Canals

By Mess+Noise 2014-12-01T13:00:00+00:00

This month’s New Zealand roundup spans throwback techno, boisterous noise-pop, sparkling jangle and open-hearted lyrics.

? While we wait for a third album from one of our favourite Kiwi bands – Terror of the Deep – drummer William Daymond has stepped out with the solo venture The Winebox Enquiry. Debuting back in July with a free-download seven-song EP, the home-recorded project luxuriates in Daymond’s breathy vocals and sparkling, ever-present guitar jangle for results that are less Flying Nun-esque (although, to be fair, Daymond played in the Flying Nun band The Axemen) and more like classic Byrds or Chad & Jeremy. That said, the budget keyboard, drum machine and distorted guitar on the first half of ?Depends Who You Know/Reminds Me of You? happily evokes Tall Dwarfs. Another standout is the ballad ?I Am a Clich??, steeped in relatable humour. But really, it’s all very good indeed.

The Winebox Inquiry by The Winebox Inquiry

? Billed simply as ?girl in bedroom on guitar,? Fazerdaze is the self-produced solo work of Amelia Murray, who’s backed live by a bassist and drummer. Her self-titled six-song EP is at once snappy and dreamy, anchoring her diaphanous voice with perky guitar melodies on tracks like ?Reel? and ?Zero?. ?Treading Lightly? and ?Tired of Waiting? are darker and noisier, yet there’s a lovely sun-baked daze that persists throughout the EP and really comes into its own on ?Jennifer?. Murray’s open-hearted lyrics match the warmly open space in these songs, making for a quietly rewarding debut. The EP is available digitally through that first link, or on CD here.

Fazerdaze EP by Fazerdaze

? The 1080p hit factory continues to crank out brain-busting electronic releases, and the influential tape label turns its attention to New Zealand with the eight-song debut album I Don’t Give It* from Wellington’s Mongo Skato. Following 2013?s *Granulated EP, the 100-run cassette deals in glazed, wobbly, wafting throwback techno suited to blissed-out dancefloor reverie. Grab two of the tracks – ?Fela? and ?Turismoc? – free below.

? Dunedin multi-purpose space The Attic isn’t just great for bands looking to practice, record and sometimes play to a live audience. It has also documented lots of rising NZ bands via 2012?s It Came From the Attic compilation and a subsequent singles series that pairs one original with a B-side cover song chosen by The Attic. (The covers so far have spanned everyone from Solange and Earl Sweatshirt to St Vincent and David Lynch.) Number nine in the free-download series comes via noise-pop quartet The Canals, whose A-side ?Desperado? is an attention-grabbing, effects-smudged anthem that splits the difference between countrymen Popstrangers and Die! Die! Die!. The flipside, meanwhile, is a version of Youth Lagoon’s ?Cannons? that turns the sighing, melancholy original into an outlet for boisterous release.
