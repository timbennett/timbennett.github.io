New Noise: Cull, The Citradels, Gil Michell, t:dy t:wns

By Mess+Noise 2013-09-18T14:00:00+00:00

It’s an especially dreamy roundup this week, from spacious shoegaze and droning psych to submerged beats and noisy pop.

? The rare kind of song that stops you in your tracks, Cull?s ?World Inside Your Head? sticks its groggy chorus right at the front and wanders through sighing shoegaze vistas from there. You can hear the band’s professed Deerhunter influence, as well as an unabashed affection for My Bloody Valentine. The Sydney quartet features singer-guitarist Chumpy Ly (Polar Knights), guitarist-keyboardist Alex Watts (Chance Waters), bassist-singer Fraser Harvey (Cameras) and drummer Julian Cerreto (Lion Island), and have previously yielded worthy tunes like ?Good People Disappear?. But ?World Inside Your Head? – mixed and recorded by Ly with guest vocals by Alex Cameron from Cogel – feels like a marked evolutionary step. The spacious gem is the first single from next month’s b? n’i EP, self-produced partly in a ?studio? built under a loft bed. Also check out the (/tv/4614850) for ?World Inside Your Head?, and keep your eyes peeled for gigs.

? Geelong-via-Melbourne five-piece The Citradels embrace the slow and droning, raga-damaged side of psych-rock on their second album, Our Lord’s Secret Service. Self-recorded over three months, the album came out at the end of August through Psyche Ward [sic]. It launches this Saturday (Sept 21) at The Public Bar with Local Group, The Grand Rapids and DJ Kezbot. It’s $8 at the door from 8:30pm.

Our Lord’s Secret Service by The Citradels

? Add Wooshie and Galapagoose and what do you get? Gil Michell, the team-up of rising Melbourne beatmakers Dylan Michel and Trent Gill. Despite its title, their new album Whimsical Raps is mostly instrumental, described as ?a long-form exercise in meditative arrangement and rhythmic deconstruction that] focuses on themes of disparity and social indifference.? It’s up for [pre-order on vinyl and CD before its October 1 release through This Thing, with the 12? limited to just 200. Mastered by Mikey Young, the album evokes what was briefly called illbient for a while there, yet is generally hard to pin down in genre terms. It’s pulsing, absorbing and beautifully submerged, revealing generous depths of melody and noise. Delve into ?No Friends?.

? As previously explained, t:dy t:wns is the synth-pop project of Peter Joseph Head and a revolving cast of vocalists. (It’s like Melbourne’s answer to The 6ths). Where the debut 7? featured Evelyn Ida Morris (Pikelet) and Sez Wilks (Kikuyu), the noisier new offerings ?Bask? and ?I’m Gonna Get Dressed Up? hinge on Eva Popov (Hello Satellites) and St. Clare, respectively. It’s the second double A-side single from November’s debut album Sister City*, which also enlists Prudence Rees-Lee, Louise Terry (Flying Scribble), Irit Rozenfeld (Oblako Lodka) and Elizabeth Mitchell (Totally Mild) and sits opposite a planned all-male album called *Brotherhood. You can buy the new single digitally here. It launches Saturday, September 28 at The Spotted Mallard with Hello Satellites, Milk Teddy and The Motifs. It’s $12 from 8:30pm. The live version of the band pairs Head (Actor Buddhists) with Neil Erenstr’m (The Crayon Fields, The Motifs), Zachery Schneider (Great Outdoors), Terry and Wilks. And yes, some mind-blowing jumpers.
