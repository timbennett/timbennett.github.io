New Noise: EATW, Liam Finn, Pajama Club, Emma Russack

By Darren Levin 2011-05-16T05:00:00+00:00

A regular column, where we pillage Soundcloud for the best new music on the interwebs so you don’t have to.

? Sounding like a long lost Pet Sounds* outtake, ?Too Soon? is the first new music to emerge from Eagle and The Worm since a couple well-received singles surfaced last year. The Melbourne eight-piece, who were voted ?Best New-ish Act? in the 2010 Readers Poll, will release their debut album *GoodTimes through ?uber cool ?70s label? Cotillion on June 3. ?Too Young? will be available in 7? format from shows, as well as digitally via iTunes on May 20. As with the rest of the album, it was cut across four days in 2010 in a single room in North Fitzroy with Steven Schram (Little Red, Little Birdy).

Too Young by Eagle and the Worm

? Liam Finn?s forthcoming new album FOMO* promises (once again) to be a mixed bag. Following vaguely Drones-ish first cut ?The Struggle?, new single ?Cold Feet? is described as the most ?assured and concise pop moment of the new album – a prelude to what can only be described as his most accomplished work to date?. It’s also as close as he’s ever sounded to his famous dad Neil – and we mean that in a good, non-Julian Lennon way. Recorded in New Zealand’s Round Head Studios with Burke Reid (The Drones, Jack Ladder), *FOMO* is the follow-up to 2007?s critically acclaimed, *I’ll Be Lightning. It’ll be out through Liberation in June.

Liam Finn – Cold Feet (Radio Edit) by LiberationMusic

? More Finn family fun, this time from dad Neil (Crowded House) and mum Sharon, whose new outfit Pajama Club started out as a post-dinner jam with a couple bottles of wine. They’ve since drafted in Auckland singer-songwriter and musician Sean Donnelly on keyboards and guitar and ex-Grates member Alana Skyring, who we erroneously thought had quit cooking for a career in the culinary arts, on drums. ?From A Friend To A Friend? is the first taste of a full-length album, due out in August. Pajama Club will be touring the east-coast in June. Dates here.

Pajama Club – From A Friend To A Friend by Mess+Noise

? Following the divisive ?Cheesecake?, comes a poignant bit of home-recorded balladry from Melbourne singer Emma Russack (formerly Lola Flash). Recorded with the help of Jake Phillips and collaborator Alec Marshall (guitar), ?Friends Not Lovers? will be re-recorded when Russack heads into the studio to lay down tracks for a debut LP. It’ll feature a rhythm section of drummer Cameron Potts (Ninetynine, Cuba Is Japan) and bassist Tim Picone (Sir), as well as various guest musicians. Russack continues a month-long residency at The Empress in Melbourne this Saturday (May 21).

Emma Russack – Friends Not Lovers by Why Don’t You Believe Me?
