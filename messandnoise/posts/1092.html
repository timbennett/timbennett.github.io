New Noise: Premium Fantasy, Jack Lee, Phia, Food Court

By Mess+Noise 2013-11-06T13:00:00+00:00

Easy listening, surf garage, loop-pop and more comprise this week’s roundup of new music.

? Premium Fantasy is the blissed-out work of Melbourne’s James Payne and Zoe Hunting. They pen woozy sermons to synths and drum machines that are made even more gorgeous – as on ?Melody (of the Spheres)? – by Hunting’s wistful, floaty vocals. Their Active/Passive EP comes out today, including that song, while a tape version includes a 12-minute alternate version of ?Passive/Active?. Recorded over three weeks at Horiscopic Sound, it’s been compared to ?a somewhat impoverished Tangerine Dream gig in an industrial complex in the northern suburbs.? The ?easy listening? EP launches tonight (Nov 7) at The Gasometer with a full plate of support from D.D Dumbo, Snowy Nasdaq, Yes/No/Maybe, Pink Health and O Littleblood DJs. It’s $10 from 8pm.

Melody (of the spheres) by Premium Fantasy

? With songs about the endangered Chiko Roll and noise complaints sparked by sharehouses, Jack Lee?s 11-song album Feline Fine is another solid entry in the camp of jangly bedroom-pop with a distinctly Australian edge. It’s out now through Osborne Again on either tape or name-your-price download, and features drums throughout by that label’s owner Lachlan Denton (also of The Ocean Party). Lee’s songs are short, ramshackle and affable but unfortunately no launch gigs are planned, as he’s headed overseas at the moment.

Jack Lee – Feline Fine by Osborne Again

? Berlin-based Sophia Exiner chimes in with looped and layered pop on ?Do You Ever??, her latest single as Phia. Produced by fellow Berlin expat Mez Medallion, the song leans nicely on a kalimba melody – her adopted instrument of choice, despite being a classically trained pianist – and evokes a more mainstream answer to The Blow’s Paper Television. Having already racked up 17,000 plays on Soundcloud, it foreshadows her in-the-works debut album, also being co-helmed with Mez Medallion. In other news, Exiner has been named a recipient of the inaugural Musicboard Berlin Scholarships – alongside Pantha Du Prince and Easter – with the funds going towards said album.

? Last seen playing edible instruments and making quite a mess of it, Sydney beach-garage quartet Food Court are about to launch their grunge-loving six-song debut EP Smile at Your Shoes, recorded in Melbourne and mixed by Mikey Young. You can grab the perky title track right now for free.

Fri, Nov 8 – Captain Cook Hotel, Sydney, NSW [w/Bad//Dreems]
Thurs, Nov 14 – Beach Road Hotel, Bondi, NSW [w/The Murlocs]
Thurs, Nov 21 – Marlborough Bar, Sydney, NSW [w/Pretend Eye]
Sat, Dec 7 – Grace Darling, Melbourne, VIC [w/Ross De Chene Hurricanes]
