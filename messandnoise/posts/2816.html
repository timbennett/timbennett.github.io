Dirty Pop Fantasy

By Doug Wallen 2013-09-29T00:00:00+00:00

If you’re one to put much stock in band names, Regurgitator have an incredibly apt one at the moment. While it might have once sparked images of a costumed metal band a la GWAR, it now suits the long-trucking duo of Quan Yeomans and Ben Ely down the ground. They regurgitate, happily and omnivorously, everything they consume on this eighth album. And everything they consume includes radio R&B, hardcore, ?80s synth-pop, quirky folk and comic rap.

As with 2011?s SuperHappyFunTimesFriends*, pastiche isn’t an insult here. It’s the brief. Self-produced in Melbourne and in Yeomans? adopted Hong Kong, *Dirty Pop Fantasy veers between extremes with the same frazzled attention span of its 31-second, channel-surfing opener, ?Brain Brain?. It’s full of two- and three-minute songs, one-minute interludes and a great deal of weirdness. And, this being Regurgitator, a sure-fire arsenal of instant choruses and jokey lyrics. It’s Regurgitator both more pop and more meta than ever.

Dirty Pop Fantasy by regurgitator

How you take all of that depends largely on your fondness for bubblegum immediacy as well as constant elbow-nudging. For mine, it’s a lot of subversive fun, blasting by with shaken-soda fizziness before you properly catch the oddest touches. Some stretches are played close to straight, like the early rockers ?Sine Wave? and ?Made to Break? (albeit about shoddy manufacturing, echoed in the travails of love) as well as the closing Magnetic Fields homage ?Fantasyland?, though even that’s got a singing minotaur. Actually, the grunts and ?Let’s go? of ?Made to Break? show the thin line between rock-star parody and idolisation.

But for all the brooding, throwback pop of certain songs – including ?Mountains? and ?Home Alone Stoned? – there’s a rush of disparate ideas elsewhere, spanning Ely’s minute-long hardcore valentine ?So Tuff?, the bass-driven pop-punk of ?My Little Terrorist?, the heart-on-sleeve yet self-aware acoustic ballad ?Answering Machine?, the throbbing synth-strumental ?Hong Kong?, the Prince-loving title track, the biker-movie stoner-rock of ?Bongzilla? and those quickie interludes.

Dirty Pop Fantasy by regurgitator

Tapping failed love with sugary metaphors involving brand-name lollies and sour tarts, ?Fuck You Sweetness? sounds jovial but says it all in the refrain: ?I guess all milk curdles, baby, and all sugar ferments.? The super-catchy ?We Love You!? has a parade of Yeomans cracks, from ?We’ll be the doves in your purple rain? to ?We’ll be the rapper in the middle-eight padding of your R&B ballad.?

The far-flung impulses combine most dramatically in ?Can’t Stop?, which starts with a homemade chart-pop aesthetic before moving into the usual cartoony lyrics and a rap section that cites the requisite montages in ?80s movies. Whether or not this turns out to be the last Regurgitator album – they’re going on hiatus after touring it so that Yeomans can embark on fatherhood – they’ve given us one more high-five-worthy ?80s-movie montage in Dirty Pop Fantasy. There’s even a sax solo.
