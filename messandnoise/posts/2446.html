Homo

By timscottt 2011-04-01T00:00:00+00:00

Though they have considerably more competition then in 2007 when they released their first cassette of primitive thud, Homo proves that the UV Race have few equals when it comes to mixing punk with just the right amount of hooks, controlled chaos and sense of fun.

Released in the US on In the Red (The Dirtbombs, Thee Oh Sees) and in Australia by Aarght! (as a limited-edition vinyl with hand-crafted sleeves), Homo* – the band’s second LP and sixth studio release – is a slight departure from their eponymous debut. It’s still a gritty punk rock record, but whereas their debut was inspired by The Velvet Underground and garage, *Homo* sees the band takes on a more explorative approach: There’s ?70s rock and glam, buzzy pop and the closing seven-minute title track, which morphs into a psychedelic freak-out reminiscent of the final minutes of The Stooges? *Funhouse.

The record kicks off with one of the strongest tracks, ‘Girl In My Head’, a stomping but melodic hip-shaker backed by a buzzy keyboard. ?Hey Marcus do you remember what happened?? guitarist Al Montfort asks vocalist Marcus Rechsteiner during what’s essentially a song about going to a show, getting lucky and waking up the next day with a girl in your bed who asks ?for a bit of toast?. It’s also damn infectious.

Working again with Eddy Current Suppression Ring guitarist Mikey Young at the recording and mixing stages, many of the songs are seemingly more focused than earlier efforts. Saxophonist/harmonica player Georgia Rose takes on more vocal duties on ‘Low’, ‘Lost My Way’ and the tender ‘Always Late’, which tempers the more manic side of Rechsteiner.

While he’s no Gareth Liddiard or Dan Kelly, on ‘Inner North’, Rechsteiner has created one the funniest stream-of-conscious vocal treatise of life in Melbourne’s inner-north. Backed by a solid drum beat and sax line, he basically maps the streets of the area and references landmarks including Edinburgh Gardens, The Tote and Tabet’s Bakery on Sydney Road. It’s a punk-rock Google Maps.
