New Noise: t:dy t:wns, Devotional, Drunk Hands, Bowl Cut

By Mess+Noise 2013-05-15T14:00:00+00:00

This week’s roundup of Soundcloud offerings swing from unpolished solo musings to an atmospheric three-piece and a unique citywide collective.

? ?What do you do if you’ve got a song but you haven’t got a voice, or you’ve got a voice but you haven’t got a song?? asks Peter Joseph Head in the presser for his synth-pop collective t:dy t:wns. In his case, he teamed up with vocalists Evelyn Ida Morris (Pikelet) and Sez Wilks (Kikuyu) for a double-A-side 7?, and with many more Melbourne artists for November’s debut LP Sister City*. Among them: Prudence Rees-Lee, Louise Terry (Flying Scribble), Irit Rozenfeld (Oblako Lodka), Eva Popov (Hello Satellites) and Elizabeth Mitchell (Totally Mild). All female collaborators, yes, but with an all-male album called *Brotherhood to follow. In two other unusual choices, Head sourced lyrics for ?Dear Joseph? from Penelope Chai of Clothing for Correspondence – in exchange for a scarf, naturally – and the 7? will be sold in hand-knitted bags made by artist Akiko Yamasaki. The 7? is coming out through Knock Yr Socks Off/Airpunch, with a launch at The Grace Darling on Saturday, June 15. Morris and Wilks will perform live on both sides of the single as well as play support sets alongside DJs Matsu and Hunch.

t:dy t:wns double A side single (Featuring Evelyn Ida Morris & Sez Wilks) by t:dy t:wns

? Sydney trio Devotional have a new limited 7?, with the cover art by Amanda Meares carrying on a theme from last year’s Southern Skies EP. The pairing of the uncharacteristically upbeat ?My Baby Loves Me All the Time? and ?Paradise? is also up for free download alongside the vinyl pre-order. There’s even an appropriately atmospheric video for ?Paradise?. The single launches this Sunday (May 19) from 4 to 8pm at the free weekly series Sunset People at Hollywood Hotel in Surry Hills, with support from Lo Carmen and Quaoub.

My Baby Loves Me All The Time by devotionaldevotional

? Duncan Blachford may be fully occupied with various projects – Exhaustion, Snawklor, Actual Holes, Cross Brothers – as well as the label Endless Melt, but he’s still found time for his long-running outlet Drunk Hands. Out May 27 on said label, Suite for Piano is a three-part album recorded on an open-access piano at Melbourne’s Preston Market late one night, with percussion cut the following night at a studio/shed ?on the industrial outskirts of Reservoir.? Incidental sounds make their way into both recording sessions, from passing trains and creaking structures to electrocuted mosquitoes and humming refrigerators. Below, hear a seven-minute excerpt from the melancholy and meandering ?Security?.

Drunk Hands – Suite for Piano by Endless Melt

? Is Bowl Cut best summarised as a ?bedroom boredom project from a 17-year-old Australian fucker?? Maybe so; it certainly foreshadows the one-minute blasts of fuzz-punk on Curtis Judge’s half of a March split EP with kindred spirits Lunatics on Pogosticks. The free-download Sexist Texas features the below ?She’ll be Right? and the Thermals-ish ?London Bridge?, each a quickie bludgeoning of junkyard catchiness in the vein of his 2012 Kylie cover.
