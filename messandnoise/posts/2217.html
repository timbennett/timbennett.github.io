Humans and Luxury

By Shaun 2010-03-01T00:00:00+00:00

According to their Wikipedia page at the time of writing, during their live show this Melbourne-via-Tasmania duo loose forth scents ?from a seven inch metal coffin? with a view to having the audience identify smells that, as they claim, have caused ?utter repulsion? and at times ?complete and utter asphyxiation?. The evidently self-written wiki also informs that Go Genre Everything harbour a dinosaur obsession that bleeds subliminally into their music and artwork. These arbitrarily concocted shenanigans are representative of the way this duo has presented themselves over the years, to the point where their reputation, here at least, totally eclipses the recorded output.

Judging from this EP their records are similarly ridiculous. The four songs here aren’t unlistenable per se: if you like music that desperately latches to a lyrical gimmick and runs with it for three odd minutes (way too long) then you’ll savour this. Furthermore, if you have an infant child then ?Humans and Luxury? could easily facilitate a bonding session. In this incarnation at least, GGE is a riff-domineered hard rock group with an aspiring glam-rock vocalist singing stiffly through the nose: it’s vaguely catchy, and songs such as ?I Am The Best (and No-One is better a-hahahaha)? have a sort of adolescent menace, but it’s neither funny nor as clever as their extra-musical mythmaking confusedly wants you to believe.

It’s frustrating, because often they tap into an edge-of-sleep surrealism – a druggy, dreary discordance – that exhibits a potential shattered by the (anti)pretentious stupidity of their lyrical mantras, carrying on like another forgettable Mike Patton project. If these were the dreamt-up observations of some newly sentient being and that was their shtick (as ?Humans Likes Collecting Things That Match? hints at) then this could be fun. But while apparently un-cynical and playful, Humans and Luxury is adamantly unloveable: a joke either too elaborate or too banal to keenly invest in.
