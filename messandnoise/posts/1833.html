Paper Planes

By David 2008-10-01T00:00:00+00:00

After about my ninth listen to the Paper Planes? album I started to feel uneasy about how I should review it. There seemed to be very little I could say of a negative nature about this extraordinarily textured, accomplished and sumptuous rock album.

If anything, this debut reminds me of Jimmy Webb Sings Jimmy Webb*, a hilarious, corny genius album of songwriting demos that Webb himself has disowned, ridiculously, as it’s about the best thing he’s done. In truth, *Paper Planes* has no single quality in common with *JWSJW except that it’s songwriting of the highest order, crafted and played with aplomb. Also, they’re both the kind of records that if I’d made either of them I would say, ?Enough. That’s it. I can’t improve on this. Burn my pianoforte or other songwriting tool.? (But that’s me, of course, and I wouldn’t advocate it for PP or JW).

In essence, the band is a vehicle for the high-quality songwriting talents of Claire Birchall (very high quality). I was going to say that ?I Just Don’t Understand? sounds too much like Bruce Springsteen’s ?Fire? just to say something a bit negative for texture. Until, of course, I realised it’s the album’s only cover.

If you had three minutes left to reaffirm your faith in great rock music, you would turn to ?Do It For the Kids?, which Paper Planes also released as a single last year. But everything is superb, with special kudos to ?Over From The Ground Up? and ?The Train Song?.

Words fail me. Perfect.
