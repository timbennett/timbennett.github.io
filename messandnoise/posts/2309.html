Wherever The Wind Blows

By Shaun 2010-07-01T00:00:00+00:00

Nobody seems to ask what’s happening in Canberra, but they should from now on. Wherever the Wind Blows is a debut of sorts for Canberra’s Jonny Telafone, though a number of his digital-only releases popped up on the Dream Damage web label last year. Those past recordings spoke of an artist unsure whether to pursue, on the one hand, the type of motley retro-futuristic pop music so fashionable at the moment (see fellow Canberra resident Horse MacGyver), or a sparse folk pop replete with canned percussion, misty eyes and a broken heart.

As if to be contrary, this cassette sees Jonny steer emphatically towards the latter. The tenor of Wherever the Wind Blows is of a static melancholy; of hours spent staring at a wall and catching fleeting sentiments at their most potent before beating them into songlike shapes. This is bare-essentials pop music, with means wielded only towards some cathartic end. There’s a looseness that feels agreeably spontaneous, which is crucial for a songwriter as thus far undeveloped as Jonny Telafone.

The simplicity of these songs will frustrate many, and there’s rarely a slowly resolving melody here you haven’t heard many times elsewhere. The songs are not eventful: they dutifully appear in the same uniform, repeat themselves and then disappear, usually very abruptly. Compared to his Canberra contemporaries this is unexciting music, bereft of the shock and awe of Assassins 88 or TV Colours, but let Jonny Telafone become an acquaintance and you’ll find something in common with him. ?It’s Only Ever You? describes the agonising pervasiveness of love, how it leaves nothing unaffected, while ?All I Ever Want? grapples with the tangled line between love and lust, and how to purvey the former while the latter is more apparent.

So, yes, this is standard dour-boy-in-his-bedroom material, but it’s charismatic and occasionally affecting – even funny sometimes. Let it linger in the background and you’ll be drawn to it; focus too closely and you’ll become bored. If Jonny Telafone manages to mix all his inclinations one day he’ll probably release an unconditionally lovable record.
