New Noise: River of Snakes, Free Time, School Girl Report, Big Yawn

By Mess+Noise 2014-07-23T14:00:00+00:00

This week’s roundup of new music veers all over the shop, with sterling results.

? It seems hard to believe that River of Snakes are only now releasing their first album. But yes, the post-Magic Dirt trio led by powerhouse guitarist Raul Sanchez has finally answered a series of singles and EPs (including 2011?s Bad Blood*) with *Black Noise. And it sounds pretty much like you’d expect: brash, brawny and lovelorn anthems that scour the whole history of alt-rock for inspiration. It kicks off with a Mascis-style solo on the opener and single ?Beauty?, before bassist Elissa Rose (The Loveless) takes a turn at the mic on the next song to growl the refrain ?Hacksaw my heart.? Later on we have the JAMC-steeped ?I Wanna Be Your Baby? – again with a great solo worthy of Dinosaur Jr. – and the more simmering (rather than boiling) ?I Lied?, while the closing ?Separation Anxiety? is a dirge-y ballad. The band’s current drummer is Ben Wrecker from Hotel Wrecking City Traders, who took over for Glenn Evans since the recording of the LP. The album comes out tomorrow (July 25) digitally and on $10 CD through Bro Fidelity, and launches the same night at The Old Bar with support from Sun God Replica, Claws & Organs and The General. It’s $10 at the door from 9pm.

Black Noise (Album) by River Of Snakes

? Since relocating from Melbourne to New York, Panel of Judges? Dion Nania has found a good thing in his current quartet Free Time. Following last year’s self-titled debut on Underwater Peoples, the same label is releasing the new double A-side 7? ?Esoteric Tizz?/?Guess Work?. So far all we’ve heard is ?Guess Work?, a truly languid love song that takes time to question love itself. It was recorded by Real Estate’s Martin Courtney, while Woods? Jarvis Taveniere recorded the apparently more upbeat flipside. The 7? comes out August 19 with cover art by Sylvia Je’riess, who also did the artwork for the album.

? School Girl Report aren’t an easy band to suss out. After the ?gnarly internal repetition and detached shouting? heard on last year’s Success is Dating School Girl Report* CDR through Heavy Lows, Samuel Miers and Daniel Oakman have returned with the similarly self-referential *When School Girl Report Cry. Out now on limited CD through new Canberra label Valley Spirit, it’s immediately much mellower than its predecessor, reportedly capturing ?their current state of ?sad bliss?.? Produced by Bum Creek’s Sam Karmel, it includes samples of their own work, field recordings from their hometown of Bateman’s Bay, prepared pedal-less guitar and other tactics. The Tinder-browsing new video for ?Def Till We Deaf?, for example, samples ?John Da Wolf? and ?Neural Tension? from this year’s debut album by Bare Grillz.

when school girl report cry by School Girl Report

? Big Yawn made a real impression on us with last year’s dubbed-out 7? on Fallopian Tunes, and they achieve fried entrancement again with their new 12? EP Future Traffic*. Repetition is still at the centre of the Melbourne duo of Tim Slattery and Kinlay Denning, as is a nagging undercurrent of dread. Recorded and mixed at Head Gap by Fallopian Tunes fixture Simon Cotter (Document Swell), the EP blurs the lines between dub, drone and Krautrock. Harshness is a common thread through the EP, but the battered drum patterns and gauzy synth textures help make it surprisingly accessible. It’s out this week on limited vinyl and $4 download, and will be launched alongside labelmates Yolke, who are launching their new 10? *Real Memories. Also on the bill is a new Fallopian Tunes ally in Brisbane’s X in O, the work of Brainbeau?s Kat Martin, who recently did a split 7? with Document Swell. The launch is this Saturday (July 26) at Northcote Social Club, for $12 at the door from 7:30pm. More info here.
