New Noise: Melodie Nelson, Demon Parade, Oblako Lodka, FVK

By Darren Levin 2011-05-24T06:00:00+00:00

A particularly ?60s-flavoured edition of our regular round-up of new music on the internet.

? ?My Johnny? is the first single from Meditations On The Sun*, the new album by Melodie Nelson, the solo project of Lia Tsamoglou, one half of now defunct Sydney duo Moonmilk and ex-bassist for Rand and Holland. True to her Serge Gainsbourg-inspired moniker, the track is a shift away from Moonmilk’s experimental drone towards an orchestrated, evocative pop sound. It’s also been remixed by Adelaide/Sydney duo Collarbones, who render it almost indistinguishable from its original form. *Meditations On The Sun was recorded with Tony Dupe (Holly Throsby, Jack Ladder). It’ll be out through Other Tongues on June 3.

05 My Johnny by Melodie Nelson

My Johnny (Collarbones Remix) by Melodie Nelson

? The great, albeit fractious Melbourne pop band Treetops may be on permanent hiatus, but its former members are still churning out the goods. Drummer Leif Gordon-Bruce plays in Baptism of Uzi, Ben Montero has started an eponymous new outfit, and Jordan Speering has recently joined Demon Parade on guitar. Fronted by Michael Badger (ex-The Sparrows), the band has a psychedelic new single called ?To The Mountain?. It’s from an upcoming debut album mixed by Tony Lash (Dandy Warhols, Elliott Smith), which will be launched at Tone Bar in Sydney on June 17.

To The Mountain by Blank Generation

? More spot-on ?60s revivalism from Melbourne’s Fearless Vampire Killers, whose debut album is due for release in late 2011. Unsurprisingly Demon Parade’s Michael Badger has engineered first single ?For You And Me?, a garage-y pop number that dissolves into a stoned psychedelic groove midway through. Produced by Lars Stalfors (The Mars Volta), the album single will be launched in Melbourne at The Toff In Town this Saturday (May 28) and through regional Victoria in June. Details here.

For You And Me by TheFearlessVampireKillers

? Formed by Irit Rozenfeld in 2007, Melbourne’s Oblako Lodka features members and ex-members of The Motifs, ii, Crayon Fields, Milk Teddy & Hammocks and Honey. ?Brail Footpaths? is a good indication of the kind of ?ambient tropicalia? you can expect on the band’s self-titled debut LP, the second vinyl release for Melbourne micro-indie label Special Award Records. Recorded with Neil Thomason (Crayon Fields, My Disco) at Head Gap Studios and at Rozenfeld’s home in Melbourne’s inner-north, the album will be launched at Melbourne’s Grace Darling on June 10.
