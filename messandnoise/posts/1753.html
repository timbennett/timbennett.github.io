Record Reviews

Even

By Trevor 2008-04-05T00:00:00+00:00

This is album number what, six or seven? from Melbourne trio Even, and was apparently two years in the making, a fact that is proudly pointed to as a mark of intrinsic quality by their record company. And while you can certainly hear the time and care spent on it, what seems to be missing is here some sort of vital spark. There’s no sense of a driving energy under that immaculate sound.

Maybe it’s the production – a total of seven people, plus the band, get various production, co-production and executive production credits, and those are on top of engineering & mastering.

Or maybe it’s the nagging thought that when the artists whose influences are most apparent here – the Beatles, Cream & Jimi Hendrix to name a few obvious ones – were creating their music, it was new, exploratory, bursting with new ideas. It is possible for a band to work within a fairly narrow frame of reference and still avoid clich’s, of course. To take a convenient if lazy example, You Am I share a lot of the same influences as Even but build on them to create something that, while often clearly grounded in the past, sounds new. Recently both the Bakelite Age & Chris ?Klondike? Masuak have released albums that drew on similar background, yet managed to produce inventive music that was distinctly their own. But Even haven’t quite pulled it off.

There’s a token bluesy track, ‘Superstition Blues’, and a countryish one, ‘The Fool Who Made You Sad’, which came across as simple genre-hopping for it’s own sake, and some often lazy sounding moon-spoon-June lyrics, which include a couplet pairing ?pinnacle? with ?cynical?.

That said, it’s not a bad album per se, just a bit too predictable. Seeing them play live recently would indicate that many people find them agreeable enough, and they certainly have a lot of respect from their peers and their record company. This album probably won’t lose them any fans, but it may not make many converts, either.
