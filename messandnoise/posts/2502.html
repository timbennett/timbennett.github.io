Psychic Civil War

By Doug Wallen 2011-08-01T00:00:00+00:00

A social worker by day, Dan Flynn obviously takes his work home with him. His third album as Major Chord is all about navigating this life. Inspired by English therapist Jacqui Dillon, a specialist for people who hear voices, Psychic Civil War* is fixated on coping and healing. Also a recent father, multi-instrumentalist Flynn has home-recorded a collection more muted and harrowing than 2009?s *The Rabbit Hole. It’s spooky, wise, and probing, guided by fluid acoustic guitar and meditative mantras.

But despite the opening line, ?We’ve got nothing left/We’ve not been told yet?, Flynn’s songs play like hard-won celebrations, not defeats. Providing his own folk-pop backing (guitar, organ, keyboards, drums, harmonies), he weaves between light and dark as he tries to reconcile the two. The witchy standout ?Gather All This Love? communicates dread even as it calls for flanking oneself with the support of others. The title track belies its image of fraught internal battle with a sense of dreamy relief, and the closing ?Lay Down Your Gun (Before You Go And Shoot Someone)? ends things on a much more uplifting note than they began. A few songs lend themselves to Iron & Wine comparisons, yet mostly they feel all too unique.

Tomorrow Night – Major Chord by showoffservices

Guests appear on violin and cello, but Psychic Civil War remains a direct line of communication with Flynn (also of Children of the Wave). He wants to help others and himself at the same time, and these songs prove an effective balm. Whether or not the lyrics succeed as therapy for everyone, the melodies are sweet and warm.
