Walls

By Steph Kretowicz 2011-05-13T00:00:00+00:00

You realise what egoists us humans are when a group like Tegan and Sara pick a band like An Horse as their newly discovered talent to introduce to the world. Perhaps the comparison between the two acts is tired, but it’s still rather apt within the earnest indie-pop spectrum. There’s a palpable affinity between the Quin twins and the Brisbane-born band’s emotional songwriting, tied over by a minimal rock sensibility and a conscientious focus on introspective lyrics about feelings and being in love.

Now based apart – lead singer/guitarist Kate Cooper is in Montreal, while drummer Damon Cox is in Melbourne – the duo have followed their 2009 debut Rearrange Beds* by reuniting in Vancouver with producer Howard Redekopp, who’s also worked with (surprise, surprise) Tegan and Sara. (Sara Quin even makes a few cameos on the record, most notably singing back-ups on ?Swallow The Sea?.) Inspired by the departure lounges and introspective downtime of years of touring, *Walls begins with an open letter to nostalgia in ?Dressed Sharply?. ?I have nothing to tell you, since the last time I wrote,? begins Cooper, striking a poignant note on the displacement and anxiety of a nomadic existence; a theme that pervades the rest of the album.

An Horse – Dressed Sharply by anhorse

Within the pulsing rawness of limited means that no doubt makes this band so appealing to some, An Horse presents itself as a career band with little ambition beyond writing cathartic tunes for the hypersensitive. As indisposed as one might be to the often-stifling earnestness of the subject matter – of counting a lover’s eyelashes or the emotional processes of forgetting an ex – there are moments here of creative clarity. Like the breathless repetition and telling silences of songs like ?Brain on the Table?, or ?Windows of the City?, which captures a rising passion within measured build-ups. It goes some way in explaining why people have become so emotionally invested in them in the first place.
