New Noise: The Fabergettes, Gold Class, Pronto, Red Red Krovvy

By Mess+Noise 2014-10-01T14:00:00+00:00

This week’s roundup is heady with great new punk, post-punk, garage and power-pop offerings.

? Self-described ?queens of trash-bag pop,? The Fabergettes indeed stick to the poppier side of the garage canon without ever sounding glossy or wan. Their new single ?Ready to Go? is short and scuffed, with an infectious enthusiasm (cue handclaps) rising up from the lo-fi clatter. Armed with a cute video, it’s a worthy successor to last September’s more doo-wop-leaning four-song 7? Big Bruiser. The Sydney quartet launch the single this Saturday (Oct 4) at Brighton Up Bar with kindred spirits Food Court, Disgusting People and The Bad Bitch Choir. It’s $10 at the door from 8pm.

Ready To Go by The Fabergettes

? Belying their shiny name, Gold Class make spare, scratchy post-punk that hinges on the gloomy vocals of M+N* contributor Adam Curley. Their debut single ?Michael? is a perfect example, with Curley evoking Zoo-era Ceremony (and of course Joy Division before that) over tangled melodies. It’s got a simple, effective (/tv/4672856) by Chicago filmmaker John Orris, and a much more restless B-side in ?Gone?. The two songs were recorded with Neil Thomason at Head Gap and will pair up for a limited 7? out next month. It’s launching on Saturday, November 1 at Boney, with supports TBA, but first Gold Class are supporting The Peep Tempel tomorrow (Oct 3) at The Tote for the launch of their terrific new album *Tales. Then they head up to Brisbane for a free Trainspotters gig on Saturday, October 25 with Per Purpose, Multiple Man and Workshop.

Michael/Gone 7″ by Gold Class

? How good are Pronto? The latest discovery for Melbourne label Off the Hip, the five-piece play a punked-up strain of power-pop that nails the catchy anxiety of the classic Stiff Records output. We’ve heard all of their debut album, When You’re Gone, and it’s a cracker. Only three songs from it are online right now, including the gnashing, spittle-flying gem ?Red Flag?. The band just launched the album last weekend at the Off the Hip store, and they’re playing again soon supporting Red Red Krovvy (see next capsule down). Let’s hope for even more shows to come, because this is the best power-pop we’ve heard since Loose Pills.

? Man, the bratty-as-hell vocals on the new Red Red Krovvy single are just an instant draw. The rest of the 66-second ?Real Life? is pretty great too, all buzzy with distortion and thudding along. Self-described as ?a sequel of sorts,? it comes from a forthcoming 7? on Perth label Helta Skelta. It follows an eight-song (!) 7? on R.I.P. Society and coincides roughly with a four-city East Coast tour starting tomorrow. If you didn’t know, the Sydney trio includes Ben Warnock from Bed Wettin? Bad Boys, Adam Ritchie from Drunk Mums and Ash Wyatt from Little Desert (and ex-Pop Singles).

Fri, Oct 3 – Agincourt Hotel, Sydney, NSW [w/Housewives, Sweat Tongue + Meat Tray]
Sun, Oct 5 – Newcastle Weekender @ Croatian Club, Newcastle, NSW [day gig]
Sat, Oct 11 – Brisbane Hotel, Hobart, TAS [w/Drunk Elk, Bi-Hour + Mess O Reds]
Sun, Oct 12 – Grace Darling, Melbourne, VIC [free, 5pm, w/Pronto + Power]
