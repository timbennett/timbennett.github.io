Track by Track: Pearls

By Mess+Noise 2015-02-22T13:00:00+00:00

RYAN CAESAR* and *CASS KIELY of Melbourne trio Pearls take us through their long-awaited debut album, from vintage synths to shitty commutes.

?Big Shot?

Ryan: Last song written, first released. I wrote the main riff to this tune while tracking guitars for the rest of the album at Soundpark. Recorded a rough version at home and then we put it all together at Haima [Marriott]?s studio. In fact, I believe most of the guitars are takes remaining from the initial demo that somehow Haima polished up. They sure didn’t sound like that when I finished them. ?Big Shot? was by far the easiest song to deal with. There were no big changes made, everything just slotted in to place. Most of the work done on the song was fun stuff like messing about with space echoes and the like, with further turd polishing by Haima. They certainly weren’t all like that.

?Albion?

Ryan: Years ago I moved from the strange bubble of North Melbourne to a house on Albion St in Brunswick. I thought I’d moved to the end of the earth. I was certain my life was over. This tune is loosely about that time. Haima owns this great synth, the Ensoniq SQ-80. It’s all over the record but probably most so on this song. It seems to sit really well against the organ. The song was written around the main guitar progression at rehearsal and has not changed too much since its inception. Cass sorta automatically hit the harmony she’s singing in the first few run-throughs. Low-maintenance song.

?Straight Through the Heart?

Ryan: This song, on the other hand, was nowhere near as easy. We found ourselves chasing our tails with it a few times. It was an early favourite among the band so we stuck with it, thank god. I love how it turned out. The drum machine is a mixture of Ellice Blakeney]?s live drums and an [Oberheim DX. I suspect I may have used every guitar I own on this tune too. What a prick.

?Me and My Girl?

Ryan: More SQ-80. I actually started writing this song singing it to my cat but thought a cat song may not make it onto the album, so I smartened it up with some deeply emotional lyrics and here we are. I do love my cat though. She, as usual, is unresponsive to my wooing.

?Better Off Alone?

Ryan: The original Pearls song. The first one we ever wrote. I’m sure it was probably longer back then but I can’t seem to remember what changes we made to it. I broke my ?Better Off Alone? guitar the week before recording so John Lee was kind enough to lend us a bunch of cool gear and also, thank god, a beautiful Fender Jaguar. A much finer guitar than the Jaguar I’d broken in the first place. Mine has been in the sin bin ever since. Shoot me a msg if you want to buy a Jag.

?Part Timer?

Ryan: I used to live in Coburg and work in Armadale. That’s a shitty commute at 6am. This song is a whinge about that and a few other things. First-world blues.

?Baby?

Cass: ?Baby? is one of the songs we recorded a bit differently – the drums are a bit looser and it has more of a live sound. I always think of it as a kinda ?90s love song. We played around with the vocal melody a bit in the studio, trying a few different ways of singing it and using doubled vocals. I was listening to a lot of early Anita Lane at the time of the recording and I wanted it to have that sort of hushed/delicate feel. I’m pretty much just droning three notes on the organ the whole time.

?Dirty Water?

Cass: It was cool to get asked to cover this song for the comp Antipodean Interpolations of the First Psychedelic Era*] as, like most people, I grew up on *Nuggets and it was an introduction to heaps of great bands for me. We changed [[the original] up quite a lot; the vocal melodies and structure is kind of stripped back. We met Haima for the first time recording this song. It was a really fun day. We recorded it at Soundpark: I remember doing my vocals in one take and it all came together pretty easily, which was good. We thought Haima was a champ and ended up recording our whole album with him.

?Pretend You’re Mine?

Ryan: This started off a hugely different tune to the album version. The very last night of recording, Haima plugged my guitar into a world of nifty gear and thus created the main tone of the song. I love it. It probably would have fallen by the wayside without that spark. It was a tough one to put together but we got there in the end. Handclaps done in Haima’s kitchen and more space echo than a King Tubby record.

+
