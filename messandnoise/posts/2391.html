The Bones

By Jodi Biddle 2010-10-24T00:00:00+00:00

I’m going to tell you about my new favourite band. They’re called Rocketsmiths. Their debut LP The Bones has been playing non-stop out of every musical device I own for more than a week. They had one of those names that became familiar in support slots on gig posters around town but I hadn’t ever really looked into them until they were playing with The Boat People recently. The got up on stage in front of a crowd of seated punters (do people in Melbourne do that? Or is it a northern peculiarity?) and looked around uncertainly.

?Well, we have a new record coming out soon, so… we thought we’d just play it from start to finish,? explained front-man Dom Miller. Then they ripped into some of the most frenzied, glorious, ridiculous, messy live music I’ve ever seen. On-stage, Miller is all wild eyed mania and peculiar bouncing mannerisms that make watching them perform completely captivating. He shrugs his shoulders, he flails his mic stand, he throws about his mop of curly hair and nearly froths at the mouth. You’re wondering what the fuck he’s doing, but it’s all perfect for their brand of slightly askew, carnivale pop rock.

The Bones is the culmination of a few years of hard work – and it shows. There’s no fat here, no weighty bloated album fillers or thoughtful pauses of balladry. It’s full of curious narratives, too; tales of lost love and violence and monsters and a guy called Dennis. With barely constrained vocals laid across keyboard lines picked straight from black-and-white horror movies and shifty circus tents, messy punk guitars and pounding, brassy percussion, this album is like a cabaret for petty criminality and heavy drinking.

Standout tracks curiously don’t include the two singles they’ve released so far (‘Underground’ and ‘A Shot At The Seat’). They’re good album tracks but they do less to showcase their strange aesthetic than others. Rather, I’d choose ‘Doctor’ for its catchy vocals that teeter wildly between smooth contemplation and bawling, feverish shouts; ‘Henry Winkler’ because of its utterly classic cock-rock line, ?You! You gotta lotta nerve/To walk in here like you’re Henry fucking Winkler?; and ‘Demons’ for its pitch-perfect harmonies and generally agreeable theme (it’s about hating Brisbane).

In a town overflowing with polite indie folk-pop, it’s good to hear some balls-out fucking rock’n’roll again.
