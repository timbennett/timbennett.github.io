Kram?s Ultimate Mixtape

By Darren Levin 2009-03-12T13:00:00+00:00

With Kram’s first solo disc, ‘Mixtape’, hitting the shelves today (March 13), we asked the Spiderbait sticksman to put together his ultimate compilation tape.


1. Black Sabbath
?Black Sabbath?

?This song and album is awesome and scary and wicked and metal and I dig how it starts with a stormy rain from Satan.?



2. Devo
?Girl U Want?

?I was a huge devo fan as a kid and still play this song heaps. A great band indeed.?



3. Dead Kennedys
?Holiday in Cambodia?

?All of Spiderbait were DK fanatics and we got to meet Jello Biafra once. Amazing dude, incredible song.



4. The Beatles
?You’ve Got To Hide Your Love Away?

?I’ve always loved this tune from the moment I first heard it. It’s actually a really sad song about their manager Brian Epstein, but the melody is really uplifting. Great tambourine work from Ringo too.?



5. Stevie Wonder
?Superstition?

?The funkiest track ever recorded. Truly fucken awesome.?



6. Patsy Cline
?Walkin? After Midnight?

?Love patsy and she had a massive voice that could also make you weep.?



7. Bob Marley and The Wailers
?Exodus?

?Bob was the man and The Wailers were incredible. One of the greatest bands of all time.?



8. AC/DC
?Highway to Hell?

?What can you say about early Acca Dacca that hasn’t already been said? The best rock’n?roll band ever, no contest.?



9. Tina Turner
?Nutbush City Limits?

?This track is one of my all-time favourite dance tunes. Tina is the bomb.?



10. The Beach Boys
?Good Vibrations?

?Probably the best single ever written. Poor Brian lost his mind after though. That can happen sometimes.?



11. Genesis
?The Musical Box?

?Total hippy prog rock epic from the ?70s when Peter Gabriel was the lead singer. The song goes for 14 minutes. Oh yeah – ?



12. De La Soul
?A Roller Skating Jam Named “Saturdays”?

??90s hip-hop is awesome and I love De La Soul. Such a great vibe and lyrics.?
