M+N Critics Poll 2014

By Mess+Noise 2014-12-17T13:00:00+00:00

The results are in for our annual critics poll. We count down our writers? top 25 Australian and New Zealand records of 2014.

25. Infinity Broke

River Mirrors
(Come to the Dark Side Luke/MGM)

What we said: ?It’s a brazen rock album courting danger without a safe word. Comfort zones are left buried in the rubble.?

24. Geoffrey O’Connor

Fan Fiction
(Chapter Music)

What we said: ?A work just short of pornography – 11 brilliant bursts of ceaseless titillation.?

More reading: Interview

23. The Harpoons

Falling For You
(Two Bright Lakes/Remote Control)

What we said: ?Songs to turn a listener into a believer.?

22. East Brunswick All Girls Choir

Seven Drummers
(Independent)

What we said: ?Frayed ends, grotesquery, darkness and futility.?

More reading: Track by Track

21. Guerre

Ex Nihilo
(Yes Please/Remote Control)

More reading: Forthcoming

20. Tiny Ruins

Brightly Painted One
(Spunk)

What we said: ?Elegant, folk-inflected songs – imbued with a gentle sort of wisdom.?

More reading: Europe photo diary

19. Augie March

Havens Dumb
(Dark Satanic/Caroline)

What we said: ?This is a good record, secure and agreeable, but far from living dangerously.?

18. You Beauty

Jersey Flegg
(Rice is Nice)

What we said: ?It’s a genuine dark horse to take home the Winfield Cup for album of the year.?

More reading: Interview and Track by Track

17. Ausmuteants

Order of Information
(Aarght/Goner)

What we said: ?Infuriating, repetitive and exasperating. So why does typing those words not feel like an insult??

16. King Gizzard & The Lizard Wizard

I’m in Your Mind Fuzz
(Flightless/Remote Control)

What we said: ?With each new [album] comes stronger songwriting and more consistently excellent collections of songs.?

15. Jack Ladder & The Dreamlanders

Playmates
(Self Portrait/Inertia)

What we said: ?Poaching from the middle-of-the-road markers of a generation prior isn’t always the most inspired musical move, but it’s undeniable how much it suits Ladder.?

14. Peter Escott

The Long O
(Bedroom Suck)

What we said: ?He’s given us an album ruddy with poetic detail, only to fog all that detail with eccentricity and uncertainty.?

13. Eastlink

Mullum Mullum
(In the Red)

More reading: Track by Track

12. Mere Women

Your Town
(Poison City)

What we said: ?A deeply relatable record that strikes at the heart of our upbringing.?

More reading: Track by Track

11. Low Life

Dogging
(R.I.P. Society/Disinfect)

What we said: ?Aggressively taking society to task while it subverts and reflects it through a haze of ragged guitars.?

More reading: Interview

10. The Peep Tempel

Tales
(Wing Sing)

What we said: ?Not only a near-perfect punk rock record, but a brutal narrative of the reality of contemporary suburban Australia.?

More reading: Track by Track

9. Harmony

Carpetbombing
(Poison City)

What we said: ?These songs sound like they writhe from his throat against their will, more exorcism than catharsis.?

More reading: Interview and Track by Track

8. Seekae

The Worry
(Future Classic)

What we said: ?While the reference points to IDM remain, the abstract experimentalism is hand-selected from the underground rather than entrenched within.?

7. HTRK

Psychic 9-5 Club
(Mistletone/Ghostly International)

What we said: ?Defined more by open spaces than light-filled ones – and space can be a dark place.?

6. Blank Realm

Grassed Inn
(Bedroom Suck/Fire)

What we said: ?Markedly cleaner than what Blank Realm are known for, but it’s still a long way from sanitary.?

More reading: Interview

5. The Ocean Party

Soft Focus
(Spunk/Jigsaw)

What we said: ?A group of lifelong friends growing up together in front of an audience, maturing as people while honing their songwriting.?

More reading: Track by Track

4. Black Cab

Games of the XXI Olympiad
(Interstate 40/Remote Control)

What we said: ?Black Cab have created a masterpiece.?

3. NUN

NUN
(Aarght!/Avant)

What we said: ?NUN operate from mirrored halls, looking to the future from the past, disorientated and out of place.?

More reading: Track by Track

2. Laura Jean

Laura Jean
(Chapter Music)

What we said: ?So clear and honest in its expression, it creates its own still waters. A restful, meditative place to be, even if just for a little while.?

More reading: Interview

1. Total Control

Typical System
(Iron Lung)

What we said: ?It’s a record so powerful that it can be accepted not just by the niche audiences it has always fuelled, but in a context broad enough to reach both the national broadcaster and mainstream international audiences. Enlighteningly, Total Control gave nothing up to get there.?
