Making Mirrors

By Matt Shea 2011-08-19T00:00:00+00:00

One of the more interesting stories to come out of triple j’s Hottest 100 Australian Albums of All Time poll in July was the high placement of Gotye’s Like Drawing Blood*. Clocking in at number 11, it was a timely confirmation of the way the record has drip-fed into Australia’s pop-cultural consciousness over the past five years. It was also a reminder that, regardless of its eventual quality, there would be a very different type of reception for Victorian multi-instrumentalist Wally De Backer’s follow-up release, *Making Mirrors.

Like Drawing Blood* had time to sink in with local audiences, its lengthy public digestion giving it potential to soundtrack summers and winters, new loves and fresh break-ups. *Making Mirrors won’t necessarily have that luxury, and ratcheting up the tension prior to release was of course the epic single ?Somebody That I Used To Know?, De Backer’s duet with Kiwi singer Kimbra Johnson rapidly accumulating YouTube hits and radio plays.

It turns out that ?Somebody That I Used To Know? does indeed stand head and shoulders above anything else on Making Mirrors. But it never dominates, and that speaks to De Backer’s determination that this would truly be an album, rather than just a collection of songs. ?Somebody That I Used To Know?, just like everything else here, is seamlessly sewn into the fabric of the album and never allowed to shade the rest of the work.

Somebody That I Used To Know (feat. Kimbra) by Gotye

It’s a trick that works both ways. There are cuts on Making Mirrors that would be considered lacklustre if it wasn’t for the company they keep. When ?Eyes Wide Open? appeared in late-2010 as a precursor to the album, many Gotye fans listened in mild disappointment to the over-earnest enviro-lite anthem that had been dished up. But here, couched between ?Somebody? and the rolling thunder of ?Smoke And Mirrors? (and removed from its appalling video), it works a little better.

Eyes Wide Open by Gotye

Other songs are elevated by their details. De Backer is a fine songwriter, but he’s an even better technician. He’s not afraid to give himself space to fill his songs with exhilarating flourishes – whether the stabbing brass of ?Smoke And Mirrors?, or the dramatic orchestral drops scattered throughout ?State Of The Art?. Generally speaking, Making Mirrors* is at its best when De Backer stretches his legs like this, in a way that’s not altogether dissimilar to *Like Drawing Blood.

Still, there are a few occasions when things don’t come together quite as well as they should. ?In Your Light? sounds like Paul Young being rescued from the restaurant game – it’s little more than pleasant filler – while ?I Feel Better? is almost brought down by its own hackneyed lyrics. Separated by different sequencing these songs would have worked better, but they sit together at the heart of the album and the impetus they provide on initial listens soon turns into irritation.

Somebody That I Used To Know (feat. Kimbra) by Gotye

Making Mirrors* nevertheless carries its weaker moments like other albums can’t, and proves superior to *Like Drawing Blood* at least in its logic as a complete piece of work. De Backer’s ease with matching intelligence to a pop sensibility often has him compared to Peter Gabriel, but it’s an idea that could just as easily be applied to his fastidious need to create an album around a single theme. As a listener it makes it easier to accept the weird cross weaving of Midas touch and patent absurdity. There are times when he gets through on sheer conviction (?I Feel Better?) and others when he’s so subtle you almost miss the song completely (?Bronte?). It means *Making Mirrors is a rich experience, even if its not always a totally convincing one.
