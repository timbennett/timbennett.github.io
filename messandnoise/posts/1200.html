New Noise: Dick Diver, Step-Panther, Geno Carrapetta, Soft Power

By Mess+Noise 2014-06-11T14:00:00+00:00

Soundtracks, dreams, approaches and genres blur together in this week’s roundup of new music.

? ?Dream time is done,? sings unofficial Australian of the Year Al Montfort on ?New Name Blues?, the gorgeous new single from Dick Diver. And yet the tune is very much defined by dreaminess, from Montfort’s leisurely drawl to an extended instrumental send-off smudged with effects and saxophone. It’s the first song from the Melbourne quartet since last year’s Calendar Days, which beat out The Drones to top our Readers Poll. It’s also the band’s debut single to be released in the US – thanks to boutique label Fruits & Flowers – coming out June 29 as a 7? with characteristically perfect cover art by the band’s Steph Hughes. What’s more: the B-side is a Rupert Edwards-sung cover of the jangly 1980s singalong ?Lonely Life? by Coloured Stone. Both songs endear us all the more to Dick Diver, while letting the band stretch happily out into more country, folk and experimental territory.

Dick Diver – New Name Blues by fruits & flowers

? Step-Panther have tapped a lot of different sides of themselves in just a few years, swinging from the surf cues of their first EP and Black Lips-ish garage of their self-titled album to the itchy ska of ?D Minus? and Weezer-style stoner bubblegum of 2013?s Dreamcrusher* EP. The new single ?Nowhere? is another relative change of pace, starting with clean guitar strumming and frontman Stephen Bourke’s trademark shaky vocals before things turn dark and violent (*Game of Thrones* was reportedly an influence) then jammy and, finally, stricken with consumptive fuzz guitar. Produced by Big Scary’s Tom Iansek with a lo-fi animated video in tow, it’s the first we’re hearing of the Sydney trio’s second LP, *Strange But Nice, slated for August release through HUB/Inertia. With Hockey Dad’s Zach Stephenson now playing bass live with the band – as their usual bassist takes an overseas break – Step-Panther are about to launch ?Nowhere?.

Sat, June 28 – The Workers Club, Melbourne, VIC [w/Warmth Crashes In + ESC]
Fri, July 4 – Brighton Up Bar, Sydney, NSW [w/Adults + Doom Mountain]

? London-based Sydney native Geno Carrapetta caught our ears with his April single ?ternit??, and thankfully his new album Priv?* follows suit: sleazy, sensual and slinky, with just the right quotient of Eurotrash. Carrapetta recorded the album at home in London with Paul Housden, his bandmate from Gentlemen (who are not to be confused with these guys). His fourth LP, it’s steeped in the French influences of Serge Gainsbourg, Jacques Dutronc and Fran’oise Hardy – and yet it’s a great deal more murky and moody than you might anticipate from just those touchstones. Carrapetta’s is an easy sound to sink into, but not to dissect. (Although soundtracks to classic Italian horror films and *Rosemary’s Baby* factor in somewhere.) *Priv? is out now through RIP Records in the UK and First Love in Australia.

? When they’re not busy with Greg Boring – ?the Citizen Kane of consciously shitty backyard punk bands? – and the label-ish creation Disembraining Machine, Joel Stern and Andrew McLellan have made time for Soft Power, a new project that’s pretty impossible to pin down in terms of genre. Shape-shifting pop? Mutant hip-hop? Synth-pop turned to drone? Whatever you call it, ?If You Come Around: You Know What Happens? trips many distinct triggers in its 145-second lifespan. There’s even a (/tv/4659180) to further mess with our heads. A motley ?collection of souls? that also includes Sophia Brous and Sewers? Joshua Watson, Soft Power make their proper debut later this month with a pink-vinyl 12? – including this track – limited to 250 copies through Brisbane entity All Day Breakfast Enterprises, with the cover art to feature Alice Lang’s 2012 piece ?Smiley?.
