Denpasar Love Triangle

By timscottt 2010-05-01T00:00:00+00:00

Splendid Friends is a collaboration between Craig McElhinney and Camryn Rothenbury, two Perth guys making things happen in the west’s growing experimental music scene. Between them they have put out stuff on labels such as Meupe, Farmer Frontier and Badminton Bandit, but team up this time to release this CD-R of droney, riff-heavy loopage on the Grave New World label.

Their bio claims that the two ?spazz out on cosmic, tropical drone grooves, encounter all new cebro-percussive waterslides, play gamelan upon elephants and braid multi-coloured leisure soundscapes?. Loosely translated: it sounds like they’re tripping balls.

Judging by its title, this seems to be a concept record of sorts about Australian tourist bungles at Indonesia’s Ngurah Rai International Airport – and the opening 21-minute opus ‘Give Me Corby Bud’ confirms as much. It’s an instrumental about a certain Bali inmate and her body board bag. The first five minutes of tinny percussion even sound like I imagine cup, spoons or well-manicured fingernails running across the bars of a Kerobokan Prison cell would. From here the song pushes further into a dark vortex of unappetizing nasi goreng and echoey rhythms. ‘Head of the Evil Gang’ brings more of the same haunting eeriness, this time accompanied by bugged-out howls and affected moans. Darkness just got darker.

These long, drawn-out and abstract pieces won’t be everyone’s cup of tea. But if you’re up for something challenging and experimental, you’ll find it on this trio of tracks.
