Track: Tin Power Pt 1

By Shaun 2009-09-10T00:00:00+00:00

For the purposes of this review Oliver Mann is not from Melbourne, he’s not Paddy Mann’s brother, and it doesn’t mean a damn whether this is his first or last ?release?. No facts or context can begin to explain or answer for ?Tin Power Pt. 1?, nor will hearing it necessarily prove helpful.

Listening to Mann can be like seeing a familiar ghost: your muscles seize up, your jaw locks and scepticism drops its shields, leaving you weak and captivated in front of an apparition – or in this case – merely a song. Here, Mann’s tremulous baritone spooks a circuitous guitar line that resonates barrenly, as if wrenched from the atmosphere of a wilted and forgotten country town somewhere near Bundanyabba. This quietly tragic music plays forever in this town and ?Tin Power Pt. 1? documents the five minutes Mann spent passing through it.

Where artists like CW Stoneking drink deeply from the past and then deliver us their resulting piss, Mann’s deeply masculine operatic vocals manage to imbue his songs with a desolate longing for some ostensibly purer past. He haunts from the periphery of parched golden-hued memories; his grandiose voice suspended in a soundscape carefully devoid of artifice.

Yet it’s not at all nostalgic really. It is, on the other hand, far too generous and uncomplicated to really feel a part of this time. ?Tin Power Pt. 1? is such an anomaly that after these five minutes the world and all the fancy modern music it favours seems eons away. It’s stunning, uncanny and emotionally draining.
