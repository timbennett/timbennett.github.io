Cellular Bits #3: Scissor Lock, Abstract Mutation, Elizabeth Rose

By lawsonfletcher 2013-09-23T14:00:00+00:00

LAWSON FLETCHER rounds up the latest Bandcamp, Soundcloud and YouTube streams from the fringes of local electronic music.

Charles Murdoch
Weathered Straight
(EP, Future Classic)

Bass music – a meeting point between house and R&B overlaid with smudged, smoky atmospherics – has long since stopped registering anything like a new horizon that the somewhat ambitious and often appended ?future? prefix would suggest. Instead, as the sensual mirror of club music, ?future bass? is basically late-night comfort food, easily digested and leaving you with a warm but vaguely empty feeling inside.

Weathered Straight is a paragon of the genre in this sense, a sophisticated, softly-padded EP positively lush with woozy bass lines and plush synths. Oscar Key Sung – currently locked in an epic battle with Marcus Whale for the best ?feat.? in Australian electronica – steps into ?Dekire? to add some restrained but welcome textural variety. His fragile falsetto plays off the thickly layered synths and quasi-garage rhythm nicely. A definite highlight of an EP that could be summed up as a particularly pleasant nod.

Elizabeth Rose
?The Good Life?
(single, Inertia Access)

In a fairer world, 22-year-old Sydney singer and producer Elizabeth Rose would be Australia’s future-pop megastar, instead of the bland talent-show runoff and desperate Beyonc? wannabes we (cringe) currently export. Grafting the bass template onto a bracing sub-130 BPM tempo, ?The Good Life? perfectly locates the pop sensibility within an expanded sonic palette.

If it sounds like I’m writing PR copy right now, well, good: this is pure fire. Australia doesn’t do pop this well that often, and when we do it should be celebrated.

Scissor Lock
Degrassi Dream
(cassette, 3BS)

Collarbones? Marcus Whale continues his solo project’s #conceptual* but also highly listenable exploration of the mechanics and mythos of celebrity, with two tracks made solely out of processed samples of songs by The-Dream and dialogue from breakup scenes on teen drama series *Degrassi*. Perhaps the unintentional but highly suggestive connective membrane here is the now infamous ambient version of Justin Bieber’s ?U Smile?, made simply by slowing the track down 800%, and the obscured mug of the Biebz that graced the cover of Scissor Lock’s last EP, *Churn.

Don’t be fooled into thinking this is just some ambient prank, though. Degrassi Dream* is actually a far more cerebral and unsettling listen, suggesting not so much a pleasant space of nostalgia mediated by our teenage obsessions but rather a murky, altered zone of mutated memory stains. Like *Churn before it, you sense that Whale is clutching at some ineffable dimension that perhaps only music can hope to represent. It makes for an uncanny listening experience.

Friendships
?Body Melt? feat. Crescent (DEER remix)
(track, independent)

Having already produced a club-ready refix of Kanye West’s ?New Slaves? and a blissed-out take on the much-remixed Cashmere Cat joint ?Kiss Kiss?, DEER has continued his run of strong remixes with a Jersey Club flip of previous Cellular Bits inclusion and fellow Melbourne producer Friendships.

DEER seems to approach remixes as an opportunity to amplify latent directions or possibilities within the source material. ?Body Melt? already suggested the rapid-fire bass kicks and vocal cut-ups that are Jersey de rigueur; DEER intensifies these and adds a classic break, some cheeky samples and synth staccato, and the result is a hybrid bouncy/druggy tribute to one of the most exciting and gleeful styles currently enjoying its time in the sun.

Various Artists
Winter
(EP, Feral Media)

Feral Media has long been at the forefront of the smartly packaged and curated series. The latest is a series of multi-artist EPs inspired by that perennial artistic muse, the four seasons (the climactic segments, not the hotel chain). Kane Ikin’s ?Storming? is a brilliant introduction to Winter – a boldly unfurling anatomy of a storm front that is documentary in its shift from detail to atmosphere. This almost imperceptibly slides into Tuff Sherm’s own tempest, the primal, haunting ?The Meriton Winter?.

Whilst the first two tracks grapple with the season’s darkened, rumbling tenor, Scissor Lock and Panoptique Electrical offer treatments that seem more inspired by cascading raindrops and glistening streetscapes. It’s a beautiful, heady compilation whose clarity of vision stands out even more starkly now that spring has sprung.

Winter EP by Various

Sh’nya
0
(EP, Bossman Records)

Indian-born, Melbourne-based Bal Singh’s Sh’nya project is a welcome inversion of the typical ?world music? narrative, in which Western producers cynically add high-gloss post-production percussion to various musical traditions in order to make them palatable for cafe patrons and suburban dinner parties. 0 is a deeply knotted, frequently unpredictable exploration of the shared abstractions of beat culture and classical Indian music.

Here, cultural exchange, rather than cultural imperialism, becomes the ground zero for playful experimentation. A track like ?Akashic Record Store?, with its diasporic multi-vocal and multi-instrumental tapestry, perhaps best achieves Singh’s goal of an integrated diversity. But the EP as a whole stands as an adventurous, artful record.

BR011 – 0 by Sh’nya

Abstract Mutation
Fake Keygen
(cassette, 1080p)

The perfect soundtrack to corporate calisthenics sessions held on Level 3 of the virtual plaza, Abstract Mutation injects the stock vaporwave sound with a bit of Antipodean shuffle and swing, in the process suggesting that Melbourne’s current fetish for the scratchy house template might be its own unwitting branding exercise, fated to decadence and decay.

The same on-trend retrofitted acid/techno/house textures and tempos are deployed here, but rather than in service of an arch, writerly genre worship, they’re used only as materials for a goofball reappropriation into the territory of parody. True to its title, the EP is the sonic analogue of an incorrectly parsed zip file, all broken structures and unknown extensions. It’s like a constantly reappearing error window telling you, ?This will not work.?

“Fake Keygen” by Abstract Mutation
