New Noise: The Panics, Ben Salter, Harmony, Naked On The Vague

By Darren Levin 2011-05-29T14:00:00+00:00

A regular column, where we pillage Soundcloud for the best new music on the interwebs so you don’t have to.

? On ?Majesty?, the first new music from The Panics since 2007?s Cruel Guards*, Jae Laffer offers a compelling case for a republic over rolling timpani and acoustic guitar: ?Who needs advice from cardinals? Who votes for Governor Generals? Who’ll offer those fellas an amnesty? Who’s been touched by Her Majesty?? The single hails from the band’s fourth album *Rain On The Humming Wire, which will be out through Dew Process in late July. It was recorded at New York’s Dreamland and Electric Lady studios with producer John O’Mahony (Metric, Alberta Cross). The Panics will be launching the single on the road in July. Dates here.

? For his debut solo offering, The Gin Club’s Ben Salter has dug deep into his suitcase of songs to bring you ?The Coward?, a melancholic pop tune written more than 10 years ago. As expected, it deals with the kind of things confronting your average 20-something student: poverty, belief, mental health, love and share-housing. The track comes from Salter’s debut album The Cat, which was recorded and produced with Gareth Liddiard (The Drones) and Robert F. Cranny (Sarah Blasko, 78 Saab) at Liddiard’s rural studio in Havilah, Victoria. Financed to the tune of $7000 via online funding platform Indiegogo.com, it’ll be out independently via MGM Distribution on August 5, with ?The Coward? available as a digital download this Friday (June 3) via Salter’s website.

Ben Salter – The Coward by Mess+Noise

? A mongrel concoction of gallows blues and balladry. This is how Melbourne collective/supergroup Harmony describe themselves. The band features Tom Lyngcoln (The Nation Blue, Lee Memorial), Alex Kastaniotis (Remake Remodel) and Jon Chapple (McLusky), along with a three-part gospel vocal ensemble comprised of Amanda Roff (Ukeladies), Quinn Veldhuis and Maria Kastaniotis. ?Cacophonous Vibes? is the title track of their debut 7?, which is out now on their own Solar/Sonar label. An LP is due for release soon.

Cacophonous Vibes by Solar/Sonar Records

? Sydney’s Naked On The Vague have a new double A-side out through R.I.P Society Records that further states their ?stadium goth? ambitions. A shift in aesthetic from 2010 Siltbreeze LP Heaps Of Nothing towards a more accessible sound (relatively speaking, of course), the ?Abstract Figures?/?Reflections of Strangeness? 7″ will be launched this Saturday (June 4) at Red Rattler in Sydney with Lakes, Blank Realm and Model Citizen; June 11 at The Workers Club in Melbourne with Fabulous Diamonds and Blank Realm; and June 18 at Woodlands in Brisbane (supports TBA).
