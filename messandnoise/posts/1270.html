New From NZ: Bunnies on Ponies, Career Girls, Dad Jokes, Emily Edrosa

By Mess+Noise 2014-10-13T13:00:00+00:00

Our latest New Zealand roundup runs the gamut from lo-fi to, uh, mid-fi.

? Samuel F. Scott and Tom Callwood of the perennially underrated The Phoenix Foundation make up Bunnies on Ponies with Cassette’s Craig Terris. After two albums together under Scott’s name – that’d be 2006?s The Hunt Brings Us Life* and 2008?s *Straight Answer Machine* – the trio have adopted the above band name for their new LP, *Heat Death of the Universe, recorded over six weeks while Scott was immobile following an injury. The lead single is the below ?Castle Van Halen? and positively bursts with fuzzy, hooky, hummable indie rock. There’s even a gnarly guitar solo, as you’d rightly expect from a song with Van Halen in the title. You can pre-order the album on CD or coloured gatefold vinyl here.

? Billed all too cheekily as ?one dude’s doof quest,? Career Girls is the electronic project of Auckland’s Lawrence Goodwin, who has played in the noisy Caroles and pursued the janglier outlet Cheats. His name-your-price six-song EP Going Deep mingles strong whiffs of both irony and sincerity, but it’s worth a listen despite its daggier moments. There’s an exaggerated, plastic-like feel to it that helps subvert the more straightforward thump-a-thons. It’s the latest offering from the Kerosene Comic Book camp, which you should acquaint yourself with if you haven’t already. A download of the EP also gets you a remix of the track ?Pro Boarder? by Totems.

going deep… by Career Girls

? Also knowing their way around the tongue-in-cheek, Auckland garage duo Dad Jokes devote their new single ?J-C-V-D? to their wish to be Jean-Claude Van Damme. (And really, why not?) Recorded and mixed by Calum Gunn, it’s a lo-fi, barebones anthem that can be downloaded for any price. Ditto the virtual B-side ?Confidence?, a self-deprecating list of character flaws. The band are also releasing a tape called Bad Friends very soon on Muzai Records.

J – C – V – D by Dad Jokes

? Street Chant?s Emily Littler continues her solo project Emily Edrosa with a new self-titled six-song tape and free download. Check out the opener and lead single ?Animal? below (and in video form here). Driven by ticking drum machine and sparkling guitar melodies, the song’s fringe-dwelling and damaged-sounding DIY pop recalls early Xiu Xiu. The tape is Littler’s second in this guise, following Body/Christ, and it’s the first release on her newfound Pasta Tapes imprint. Stream the whole thing here.
