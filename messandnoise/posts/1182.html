Track By Track: Amaya Laucirica

By Mess+Noise 2014-05-11T14:00:00+00:00

AMAYA LAUCIRICA?s third album is palpably dreamy, as the Melbourne singer, songwriter and multi-instrumentalist underscores in her commentary.

?Three Heart Delay?

This song felt like the starting point for the album after we demoed it. I was just messing around with the drum machine on my Casio keyboard, putting it through a delay pedal, and coming up with a simple loop that the song built upon. The lyrics came from some notes I had written from a dream sequence I experienced, and the reoccurring images are echoed in the repetitive nature of the lyrics. It’s quite a simple song that became a lot bigger and wider in the studio.

?Found Some Secret?

I wrote this song at Cape Liptrap, which is an area in South Gippsland where I rent a house to work on songs with the band sometimes. It revolves around just three chords for the whole song, and coupled with the mood and the melody it has quite a trancelike quality.

?Prettier Than the Sound?

I wrote this song on my acoustic guitar when I was playing around with double Drop D tunings and lots of reverb through the amp. When I was sketching lyrics, I just liked how ?Prettier Than the Sound? looked as a bunch of words put together, and the rest of the lyrics are pretty loose and abstract.

?On the Air?

When I was writing this song, what I liked about it was the eeriness of the chord changes and the melody in the chorus. And when I played it with the band, the edginess came out even more intensely. I found it easier to base the lyrics on a character rather than on myself, and the lyrics are about a woman who is falling out of a relationship and falls into a more surreal and anxious state of being.

?You Only Say Goodbye?

I wrote this song one day when I was trying to figure out how to use GarageBand and was mucking around with laying down some parts. I put some vocals on top and then added the horn section. Matthew Habben and Adam Hutterer came in to lay the horn section down during the recording, and it was great to be able to record the real thing in the studio.

?Running Out of Time?

I had been mucking around a bit with the DM1 drum machine app and was trying to create interesting rhythms to sing along to. I found a melody to some chords and I just started singing the first things that came into my head without trying to think of subject matter. In the end I really like the stream-of-consciousness approach to the lyrics and the way they moved with the structure of the song, so I kept them.

?Not Been Sleeping?

I had a bunch of little demos I was playing to the band one day, and I only had a verse of this demoed. I think it was Andrew Cowie who encouraged me to finish writing it, which I did that day. It’s now one of my favourite songs to play live with the band.

?Falling all Around?

This song sounded completely different from the original demo. It was a lot slower and weirder. We decided to play it a lot faster one day and it gave the song a totally different direction.

?This Morning’s Blue?

This song revolves around the synthesiser riff that I was playing around with. The chorus is pretty much a visual interpretation of the song. I thought the song sounded like a blue-lit room in the morning light.

?Ocean Wide?

This song is quite stark when it sits alongside the other songs on the album. It nearly wasn’t going to make it on the album, but in the end I like the mood of it and all the subtleties in the arrangement. It initially had a drum machine backing track, which Andrew replicated on the drums in the studio.

?Falling Out of Time?

This is quite a hypnotic and dreamy number, which is helped by the fact it only has one line of lyrics. It’s an unusual structure as it kind of has two parts and no real chorus. Halfway through there is a chord change and then a horn section kicks in, which was improvised on the day in the studio and is one of my favourite moments on the album. I really like the pinging guitar sound that JP Shilo created on this; it really adds to the dreamlike nature of the song.
