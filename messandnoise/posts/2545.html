So Many Things

By Aaron 2011-10-21T00:00:00+00:00

It’s not exactly ?Singles Going Steady?, but Eddy Current’s new compilation of A-sides and rarities gives us a chance to reflect on their journey thus far, writes AARON CURRAN.


Eight years and three albums on from their earliest beginnings, Melbourne four-piece Eddy Current Suppression Ring have become a flagship act for Australian independent music, resolutely following their own path into fans? hearts, larger venues, and – somewhat surprisingly – towards the top of charts, music polls and industry prizes.

The band’s approach is rudimentary but effective: a melodic rush of over-driven garage rock backs Brendan Suppression’s sung/spoken lyrics which he delivers in a distinctly-subjective, doggedly-colloquial style, like he’s reciting excerpts from a diary, a letter or a list. The result is a thrilling amalgam of gonzoid-riffing, tightly-wound rhythms and lyrical observations that veer wildly from the disarmingly honest to utterly mundane, sometimes in the same song.

The release of So Many Things*, a career-spanning collection of 22 singles and non-album rarities, has come while the band is on an indefinite hiatus. While many listeners may be more interested in where ECSR can possibly take their sound next, given the tentative steps towards experimentation and diversity shown on last year’s Rush to Relax, for now we can but reflect on where they’ve been and how enjoyable it has been to follow.

The band’s first two singles, ?Get Up Morning? and ?Precious Rose? have lost none of their anthemic power; the percussive surge of the latter, in particular, always gets the endorphins pumping. ?It’s All Square? is another favourite, all hypnotic drone-meets-slamdance frenzy, as is the surf-stomp of ?That Time of Day?, while the main hook of ?It Ain’t Cheap? sounds a bit like The Easybeats playing the intro to The Doors? ?Touch Me? (or is it another well-known ?60s track? It’s on the tip of my tongue.)

Eddy Current Suppression Ring – Get Up Morning by Fuse Group Australia

Three cover versions feature here, songs originally played by The Pagans, The Go-Go’s and The Chosen Few and, for my money, its this last band’s ?T.A.L.O.I.G.A? that deserves to be more widely-heard, and not just because they were the best first-wave punk band on the Mornington Peninsula (Ian McFarlane called the EP which featured ?T.A.L.O.I.G.A?, The Joke’s On Us, ?the only genuine Melbourne punk record released in 1978?). Kudos to ECSR for spreading the word about this great, short-lived local outfit.

Eddy Current Suppression Ring – We Got The Beat by Fuse Group Australia

Eddy Current Suppression Ring’s most underrated single is, I think, ?Demon’s Demands?. Moody and intense, the song features a powerful pro-union lyric and was written for the documentary Constructing Fear, about the ABCC (Australian Building Construction Workers?) alleged attack on the rights of Australian construction workers. Its flipside, ?I’m Guilty?, is another temperamental but compelling corker.

?The result is a thrilling amalgam of gonzoid-riffing, tightly-wound rhythms and lyrical observations.?

So Many Things is not a particularly cohesive album, but that would be a big ask given that the songs themselves were developed and released in such a scattershot way. It’s certainly not a ?greatest hits?, as a handful of the tracks are quite ragged and fairly disposable; the opening title track for one, a defensive rant at an ex-lover, which is mildly funny at first but has barely enough in it to keep you listening to its end.

Still – and despite the fact that So Many Things* is more *Black Market Clash* or *Dead Letter Office* than *Singles Going Steady* or *Hatful of Hollow – it’s great to have every non-album ECSR track in the one place, given the silly prices that these orphan releases command on eBay and in secondhand record shops. Like the title promises, there are so many things on offer here and, for the most part, they are well worth hearing.

+

####?So Many Things? is out now through Fuse.
