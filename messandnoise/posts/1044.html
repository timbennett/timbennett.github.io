New Noise: Sounds Like Sunset, Old Mate, Hayden James, Pascal Babare

By Mess+Noise 2013-08-28T14:00:00+00:00

Noise, romance, lushness and sax collide in this week’s roundup of new music.

? ?I just want to tell a story,? sings David Challinor to open Sounds Like Sunset?s new single ?Fears?. But it’s not that line that gets our attention. No, that would be the crusty squall of noise that heralds the shoegaze-y gem. Close on the heels of previous single ?Open Up My Eyes?, ?Fears? catches the Sydney quartet at its best fusion of soft-sung pop and blistering textures (think early Teenage Fanclub). Recorded and mixed by Wayne Connolly, the song comes out independently tomorrow (Aug 30) and launches this weekend in Sydney before a couple more gigs in the next few weeks. Even better news: it heralds a new album ?just around the corner.?

Sat, Aug 31 – The Square, Sydney, NSW [w/Lowtide + Trent Marden Ich]
Thurs, Sept 12 – Tym Guitars, Brisbane, QLD [in-store, w/Iowa]
Sun, Sept 15 – Petersham Bowling Club, Sydney, NSW [arvo gig, w/Knievel + JR Smith]

? When he’s not otherwise occupied playing in Bitch Perfect – or covering Red Hot Chili Peppers, Joy Division and Ween – Pat Telfer write and records some original tunes as Old Mate. Just such a tune will appear soon on a self-titled 7? on Adelaide label Major Crimes. ?I Think of You? is the funny kind of song that feels dour and sweet at the same time, with that familiar lugubrious jangle but also a not-so-familiar reeling of saxophone. The 7? follows last year’s Word is Bond EP. Old Mate plays next on Friday, September 20 at the Marrickville Bowling Club with a nuts line-up of UV Race, Dead Farmers, Housewives and Destiny 3000. More info here.

? After pillaging Leisure Suit Larry for his ?Permission to Love? clip, Sydney singer-songwriter-producer Hayden James will release his self-titled debut EP tomorrow (Aug 30) through Future Classic. Also from tomorrow, the new single ?Embrace? will be up for free download. ?Permission to Love? has amassed more than 400,000 plays on Soundcloud and, after his recent appearance at Splendour in the Grass, James could well follow in the footsteps of kindred spirit Chet Faker. More low-key compared to that previous entry, ?Embrace? lays it all on the line romantically, pleading to a departed lover via pitch-shifted vocals, squishy synths and a spot of blue-eyed-soul humming.

? Well-travelled songwriter, multi-instrumentalist and video artist Pascal Babare has shared the first two songs from his second album Sorry, Morning*, due out early next month. There’s the alternately twinkling and bristling ?Heaven Clubs? and the lusher ?Nonna?. Melbourne-based Babare, who’s also part of Batman Park and Aleks & The Ramps, recorded the album while living and trekking through Japan, Germany and the UK, utilising ?a wide variety of broken instruments I found along the way.? His debut album *Thunderclap Spring came out in 2009 on Blackmaps and earned a glowing review from the BBC. An album launch is still being worked out, but Babare will play at the Collingwood shop URA on Sunday, September 9 from 2pm with a heap of other bands: t:dy t:wns, HOY, Super XX Man, Steph Brett, Shadow Feet and Other Places. It’s only $6 entry.
