New Noise: Ghoul, Brous, Batrider, Phrase

By Darren Levin 2011-07-03T14:00:00+00:00

Our regular round-up of the best new music on the internet, featuring Ghoul, Brous, Batrider and our first hip-hop selection by Melbourne emcee Phrase.

? Ghoul’s plans of releasing a mini-album and full-length LP in the same year were always going to be a bit ambitious, so they’ve decided to hold off on the latter until at least early 2012. To tide us over, they’ve released a new track ?Lodum (Rising)? ahead of their Splendour In The Grass debut and shows in Sydney, Melbourne and Perth with DJ Shadow. As always, it features singer Ivan Vizintin’s warbling Antony Hegarty-like vocal, but it’s maybe just a bit more danceable than anything on February’s Dunks.

Ghoul – Lodum (Rising) by inertiamusic

? Melbourne has a new indie-pop diva and her name is Sophia Brous. Her first single ?Streamers? is described as a ?sugarsweet psychpop anthem replete with fuzz guitar and Komeda-esque children’s chorus?. It’s also prone to delirious tempo shifts and left-field, lounge exotica touches. The track (out July 8 through iTunes) hails from a forthcoming self-titled EP, co-produced by Scott Horscroft and Shags Chamberlain. It’ll be showcased at Sydney’s Goodgod Small Club this Thursday (July 7) and Melbourne’s The Buffalo Club on July 9, with backing from her band comprising members of Pikelet, Ned Collette + Wirewalker and Lost Animal.

Streamers by Brous

? Stripped back to a three-piece with Sarah Chadwick on guitar/vocals, Sam Featherstone on bass and Stephanie Crase on drums, Batrider sound utterly visceral on new single ?Piles of Lies?. It’s the title track from their fourth studio album, which is set for release on July 15 on new label Two Bright Lakes via Remote Control. The follow-up to 2009?s Why Can’t We Be Together*, *Piles of Lies was recorded with Hit The Jackpot’s Kynan Lawlor in Adelaide, where the band now reside (they formed in Wellington, but have spent time in Melbourne and London).

Batrider – Piles of Lies by Mess+Noise

? Like Drapht’s ?Rapunzel?, Melbourne emcee Phrase (aka Harley Webster) has cooked up an excellent crossover single of his own in ?Apart?, a garage-inspired stomper about a chaotic relationship with vocals courtesy of You Am I’s Davey Lane (that’s him in the chorus). It’s the first single from Phrase’s forthcoming third album Babylon, which reportedly ?throws down the gauntlet to any musician feeling hemmed in by a scene and longing to paint a bigger picture?. It’s due for release in August through Universal Music Australia.
