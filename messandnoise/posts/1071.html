New Noise: Jonti/Big Scary, Popstrangers, Nimble Animal, Evening Hiss

By Mess+Noise 2013-10-02T14:00:00+00:00

Dreamy beats, layers and effects dominate this week’s roundup of new music.

? Sydney beatmaker Jonti* has helped **Big Scary bring out more of the hip-hop influence teased across the Melbourne duo’s second album *Not Art. In a collaboration sponsored by Adidas that’s the first in a series of three Aussie and/or NZ team-ups, they made the free exclusive track ?Slumming It in Paradise?, which straddles breathy dream-pop and piano-bouncy production. For all the lightness of Jo Syme’s vocals and Tom Iansek’s near-rapped delivery (plus that spongy atmosphere), there’s an awful lot going on. Let your ears pick it apart on repeated listens, and check out a quick behind-the-scenes (http://www.youtube.com/watch’v=iAGBpb9kqrs).

? Since releasing this year’s standout debut LP Antipodes, Auckland trio Popstrangers have relocated to London, where they recorded their new 7? in ?an old lightship moored on the River Thames.? It was produced and mixed by Rory Attwell (Warm Brains, Test Icicles) over a single weekend, pairing the hummable pop single ?Rats in the Palm Trees? with the B-side ?Fortuna?. These guys continue to nail the effects-clogged underbelly of ?90s alt-rock, with plenty of frisky melodies amid the noisy tinkering. The 7? comes out November 5 through their US label, Carpark.

? Dominic Stephens has covered a lot of ground between Oh Ye Denver Birds and Outerwaves, with Nimble Animal remaining a more free-form outlet compared to those indie and electronic impulses. Following last year’s self-produced debut Dumb Dirge*, Stephens has just released the name-your-price EP *Bleak Moments through Feral Media. It’s a gorgeous listen, from the shoegazing opener ?Ghillie Forever? and shape-shifting ?The Sight? (its vocals contrasting with the mostly instrumental surrounds) to the pop-burnished ?Opal Fruits? and the laughing-gas chillwave of ?Back N Forth?. All six tracks are lush and melt-prone, as if they’re dissolving even as we threaten to get an actual grasp on them.

Bleak Moments by Nimble Animal

? Brisbane visual artist Daniel Ford has detoured from his solo-project-turned-band Black Vacation with, well, another solo project. Evening Hiss materialised to complement his upcoming art exhibition later this year. He’ll play live at the exhibition and share a debut EP soon as a limited tape and digital release. The first taste is ?Melt?, a groggy bit of post-punk-y pop that sounds sort of like a stripped-down sibling to The Clean’s ?Point That Thing Somewhere Else?.
