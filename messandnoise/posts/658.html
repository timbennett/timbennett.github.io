New Noise: Geoffrey O?Connor, Jessica Says, Montero, Cameras

By Darren Levin 2011-07-26T14:00:00+00:00

Our regular round-up of the best new music on the internet, featuring three projects involving The Crayon Fields? Geoffrey O’Connor, as well as some new music from Speak N Spell signings Cameras.

? Geoffrey O’Connor sounds as cool as ice on new single ?Whatever Leads Me To You?, a synth-driven power balled (one of several we’re told) from new album Vanity Is Forever. Two years in the making, the album is the Crayon Fields frontman’s first under his own name. It was recorded at Head Gap by Neil Thomason, Audrey Studios by Craig Pilkington, and Dryburgh St by O’Connor himself, and will be out in late September through Chapter Music.

Geoffrey O’Connor – Whatever Leads Me To You by ChapterMusic

? Speaking of O’Connor, he’s lent his well-honed production talents to the new single from Jessica Says (aka Jessica Venables). ‘Viens Viens’, a reworking of Marie Laforet’s 1973 ballad sung in French, is the first new music from the Melbourne singer/cellist since divisive 2010 single ‘World Without Men’. Venables reportedly fell in love with the song after a friend sang it to her in hospital last year. (She was recovering after falling four metres out a first floor window onto a Sydney footpath – read about her long road to recovery here.)

Jessica Says “Viens Viens” by Jessica Says

? Bjenny Montero – alumni of gone, but not forgotten Melbourne indie-pop act Treetops, as well as the short-lived Brutals – has formed a new eponymous, soft rock supergroup comprising Guy Blackman (Chapter Music), Cameron Potts (Baseball, Cuba Is Japan), Bobby Fresh Bravington (Cuba Is Japan), Gerald Wells (Baptism Of Uzi) and, in keeping with the theme of this week’s column, Geoffrey O’Connor. Described as a ?schmaltz-prog anthem for tomorrow?, ?Rainman? (the a-side of Montero’s debut 7?) features guest appearances by Jon Michell (The Ancients, Mum Smokes) and Mathew Brown (Zond). It’ll be out in early September, with cover art by Montero and Sonny Smith from San Francisco’s Sonny & the Sunsets.

Montero – Rainman by Mistletone

? The new track by Sydney’s Cameras occupies a dark pop space left void by Bridezilla’s indefinite hiatus and Howling Bells? decampment to London. The band recently inked a deal with Speak N Spell, which will see the release of their debut album in late 2011. They formed in 2008 after Eleanor Dunlop (vocals, keys) and Fraser Harvey (vocals, guitars) discovered ?a mutual love for layering darker dirtier sounds with warmer delicate melodies? – or so we’re told.

I Know by CAMERAS
