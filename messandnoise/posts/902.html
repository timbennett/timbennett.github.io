Track By Track: Love Migrate

By Doug Wallen 2012-11-19T13:00:00+00:00

Songs about moving from Alice Springs to Melbourne and Cormac McCarthy-inspired lyrics factor into Love Migrate’s debut album, ?Plagued Are All My Thoughts, Like White Ants In The Fence?, says singer EDDIE ALEXANDER.

?What Kind Of Man Have I Become??

This song started as a poem written in April 2010 after reading The Road by Cormac McCarthy. It was about another 14 months until we began working on it. Although the lyrical content is quite bleak, the horns and guitar line at the end show some optimism. In a way it thematically sets the foundations for the rest of the album’s mood and is probably my favourite song on the record.

?Head In The Clouds?

An extension of the first track and a somewhat segue into ?Plagued Are All My Thoughts?. The lyrics were a snippet of the poem that didn’t quite fit into ?What Kind of Man??. We were kind of going for a sound similar to songs by J’nsi & Alex. It’s a nice change after the intensity of the opening track and sonically it fits the lyrics lying underneath it.

?Plagued Are All My Thoughts?

This song kind of represents a na’ve and skewed view of Melbourne in the midst of a freezing winter and the dream of being somewhere warmer, by the sea or in another country. As a child growing up in Alice Springs, images of Melbourne filled my mind through a book [by Robert Dickins] called Carbothello the Dragon, who seemed to have a similar opinion … I think. Casey [Hartnett] and Joe [Walker]’s vocals in the middle section completely take the track to a different place.

?Making This Hard?

We got Deniliquin local Teresa Duffy-Richards to sing on this one, as well as ?Dirty River? and ?Galah Love?. She gave the songs a completely different perspective, which was great. This song took a while to get right, in particular the beds, which were recorded live. A few people have said it sounds like Fleetwood Mac, but we were going for more of a Bruce [Springsteen] Darkness [on the Edge of Town] vibe.

?Papa?

For my beloved grandfather. Vocals and pump organ were recorded in one take, then guitar, drums and horns overdubbed on separate occasions. Couldn’t have a song about my papa without horns. He is in love with Harry James, who we’ve constantly listened to over the years.

?Little Kid?

?Little Kid? provides a bit more of an upbeat moment in between the slower, softer songs. I wrote the song in Coles Bay in February 2011 and have had a few different versions kicking around. For the album we thought it would be fun to redo it with a bit more grit in a live setting. We also roped in Liam [McGorry] and Emily [Mould] (Eagle & The Worm) to play horns, which really helped lift the bridge section.

?Dirty River?

This is another song about moving from Alice Springs to Melbourne and yearning for that comfort of your hometown. Most of the other guys grew up in small towns, so I guess we can all relate to that feeling of being alone and stuck in the city. It is one of my oldest songs but has somehow managed to stick around over the years. Joe does some really nice E-bow towards the end, which is coupled nicely with the build-up of vocals in the last chorus.

?Galah Love?

Really happy with how this song turned out. In the days leading up to recording, we still weren’t completely happy with the arrangements or how it was sounding. We ended up recording a much softer and dynamic version with added harmonica in the chorus. I think that those aspects, along with Joe’s slide guitar and Teresa’s vocals, really make the song stand out.

?I Want You To Mend?

This track is essentially a different version of ?Papa?. We tried jamming it as a band and it ended up having a completely different feel. The lyrics in this version are a bit more optimistic compared to the original, especially towards the end, coupled with the trumpet and trombone. It was never intended to be on the record but I think adds a really nice uplifting finish.

+

##?Plagued Are All My Thoughts, Like White Ants In The Fence? is out now on Flightless/Gaga Digi. Love Migrate will launch the album on Sunday, December 2 at The Toff in Melbourne.
