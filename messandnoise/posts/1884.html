Astral Travel

By Patrick 2009-01-30T00:00:00+00:00

Astral travel was once – and maybe still is – a concept associated with those beholden to the flaky idiosyncrasies of the New Age movement. Close your eyes and travel to the stars – oh, and give us some of your middle-class cash, and you’ll be on the journey sooner. Unfortunately, the soundtrack isn’t always that promising. Why the fuck would you want to travel to the stars with an elevator music incarnation of Enya?

There’s nothing flaky or New Age-ish about Astral Travel, thankfully. There’s no song titles listed on their debut 7?, an omission that’s presumably conscious and possibly part of a considered strategy to compel attention to the general musical aesthetic, rather than its discursive representation. The a-side is a lo-fi track replete with delicate pop melodies that would be perfectly home on a Calvin Johnson mixtape. As the song progresses, the tempo changes and there’s a frenzy that threatens to explode like Midnight Movies, but fades diplomatically into the distance.

The b-side is a live recording. A throwback to those crazy days of analogue bootlegs, its fidelity is more reminiscent of something captured on a battered tape recorder shoved into a beer-stained jacket than that allowed by the digital majesty of today. That said, it’s appropriate for – if not always forgiving of – the early Sleater-Kinney pop-punk style presented by Astral Travel.

More, please.
