Home Recordings Vol. 1

By ReneSchaefer 2009-02-22T00:00:00+00:00

Josh Armistead is a bit of a maverick really. For nigh on a decade this eclectic Melbourne songwriter has been performing and recording under his own name. For his third full-length album Armistead has decided to adopt a new name for no reason other than that it will allow him to perform his new songs with a group of musicians in the future.

That’s right – Team Yes! doesn’t exist yet. Josh plays all the instruments on this album, with only a little bit of help from friends Evelyn Morris, Tamara Lunn and Adam Phillips. What their contributions consist of is a bit unclear, but it I detect the influence of Morris’s band Pikelet in the subtle use of looped guitar, percussion and electronic sounds that permeate this album. Layered backing vocals flesh out the sparse sonic palette.

Mostly, these home recordings were executed on 4-track or laptop, resulting in an intimacy and simplicity that perfectly suits the understated nature of Armistead’s warm acoustic guitar picking and honeyed voice. It’s as perfect a collection of bedroom pop songs as you’re likely to encounter and firmly cements Armistead’s reputation as one of the local indie scene’s brightest sparks.

The songs possess a similar folky quirkiness to those of Extreme Wheeze (aka Joe Foley of Aleks And The Ramps), and it comes as no surprise that Armistead’s friend has been recruited as a future member of Team Yes! The two artists share a similar penchant for laid-back, atmospheric arrangements and know how to craft memorable melodies.

As a songwriter, Armistead celebrates the unguarded moments and simple pleasures of life; a kind of mythical inner-suburban landscape where hazy summer afternoons invite lazy reveries. Luckily, he avoids the clumsy twee-ness that is often associated with the introspective end of the indie-pop spectrum. Instead, he has crafted a collection of minimalist masterpieces that make for perfectly absorbing listening, as you stretch out on a banana lounge in the backyard with a glass of lemonade in hand and a kitten curled up on your lap.
