New Noise: Two Cartoons, Hollow Press, The Aves, Super Magic Hats

By Doug Wallen 2012-11-21T13:00:00+00:00

This week’s roundup of Soundcloud offerings range from hooky guitar gems to instrumentals full of airiness and unease.

? Otago University students Isaac McFarlane and Brad Craig collide as Dunedin guitar-drums duo Two Cartoons. Their early influences (and everything’s early at this point) include Best Coast and Wavves, but their songs feel a bit more classic than that. They’re loose and hooky, keeping things ever punchy even in their daydream-y, lackadaisical way. ?Tiny Terrors? is the title track of their name-your-price second EP. The boys are coming to Australia to play Victoria’s Inca Roads Music Festival next Friday (Nov 30), and may also play Melbourne closer to Christmas.

Tiny Terrors by Two Cartoons

? Adelaide’s Shaun McNamara makes music all on his lonesome as Hollow Press, named for a track by Grouper. Following this year’s Empathy* EP and album *Fleeting Joy* comes the free single ?Floating? from the affordably priced album *Between Us, out digitally and on limited CD-R through Drug Arts. ?Floating? is a fixating listen, relishing open space while layering snatches of uneasy ambience and rhythmic stirrings.

Floating by Hollow Press

? ?In Vogue? sits smack in the middle of The Aves? three-song EP Anywhere Else, recorded in Brooklyn while the Adelaide power-pop quartet was touring the US. The band have already toured Canada and played London too, which is no small feat for a young act. ?In Vogue? is a bitter little barb about waiting patiently to be considered in fashion, with Lucy Campbell’s snide vocal delivery clinching it. The EP, their second, is out now on Mirador.

The Aves – In Vogue by Mirador Records

? Melbourne producer Super Magic Hats has a knack for perfectly airy, harmony-stuttered instrumentals, as proven by September’s ?By Myself? and the new free single ?Wind?. (He’s even applied his method to a Middle East song.) The previous track ?Charcoal? premiered on XLR8R, while ?Wind? comes from a debut EP out early next year. Super Magic Hats is also a live concern, having already supported Sydney’s RUFUS and Ghostly International artist Shigeto. Like Two Cartoons above, he’s also appearing at Inca Roads.

Wind by supermagichats
