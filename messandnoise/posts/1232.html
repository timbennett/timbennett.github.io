Track by Track: Shovels

By Mess+Noise 2014-08-03T14:00:00+00:00

Split between Melbourne and San Francisco, and formerly known as Electric Jellyfish, ADAM CAMILLERI*, **MICHAEL BEACH** and *PETER WARDEN make up the caustic rock trio Shovels, who have just released a self-titled LP.

?MB Jacket?

Mike: I once traded someone guitar lessons for clothing as an attempted reconciliation following a badly failed relationship. I taught her Edith Piaf songs, and she made me a bright red jacket with my initials [MB] hot-glued to the back in large black letters. I don’t wear it much.

Adam: We started a tradition in San Francisco where we would put the MB jacket onto one of the band members when they passed out on the tour. I think I wore it that night when I fell asleep in Mike’s closet.

?Multiple Furrow?

Adam: Our mate is about to become a dad soon, so the title is loosely inspired by us imagining him assisting his partner through a water birth. Apparently he has to get into the tub, and the nurse went out of her way to tell him that he has to keep his jocks on.

Pete: I think partners in water births should wear wetsuits and goggles.

?Car Yards?

Adam: Pete and I jammed on this track while Mike was in exile in the USA. We emailed it over to him and he sent the track back with guitar on it that he had recorded while drunk. We tried to replicate this when we were finally all in the same room together, and this was the result.

Pete: That’s always the challenge, isn’t it? Sometimes your guitar line is too sober and your drums are too drunk. We try to democratically ration our wooziness between instruments.

Mike: I came home after a big night to an email from Pete with this track. It was a really blown-out Les Rallizes D’nud?s-style recording of the drum beat. I vaguely remember thinking, ?What would Karl E. Scullin play?? That guitar part was the first thing that come out.

?Grenoble?

Adam: Things start to get a bit free-form here, with us sounding like we have had too much French red wine.

Pete: This is a driving song. When we toured Europe we had a van named Geraldine. Geraldine was a tank of almost military proportions. Of course we thought it was badarse driving along the Autobahn, but things got a little dicey when we hit the streets of the provincial town Grenoble, France, where the roads are designed for horses not military vehicles. Geraldine was attacked by several bollards in Spain. We used women’s makeup to hide the damage and increase her self-esteem.

?Clyde?

Adam: This is a story of a man who long ago lost touch with reality, based on a guy we met at a servo in upstate New York.

Pete: Yeah, it’s a meditation on the intersection of paranoia, mysticism and misogyny. It’s an ode to making friends at petrol stations. Adam, you may be surprised to know that I was listening to a lot of reggae when I wrote the drums on this one.

Mike: Clyde was the sort of man that you only meet in the USA. Extremely self-assured, loud, charismatic, evangelical and delusional, though most likely a normal functioning citizen. He may as well have been mayor of the town where we met him. His art show, Clyde’s War on God, was held at the local Days Inn. Guests included ?the leader of the FBI.?

?Alternator Debt?

Pete: This is another driving song. I once owned a car named Larry. Larry was a real bone rattler. I have a lengthy commute to work and Larry would often unceremoniously shed parts of himself along the road. You know the sort: the doors don’t open, he vomits coolant and you need to get passengers to hold their own mirrors out the window as you drive. Larry needed multiple operations to stay seaworthy; he was basically a hole that you throw money in, hence the alternator debt. I reckon this song kind of sounds like Larry as he coughs smoke and splutters down the road.

?Arm Arm Leg?

Adam: We played a live-to-air as Electric Jellyfish] on WFMU in Austin, Texas, during SXSW a few years ago. We had this song but it didn’t have a name. We had to write something down so we wrote down ?Arm Leg Leg Arm Head?, which was an obscure [Wu-Tang reference. It eventually evolved into this, and doesn’t sound much like Wu-Tang. We had played in Texas one other time and spent some time in a jail cell, so this was quite a pleasant trip in comparison.

Mike: The track title was shortened to ?Arm Leg Leg? after Pete expressed concerns that the original title might get us in hot water with the Nation of Islam. When Weston-o [Bob Weston] was mastering the album, he started calling it ?Arm Arm Leg?, so we stuck with that.

Pete: Oh, I thought this song was about trench warfare…

?Expire?

Pete: This is my favourite song on the album. We tried to record this percussion-heavy track with pots and pans, but it bottomed out badly and never made it to the album. Adam had particular investment in the percussion track – after it got cut he was emotionally damaged. He channeled his disappointment into that bummer of a bass line.

Mike: I really like this track as well. It brings back vivid memories of late nights in Paul Maybury’s studio, with red wine, Adam having a sook and an obscene amount of ciggies. Dope bass line, Camo.

+

####?Shovels? is out now on limited cassette independently and on limited vinyl and digital through Homeless Records.
