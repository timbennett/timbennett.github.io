Best Of The Rest: 2011

By Darren Levin 2011-07-20T23:00:00+00:00

It’s been a vintage year for Australian music so far (check out our critics? mid-year report if you’re not convinced), but there’s still plenty left in the tank for the remainder of 2011. The list below is likely to fluctuate and is by no means exhaustive, so feel free to add any omissions in the comments section below. (If we’re feeling generous we might even put them up.) In the interests of symmetry, we’ve decided to include the entire month of July.

July

What: Future Universe by Ron S. Peno and The Superstitions.
When: July 8 through Fuse Music.
Notes: Debut solo album for Died Pretty frontman Peno after striking up a creative partnership with guitarist and composer Cam Butler (Silver Ray). Described by Peno himself as his best work since Died Pretty’s definitive Doughboy Hollow.

What: Don’t Hold Back by Donny Ben’t.
When: July 8 through Rice Is Nice.
Notes: Debut solo album for Sydney-based solo artist, the son of famous Italian disco accordionist Antonio Giacomelli Ben’t and sometime member of Jack Ladder and the Dreamlanders. Written, produced and recorded by Ben’t at home studio ?Donny Land?. Features first single ?Don’t Hold Back?.

What: Lotus Skies by Leader Cheetah.
When: July 15 through Spunk!.
Notes: Second album for the Adelaide four-piece, following 2009?s The Sunspot Letters. Recorded in late 2010 in Sydney with producer Scott Horscroft (The Panics, Little Red, The Sleepy Jackson), whose method was ?all about capturing the essence of the song without clutter?. Features first single ?Crawling Up A Landslide? and guest vocals from Holly Throsby on ?Our Love?.

What: Charisma Weapon by The Panda Band.
When: July 15 on the band’s Bam*Boo Records via MGM.
Notes: Follow-up to 2006?s This Vital Chapter* by the Perth genre-hoppers. Written and recorded in their own home studio and mixed by Magoo (Regurgitator, Operator Please). ?I try to hone in on specific sounds for songs,? singer Damian Crosbie told *M+N in a soon-to-be-published interview, ?but I just get bored with 11 songs of guitar, drums, and bass. I end up writing on instruments I don’t know.?

What: Piles of Lies by Batrider.
When: July 15 on Two Bright Lakes via Remote Control.
Notes: Fourth studio album for the newly minted three-piece: Sarah Chadwick on guitar/vocals, Sam Featherstone on bass and Stephanie Crase on drums. Follows 2009?s Why Can’t We Be Together. Recorded with Hit The Jackpot’s Kynan Lawlor in Adelaide, where the band now reside.

Batrider – Piles of Lies by Mess+Noise

What: Stories With the Endings Changed by Bart & Friends.
When: July 15 through Lost and Lonesome.
Notes: Follow-up to last year’s Make You Blush by the iconic singer behind The Cat’s Miaow, Girl of the World, Hydroplane and Pencil Tin. Features Louis Richter and Mark Monnone of The Lucksmiths, Jeremy Cole (The Zebras) and Scott Stevens (Summer Cats, Earthmen).

What: Livin? Evil by Slug Guts.
When: July 21 through Negative Guest List.
Notes: Recorded live at the Globe Theatre in their native Brisbane in late 2010, Livin’ Evil* features mostly new tracks from the group’s upcoming third LP (the follow-up to this year’s ?assured?*Howlin’ Gang), as well as a cover of Lubricated Goat’s ?In the Raw?.

What: Moods on the Moove by Panel of Judges.
When: July 22 through Mistletone Records/Inertia. (Vinyl only.)
Notes: Follow-up to 2008?s Bad Vibrations, which we described as “their most fully-realised (release) to date?. Features new-ish member Martin Frawley (The Twerps) on guitar. Recorded by Jack Farley at Headgap Studio in Melbourne. Full review here.

Panel of Judges – Dainty Vagabond by Mistletone

What: Rain On The Humming Wire by The Panics.
When: July 29 through Dew Process.
Notes: Fourth album for the Melbourne-based, Perth-born five-piece, following 2007?s ARIA-winning Cruel Guards. Written on one side of the Atlantic (Salford, England) and recorded on the other (Woodstock, New York) with producer John O’Mahony (Metric, Alberta Cross, Coldplay). Features first single ?Majesty?.

August

What: The Cat by Ben Salter.
When: August 5 through MGM.
Notes: Debut solo album for the Gin Club frontman. Recorded and produced by Gareth Liddiard (The Drones) and Robert F. Cranny (Sarah Blasko, 78 Saab) at Liddiard’s rural studio in Havilah, Victoria. Described as a ?strange beast – a blend of avant-garde pop, folk and rock?.

Ben Salter – The Coward by Mess+Noise

What: Psychic Civil War by Major Chord.
When: August 5 through Other Tongues.
Notes: Third album as Major Chord for Melbourne musician Dan Flynn. Follows 2009?s The Rabbit Hole, which we described as a ?modest, off-the-cuff outing?. Features first single ?Tomorrow Night?.

Tomorrow Night by MajorChord

What: SuperHappyFunTimesBestFriends by Regurgitator.
When: August 5 through Valve/MGM.
Notes: After releasing some tracks last year, Ben Ely and Quan Yeomans holed up in their respective Melbourne dwellings in the midst of an enforced “creative blizzard” and cranked out a concept album. Self-produced and recorded, it’s Regurgitator’s seventh.

ONE DAY (free download from http://www.facebook.com/regurgitators and bandcamp)” by Regurgitrax

What: Take Your Light by Little Scout.
When: August 5 through MGM.
Notes: Debut album for the Brisbane indie-pop four-piece. Follows two EPs: 2008?s The Dead Loss* and 2009?s *Different In The Distance. Recorded and mixed by Jonathan Boulet after 12 months of pre-production, and produced by Scott Bromiley (The John Steel Singers). ?It was great to work with someone who has so many ideas,? said singer Melissa Tickle. ?Even if we thought a song was finished, Scott would always come up with another incredible idea.? Features first single ?We Are Walking Out?.

What: Moonfire by Boy & Bear.
When: August 5 through Island Records Australia.
Notes: Debut album for the Sydney alt-folk outfit, following debut EP With Emperor Antarctica. Recorded in Nashville with producer Joe Chiccarelli (The Strokes, White Stripes, The Shins). Features first single ?Feedling Line?.

What: Arches Over The Sun* by Khancoban.
When: August 12 through Departed Sounds/Other Tongues.
Notes: Second full-length album for the Melbourne five-piece. Described as a departure from the band’s alt-country and folk-influenced sound. Produced by Nick Huggins (Mick Turner, Kid Sam), and mastered by Casey Rice (Tortoise, Dirty Three). Follows 2006?s self-titled mini-album and 2008?s Limbs May Fall.

What: Making Mirrors by Gotye.
When: August 19 through Eleven.
Notes: Third album for the Melbourne-based solo artist and drummer for The Basics. Follow-up to the platinum-selling Like Drawing Blood (2006). A mix of field recording and live instrumentation recorded mostly in a “large barn”. Features ‘Eyes Wide Open’, which we described as ?compelling?, and new single ‘Somebody I Used To Know’ with vocals by Kimbra.

What: Dark Magic by Sand Pebbles.
When: August 26 through Dot Dash/Remote Control.
Key notes: Fifth album for the Melbourne psych-rock collective. Guest contributions from Dean Wareham (Galaxie 500), Britta Phillips (Luna), Tim Holmes (Death In Vegas), Will Carruthers (Spacemen 3, Spiritualized), and Malcolm McDowell. Features first single ‘Occupied Europe [Take Me Across The Water]’, a “love song from a male perspective”.

What: White Heat: 30 Hits by Icehouse.
When: August 26 through Universal Music.
Notes: Compilation of every single the band has released (either as Icehouse or Flowers) worldwide. Comprises a three-disc set containing two audio CDs, each with 15 songs in chronological order of their release, including ?Great Southern Land?, ?Hey, Little Girl?, ?Crazy? and ?Electric Blue?, and a DVD with 32 film clips from over the years.

What: The View Is Worth The Climb by Tim Finn.
When: August 26 through Universal Music Australia.
Notes: Recorded in late 2010 at Neil Finn’s Roundhead Studios in Auckland with Grammy Award-winning US producer Jacquire King (Kings Of Leon, Tom Waits) and guest musicians Joey Waronker (Beck, REM), Zac Rae (Macy Gray, Fiona Apple), Brett Adams, Tony Buchen (Andy Bull) and Mara TK (Electric Wire Hustle). The title track is a co-write with Megan Washington.

What: Yearlings by Dead Letter Chorus.
When: August 26 through ABC Music.
Notes: Second album for Melbourne indie-pop outfit (not to be confused with Brisbane bogan prog act Dead Little Circus). Recorded between May 2010 and January this year with Canadian producer Les Cooper, and underpinned by the ?sublime vocal harmonising? of Cameron Potts (not to be confused with Cameron Potts from Baseball/Cuba Is Japan) and Gabrielle Huber. Features first single ?Yellow House?.

What: Palimpsest by Essendon Airport.
When: August 27 through Chapter Music.
Notes: Reissue of the 1982 debut by the legendary Melbourne post-punk outfit, which began as a duo in 1978. Includes a bonus disc of material featuring recordings from their final concert at the Crystal Ballroom, which was broadcast live-to-air on Triple R in 1983.

September

What: Vows by Kimbra.
**When: September 2 through Warner Brothers Records.
Notes: Debut LP from 21-year-old New Zealand born songstress Kimbra, who now calls Melbourne home. Recorded at Sing Sing Studios Melbourne and produced by Kimbra Johnson, Francois Tetaz (Architecture in Helsinki, Gotye) with additional production from M-Phazes (Amerie, Pharoahe Monche) and mastered at The Exchange London. Features singles ‘Settle Down’ and ‘Cameo Lover.’

What: A fool who’ll by Laura Jean.
When: September 3 through Chapter Music.
Notes: Third album for the Melbourne-based singer, following Our Swan Song* (2006) and *Eden Land (2008). Recorded in Melbourne with Simon Grounds (Teeth & Tongue, Kes Band), and mixed in Canada with Darryl Neudorf (Neko Case, New Pornographers). Features longtime collaborators Biddy Connor and Jen Sholakis and guest contributions from Paddy Mann (aka Grand Salvo) and Magic Silver White’s Jojo Petrina and Monica Sonand.

Missing you by Laura Jean

What: WORK (work, work) by HTRK.
When: September 9 through Mistletone Records/Inertia.
Notes: Second album for Melbourne-born, Europe-based duo, following protracted debut Marry Me Tonight (2009), which was produced by the late Rowland S Howard. Written from 2006-10 while the band were living in Berlin and London. Production finished while grieving the sudden loss of founding member and bassist Sean Stewart to suicide in March 2010. Revolves around themes of ?submission, dysphoria, sentimentality, tech-noir and corporate life?.

Eat Yr Heart – HTRK by Mess+Noise

What: The Loudest Engine by Howling Bells.
When: September 9 through Shock Records/Cooking Vinyl.
Notes: Third album for the Sydney-born, London-based four-piece (formerly known as Waikiki). The follow-up to 2009’s Radio Wars, it was recorded in Las Vegas with The Killers bassist Mark Stoermer producing. Singer Juanita Stein: ?There’s a singular sound which is more folk and rock than our last two albums, but it’s interpreted in a modern sense. It wasn’t a conscious effort to make a ’70s tinged record. It was a very natural progression.?

What: Prisoner by The Jezabels.
When: September 16 via MGM.
Notes: Debut album for the Sydney-based quartet. Recorded mostly in The Attic Studio in Sydney with producer Lachlan Mitchell, the man behind their first three EPs. Mixed by Peter Katis (Jonsi, The National, Interpol) at Tarquin Studios, Connecticut and mastered by Ted Jensen at Sterling Sound, New York. Features first single ?Endless Summer?.

Endless Summer by thejezabels

What: Vanity Is Forever by Geoffrey O’Connor.
When: September 24 through Chapter Music.
Notes: First album under his own name by Geoffrey O’Connor, frontman of Melbourne’s Crayon Fields, who’s previously released solo records under the moniker Sly Hats. Recorded at Head Gap by Neil Thomason, Audrey Studios by Craig Pilkington, and Dryburgh St by O’Connor. Mixed by Matt Voigt and Lachlan Carrick. Features first single ?Whatever Leads Me To You?.

Now And Then by Geoffrey O’Connor

What: Sketches by Wintercoats.
When: September 30 through Mistletone.
Notes: Solo dream pop project for Melbourne multi-instrumentalist James Wallace. Sketches EP will be released on 12? vinyl and digital.

What: Ex-Tropical by Lost Animal.
When: TBA through Sensory Projects.
Notes: Debut album as Lost Animal for former St Helens frontman Jarrod Quarrell. Produced by Quarrell, along with Shags Chamberlain (Pets With Pets, Brous, Pikelet) and John Lee (Mountains in the sky, Mystery Twin), and features programmed beats, horns and percussion, but very little guitar. ?I still love dirty guitar music and always will, but I’ve always done that and am just trying a different approach to constructing the songs,? Quarrell told us in an interview last year.

October

What: Howling At All Hours by The Cannanes.
When: October 1 through Chapter Music.
Notes: First album for the legendary Sydney outfit since 2002.

What: Twirligig by Jonti.
When: October 7 through Mistletone.
Notes: Debut album for multi-instrumentalist, arranger, producer and vocalist Jonti (formerly known as Danimals and later Djanimals), who recently became the first Australian artist to sign to US label Stones Throw. Entirely self-recorded and produced, drawing inspiration from Madlib, Stereolab, Free Design, The Beach Boys and cartoonist/filmmaker Norman Mclaren. Will be followed by a companion album, TokoRats, in 2012.

What: New Start Again by Dick Diver.
When: October 8 through Chapter Music.
Notes: Debut for the Melbourne fuzzy pop quartet following 2009?s Ark’s Up EP, which we described as ?an early signpost on the path to greatness?.

What: The Twerps by The Twerps.
When: October 8 through Chapter Music.
Notes: Self-titled debut full-length for the Melbourne-based four-piece. Recorded at Head Gap with Jack Farley (Beaches, Scott & Charlene’s Wedding), mastered by Brent Punshon (ex-Little Ugly Girls, Smog, Slits). Co-released on CD and vinyl with US label Underwater Peoples.

What: Free All The Monsters by The Bats.
When: October 14 through Mistletone.
Notes: New album for the Flying Nun legends. Follows 2009?s The Guilty Office, which we described as ?as fine a collection of songs from the pen of Robert Scott as there ever was?. Produced by Dale Cotton. Will be launched in Melbourne and Sydney in October.

What: TBA by Big Scary.
When: TBA.
Notes: As-yet-untitled debut for the ridiculously prolific Tom Iansek and Jo Syme. Follows seasonal project The Big Scary Four Seasons* and Iansek’s solo side project as Dads, *Man of Leisure. Promises ?elements of garage, piano driven pop and moody balladry?. Features first single ?Mix Tape?.

November And Beyond?

Who: Vince Giarrusso
What we know: Solo debut for Underground Lovers frontman. Recorded under the moniker Vincey Presents with producer and collaborator Jed Palmer.

Who: The Orbweavers
What we know: Band founders Marita Dyson and Stuart Flanagan have spent the Melbourne winter recording their forthcoming album Loom, which promises songs about ?creeks and quarries, dangerous needlework, basalt foundations, rising tide and greyhounds?.

Who: The Avalanches
What we know: Ten-year anniversary re-issue of Since I Left You. Features a bonus disc of material including remixes from the original album campaign; new reworks from the likes of El Guincho, MF Doom, Canyons, Black Dice, Jackson and his Computer Band; Avalanches remixes of other artists; plus unreleased early b-sides, demo tracks and other rarities.

The Avalanches – Since I Left You (Stereolab Remix) by modularpeople

Who: Lanie Lane
What we know: Debut album for the Sydney-based singer, who recently signed a deal with Ivy League records (Cloud Control, Sparkadia).

Who: Wolf & Cub
What we know: Follow-up to 2009?s divisive Science & Sorcery. Recording began at BJB Studios in May with producer de jour Burke Reid (The Drones, Jack Ladder) manning the desks. Frontman Joel Byrne said it’s the band’s ?best work to date?. Studio pics here.

Who: Brous
What we know: Debut self-titled EP for Melbourne-based singer. Co-produced by Scott Horscroft and Shags Chamberlain. Features ?orchestral flourishes, grandiose choirs, and timeless melodies?, as well as first single ?Steamers?.

Who: Witch Hats
What we know: Tentatively titled Enchantment Under The Sea Dance. Recorded over seven days in mid-March at Head Gap Studios in the Melbourne suburb of Preston with producer/engineer Casey Rice (The Dirty Three, Tortoise). ?The album is more song focused then the noise from the past few recordings, but still packs the dirge in on most tracks,? bass player Ash Buscombe told M+N.? Studio pics here.

Who: The Mark of Cain
What we know: Fifth album Songs Of The Third & Fifth* is slated for release in late 2011. Recorded at Adelaide’s Broadcast Studio in 2008 after brothers John and Kim Scott regrouped with drummer John Stanier, who had recently completed a national Big Day Out tour with Battles. First album since 2001?s Andy Gill-produced *This Is This.

Who: Knievel
What we know:* New album for the Wayne Connolly-fronted outfit, Through the Rainbow Dark is scheduled for release in early 2012. Features first single ?They Listen Out?.

Who: Underground Lovers
What we know: The band has teamed up again with Sydney producer Wayne Connolly, who recorded the Underground Lovers classics Leaves Me Blind* (1992), *Rushall Station* (1996) and *Dream it Down* (1994). ?Undies have written about 11 new songs and have done some basic tracking of about five new tracks,? guitarist Glenn Bennie told *M+N. Mid-2012 release planned.
