Oblako Lodka

By Doug Wallen 2011-05-01T00:00:00+00:00

Oblako Lodka announces its bedroom pop meekness right away, draping lead track ?Wet Red? in drowsy melodica, keyboard, and acoustic guitar. But something’s missing: words. The Melbourne quartet sings lyrics for just a few songs on its debut; all the other vocals are pure harmonies. That decision confirms the soundtrack-esque air of songs that are placid and minimal to the point of ambience. Averaging two minutes a piece, they drop in and out like sighing interludes.

Founded by lead singer Irit Rozenfeld with Alex Nosek (ii, Hammocks and Honey), Alexis Hall (The Motifs) and Jodi Hoffman (who also did the cover art), Oblako Lodka blend dewy atmosphere and modest pop mechanics while preserving the loveliest aspects of each. Songs glide by as if naturally occurring, and the smallest quirk of guitar or percussion becomes enough to floor us. This combination hasn’t been done much before, but it’s similar to the Japanese project Lullatone as well as The Pastels? collaboration with Tenniscoats.

Oblako Lodka – Brail Footpaths by Special Award Records

Even for the tracks with lyrics, simplicity is key. ?Here’s to Everything? and ?Left to Fight? are worthy of the classic indie pop duo The Softies. ?Brail Footpaths? is darker and almost psychedelic, inspiring a free-download remix by Ayumu. Almost by genre requirement, the jaunty ?Sleep? has endearing lyrics about a crush. The instrumentals, meanwhile, are all cut from the same cloth, whether it’s the vocal drifts of ?NYE Ideas? or the plinking afterword of the typo-titled ?Juat Walk?.

Limited to a vinyl run of 250 – plus downloads – this album is not just gorgeous but admirably specific. Oblako Lodka aims for a very certain something and nails it.
