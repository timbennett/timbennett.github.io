Track By Track: Melodie Nelson

By Darren Levin 2011-06-29T14:00:00+00:00

Songs influenced by James Bond themes, Greek mythology and doo-wop – LIA TSAMOGLOU leads us through her debut album as Melodie Nelson, ‘Meditations On The Sun’.

?Waiting?

This was one of the first songs I wrote under the Melodie Nelson moniker. In my mind, I wanted it to sound massive. Orchestral massive. I was really influenced by the theme from James Bond ?You Only Live Twice? performed by Nancy Sinatra and later sampled by Robbie Williams. The song is about longing.

01 Waiting by Melodie Nelson

?Orpheus, She’s Gone?

My grandmother was a Greek teacher and taught my sisters and I once a week. After every lesson she would tell us a story from Greek mythology. I remember being enamored by these bizarre stories as they were even more twisted in my mind because I could only understand about 70 percent of the story. (My Greek wasn’t very good!)

Anyway, when she passed away in 2009, I started reading a lot of Greek mythology and came across the story of Orpheus and Eurydice, which in my opinion is one of the saddest stories I’ve ever read. So I guess I wrote this song for her. It has two voices both telling the story, one in first person who is Eurydice and the other in third person narrative.

02 Orpheus, She’s Gone by Melodie Nelson

?Tonight?

This song is about a girl who can’t decide whether to leave or stay with her beau. I really like the song ?I Only Have Eyes For You? – the Flamingos version – so ?Tonight? is my homage to that song and those doo-wop songs from the same era. Kell [Derrig-Hall], my partner who helped me make this record and who also makes music under The Singing Skies, tells everyone that the piano part in this song sounds like Dr. Dre. I guess Dre likes doo-wop too.

03 Tonight by Melodie Nelson

?Colours Of My Dreams?

I had the chorus of this song stuck in my head for months and would always sing it when I was brushing my hair. It’s about having psychedelic drug/cheese-induced dreams. I think a lot of the imagery in the lyrics come from my when I used to work at the Australian Museum. Although, I honestly can’t say how this song came about because I don’t remember, but a famous bass line ties it all together.

04 Colours of My Dreams by Melodie Nelson

?My Johnny?

I wrote My Johnny in three different parts. The first version of ?Johnny? was just the chorus of ?My Johnny?, which made the original song only last about 1.30. The second part of ?My Johnny? was that cascading part of the four voices singing, “Ahhhh.” Anyway, I wrote that while I was waiting in the car for Kell to buy chicken burgers and I remember thinking, “Hurry up, I want to record this idea!” The cascading ?ahhh? part was actually meant for another song, but when Kell heard this other song, he said it sounded too “Euro disco?, so this part got added onto ?Johnny?.

The last part of ?Johnny? was pretty much everything else. I wrote the verses driving in the car. The car is a good place to write music. The whole vibe of the song in my mind is like Happy Days* or *Grease*, but also weird like *Crybaby. In case you don’t know, the song is about a deceptive older man preying on an innocent girl, much like Arthur Fonzarelli.

05 My Johnny by Melodie Nelson

?Meditations On The Sun?

I used to be in a band called Moonmilk, which sounds very different to this Melodie Nelson stuff, except this song is the closest link between the two bands. I wrote the vocal part years ago when I was still in Moonmilk and living in Surry Hills [Sydney] with Kell and our friend Mark. It was a strange house that resembled a pirate ship as the walls, floor and even ceiling was made of wood and the bedrooms were underground. On a rare afternoon when no one else was home, I recorded part of this song. Sometimes you can create things that are a reaction to the environment you’re in, and I think this song is like that mainly because it was a pretty dark and creepy house.

The rest of it was written a few years later. I came up with the whistling part while I was driving home from Woy Woy [on the NSW Central Coast]. It was one of the first drives on a highway I did alone, so I was nervous and I kept whistling that line as a vocal idea for a song, but it ended up remaining as a whistling part. When I got home that night, the song was finished.

06 Meditations on the Sun by Melodie Nelson

?Hurt Me So?

I originally wrote this song when I was 19 and I used to sing it with a weird fake French/Portuguese accent, because I really liked that scene in The Party with Peter Sellers, where the French actress sings this lovely lounge song in the middle of the movie. I changed the lyrics and gave it a more doo-wop feel. The lyrics are about a girl who falls in and out of love with a guy in seven days. It has this innocence to it. I feel like I wrote it for my 19-year-old self in someway.

07 Hurt Me So by Melodie Nelson

?Bovine Blues?

I love duets even though they’re the daggiest things ever. I wrote this song one night with a couple of bottles of wine. Kell sings the man part. When I recorded the demo version, we were both quite drunk and Kell was half a beat behind every time he sung. We cut about 50 percent of his lines because the original lyrics were quite wordy, and he’s used to singing much slower than I was making him sing for this song.

The album was recorded at this beautiful 19th-century sandstone church in Wildes Meadow (close to Robertson, NSW). The neighbouring property had these cows that would gather close to the fence every afternoon. On a few occasions, Kell would go up to them and play them a song on the guitar. I named this song after this event.

08 Bovine Blues by Melodie Nelson

?This Town?

I wrote this song about growing up in a strict religious Greek Orthodox family. A lot of the girls I grew up with in this church group got married very young and have heaps of kids now. This song is about that and how male dominated most religions are. It’s a funny area of my life that I don’t really think about much anymore.

I let Tony Dup? and Robin Dixon go crazy on the string parts with this song and it ended up sounding really Greek or Russian or something. My parents love this song, although I don’t think they know what it’s about.

09 This Town by Melodie Nelson

+

####?Meditations On The Sun? is out now through Other Tongues.
