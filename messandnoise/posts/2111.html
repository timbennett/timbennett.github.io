Brunettes On The Rocks

By Patrick 2009-10-01T00:00:00+00:00

There’s a thesis – plenty of them, in fact, each with lavish amounts of government funding – to be written about the girl garage rock genre and its relationship to patriarchy, pop music and any cultural and political institution known to the brown corduroy-adorned post-structuralist academic community. There are also plenty more moments of hip shakin?, martini glass tippin?, feet shufflin? happiness to be enjoyed if you leave your poorly constructed efforts at Foucauldian de-construction at the door and just settle back and enjoy the genre for its raw musical attraction.

And that’s largely the context within which The Shimmys should be appreciated. Brunettes on the Rocks* is the second Shimmys LP following 2007?s *Drive You Wild. Joined by guest players Micky 66 (Breadmakers, Shutdown 66) and Evan Millar (Hands of Time, Lords of Gravity), the album is devoid of any pretence – an omission that’s always beneficial in these dark days of PR discourse and carefully crafted media spin.

On ?Doctor Doctor?, Suzi Watusi’s voice sneers with the attitude of a frog (female bogan, for those not versed in suburban Adelaide vernacular) on a mission; ?(He’s My) Werewolf? is a three-chord special with the obligatory B-grade cinematic theme; ?Walkin? Out On You? is Zombies troglodyte garage rock with a dash of Californian surf; and while the lyrics of ?Two Pot Screamer? won’t mean shit outside Melbourne, everyone from Curl Curl to San Diego through to Tacoma will understand its snarling beachside garage simplicity.

In true ?60s fashion, there are a bunch of covers, including Roky Erickson’s ?Don’t Slander Me? and The Sonics? ?Psycho?, each of which are given an appropriately sassy edge.

There are moments where the songs are as sloppy as the pseudo-intellectual musings proffered by an average first-year student to win over the hot chick from his sociology class. But while that well-worn scenario is painful to watch – and even worse to remember – Brunettes on the Rocks is, simply, a lot of fun.
