Excerpt: The World According To Gaz

By andyr 2010-10-05T13:00:00+00:00

In a time of 15-minute phoners, ANDREW RAMADGE spent a weekend at the Yass homestead where Gareth Liddiard was recording his debut solo album, ‘The Strange Tourist’, with producer/engineer Burke Reid. What follows is an excerpt from a soon-to-be-published online novella inspired by ’90s gonzo journalism and grunge lit.


Burke only has one rule for me staying which goes for everyone and it’s that I don’t wander around the building too much while they’re recording. There’s microphones set up everywhere in the main room and on the stairs and even just closing a door too loudly might get picked up.

For the rest of the day the girls and I stick to the kitchen at the southern end of the mansion while the boys record in the other half.

Behind the kitchen is a storeroom and next to it is a laundry and another room that they say used to be the servant’s quarters.

Cara is Burke’s girlfriend and she likes to organise things and make everyone feel at home.

Courtney is a friend of Gaz and Fiona’s they met back in the Baker Street days. She’s a novelist and I’m glad to have another writer here and we talk about that sort of shit for a while as she stands up and warms her hands by the old wood fire stove in the corner.


When it gets dark we cook dinner and play cards. Fi teaches Courtney and I how to play ?Up the river, down the river?. Our eyes light up at the same time when the strategy sinks in. It’s about guessing the odds, not beating them.

We drink and deal hands for a few hours and then Courtney goes to bed and Cara goes to check on the boys and that’s when the lights go out. Fi and I are sitting at the table drinking hot toddies that are well on their way to becoming straight whiskey. Cara bursts back into the kitchen with a torch. The power’s been overloaded. Her and the boys need to drive up the road to the farm on the other side of the property to flick the mains switch.

Fi and I fumble around for the candles and a lighter or some matches and then settle back into talking about whatever it is you talk about when you’re drunk at one in the morning.

After fifteen minutes or so we hear the car pull into the drive and the front door creak open, but as soon as it slams shut the power goes again.

This time Gaz comes into the kitchen.

?You and Andrew need to come for a ride.?
?Why??
?Because it’s creepy.?
?Really??
?Yeah. There’s this weird shit on the radio.?

I stand up. Gaz seems like he might be drunk and he wants to show off a bit and who knows what’s going to happen but I’m a writer and I’m here for a story and I’m up for the ride.

We go outside and pack into the dirty sedan. Gaz and Fi are up front. Burke, Cara and I settle in the back and Max the cattle dog jumps in last and sits on my lap. We pull into the other driveway that leads up to the stables and Gaz does a U-ey.

?I was gonna to do a burnout but I suppose the owner might not like that.?
Fi tells him to settle down.
?But there’s a writer here from Mess+Noise.?

?Gaz seems like he might be drunk and he wants to show off a bit and who knows what’s going to happen but I’m a writer and I’m here for a story and I’m up for the ride.?

We go out onto the road. Gaz can’t find the creepy radio station, so we drive down to the farm in silence. It’s two or three hundred metres. When we reach the gate, Burke and Cara get out and walk past the headlights and then disappear into the dark to flick the switch and Max jumps out too. Fi and Gaz sit in the car and talk about something but I’m not really listening because I’m thinking about how fucking strange my life just got.

Burke and Cara and Max come back and pile into the car and Gaz pulls back towards the mansion. About fifty metres down the road he says something while he’s driving and then switches off the lights. Fi and Cara start shouting at him to turn them back on and he laughs. I know I’m supposed to be terrified, but it all just seems surreal. Everything’s black and we can’t see anything and I’m too drunk to feel anything and we’re moving and it’s like floating in space – except for all the screaming.

Gaz flicks the lights back on for half a second and then flicks them back off and veers a little to the right and then gets back straight and turns them on as we come through the drive.

Inside I pour myself another drink and go to bed while he and Burke start recording again.

?Night Gaz,? I say to him as I walk up the stairs between takes but I don’t know if he hears me or if he says anything back.

I fall asleep listening to him sing.
