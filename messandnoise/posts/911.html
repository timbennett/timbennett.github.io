A Dead Forest Index: Europe Tour Diary Pt 1

By Mess+Noise 2012-12-04T13:00:00+00:00

SAM* and *ADAM SHERRY of Melbourne duo A Dead Forest Index drift through Europe supporting Heirs, taking in bird remains and other scenery.


We are writing from Leipzig [at] UT Connewitz, a beautiful old theatre. My voice has given out so we had to pull out of tonight’s show. I’m upstairs at the top of the building, listening to the sounds of the other bands creaking through the floorboards. We’re halfway through our European tour with Heirs (Melbourne) and Soror Dolorosa (Paris).

We began in Bordeaux, performing on a boat in an industrial part of the city. It was interesting to get a sense of the South, passing field after field of sunflowers, darkening and lowering, but still gold everywhere. It would have been great to veer off track there and go to the coast, but we aimed to get to Paris in good time. And in convoy fashion, as we didn’t have mobile phones or GPS as such; we followed Heirs’ van in front.

Paris once again, walking, drifting around St Germain, with many tribal art galleries I’ve never seen before: masks, books about masks. We encamped at the Seine for as long as possible, spoke of masks and Chopin’s last photograph on the way to find the Chopin museum, before the show at Espace B and then getting lost trying to find the old Paris Conservatoire to see where Debussy and Ravel studied, which to my map was very close to the venue. We never found it and missed sound-check, but did get acquainted with the area that is the 19th arrondissement.


Munster, Germany: a great venue, with the kindest people. I’m not remembering many clear-cut performance side of things for any nights, as in the in-between times we are getting from point to point so quickly that it’s hard to take a place in and grasp where you are. A very strange thing happened while driving on a highway to Brussels: a mysterious dove/pigeon appeared to be coasting in the wind in front and above, and in slow motion coming down upon us, hitting the open window’s frame, becoming a disastrous moment. No trace of any feathers or body, just remains almost organised or arranged in our seated torso region. We’ll leave it at that. An omen perhaps. We kept looking over our shoulders from there on, no joke.

We then headed back to Germany, to Esslingen, performing at Kielmeyerhaus as part of a cultural festival in the city. We had the privilege of being the first band to play in this archaic 16th century basement (originally a hospital winery) never before used as a venue. Dust dirt floor with arching stone low above. The rope that cordoned off the ancient-looking stone staircase opened and the citizens of Esslingden young and old came pouring in.


Overall crowds have been incredibly receptive and enthusiastic so far as we then travelled onwards to Erlangen (Durer country). Beautiful open fields and river outside of town. Potsdam, Halle, Leipzig and Berlin tomorrow with some time off, hopefully to track down a harmonium, as we couldn’t travel with our own awkwardly heavy instrument. Heirs have been exceptional every night. The depth and presence so felt, and such a physical live show. We had a chance to listen to some unreleased versions of their songs along the way, in particular an extended and super heavy version of ‘Fowl’ which builds in a pattern that eventually fractures out. So interesting to see come into focus every night.

+
