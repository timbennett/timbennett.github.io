Milk Annual

By Nick Argyriou 2011-08-13T00:00:00+00:00

Samples and awkward time signatures merely enhance the sonic chronicle of Caitlin Park’s Milk Annual*. With acoustic guitars strummed in that time-honoured folk tradition and with slight reverb and loop in the vocal, Park sets about clearing a pastoral, clacking path accented by field sounds, breakout choruses and ensemble chants, including one that involves the Annandale Public School Choir on ?Warriors With Wild Hearts?. Typically this brand of sound is referred to as ?folktronic?, and as such it glides through one’s system like dark chilli chocolate. Intercalated throughout album is Park’s proclivity for classic 1940s and ?50s film grabs including lines of dialogue from Hitchcock’s *Rear Window (?How’s Your Wife??).

Adding to the Liam Judson stock of mixing masterworks, which includes his own band Belles Will Ring, as well as Cloud Control and Sister Jane, Milk Annual is arguably his finest achievement insofar as the manner in which the complexities of the record are managed. Park is a blithe spirit and the abstract impressionist musical voyage she takes requires a patient ear in the studio. Judson is just the man for the gig. Only 25 words worth of lyrics are hummed between Park and guest Holly Throsby on ?Cauliflower Jack? – with lingering howls, shuffled guitars, melodica and ambient chatter framing the tune – but the memories last longer than any other refrain on the record, and that’s saying something. This is pensive, poetic and tortured strains of melody of the highest order.

Caitlin Park – Baby Teeth by BSR

Milk Annual* openly takes its cues from US composer Steve Reich’s looping methodologies, while Park allows a co-write with sensual American musician Peter Broderick (?A Boat??) to slide through the perceptive mix. With opening lyrics that no doubt define both artists? work ethic, ?For days she lost a lot of sleep/In the best way possible/Her creative mind would not rest/Simple as that??, the track shambles percussively through Park’s stainless vocal range as the clarinet flutters, elevating the listener into a higher state of being. Park manages to reproduce this feeling time and time again throughout *Milk Annual. It’s seamless stuff.

The Milk Annual agenda must have seemed simple in theory, however the transformation of ideas into practice surely provided Park and Judson with night terrors at some stage. Thankfully the pair has mined, crafted, colluded and wangled a searching thread of noise that’s utterly unique.
