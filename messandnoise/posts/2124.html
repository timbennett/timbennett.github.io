Winter Got Warmer

By Ian Rogers 2009-10-01T00:00:00+00:00

In music writing, the prevailing use of phrases like ?outsider? and ?naive? is exonerative. For example: Band A sounds like shit but so what, Beat Happening did their thing and yadda, yadda, yadda, always ignoring the fact that Beat Happening were a particularly good band (ie. passionate, raw and dedicated). Talking about your average ramshackle punk-pop band in the same breath is akin to excusing Jet because The Rolling Stones were killer.

I think of this when I put on the new EP by Brisbane band Ambitious Lovers because what strikes me first is just how memorable the songs are. The outro of ?Clear The Cutlery? and the opening lines of ?Winter It Got Warmer? both feel familiar yet I would only have heard them live a handful of times, if that. (They played infrequently and recently disbanded). So while band-leader/vocalist/ukulele-player Joel may wail out of tune and have band mates Kel and Elise use bins, scrap metal and rolled newspapers for percussion, he always does so for the benefit of his songs. There’s always a sense that he’s trying to achieve something.

Accordingly, the band tended to document their work beautifully. Winter Got Warmer may be home recorded in Gatton in south-east Queensland (of all places) but this is not a lo-fi record. The 40-something seconds of a cappella that is closing track ?Fireworks? seems complete, naked as it is. ?Houses and Homes? may start with a lot of muttering and spluttering (serrated vocals, field recordings, the pounding of junk) but it soon winds its way into a sizable droning melody. And it is exactly this sense of purpose that have made Ambitious Lovers one of Brisbane’s more interesting acts over the years.

While others loiter in the space that punk opened up, clanging away because they can (and good for them), this band used that very same place to stage their own brand of self-determined and unusually beautiful music. It’s a shame they’re gone.
