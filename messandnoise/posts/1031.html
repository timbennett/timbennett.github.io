Track By Track: Tangled Star

By Mess+Noise 2013-08-04T14:00:00+00:00

Following his work in Perth bands from The Bamboos and The Healers to Outstation and The Slow Beings, WA institution CRAIG HALLSWORTH has made his debut full-length of ?slow-burn power pop? as Tangled Star. ?Let’s Adjourn to the Garden? is deceptively smart, as his commentary proves.

?Crazy Bit O? Truth?

A dark and misty (or is that dry ice?) rock song inspired by a dream I had in which I was perched on a tiny platform at the top of a rickety tower-like structure playing electric guitar. The louder I played, the more wildly the tower would teeter and sway. My instinct with using something like this for creative purposes is not to wonder what it means but to find other things for it to connect up with. Once I start playing around with words, all sorts of bits and pieces arrive – there’s a line here from Black Sabbath’s ?War Pigs?, and another from Michel de Certeau’s astonishing work on mystics, mad women and holy fools, The Mystic Fable.

Let’s Adjourn To The Garden by Tangled Star

?In Bed with the Averys?

Sometimes even the face of a relative stranger will make an impression on me, and cause me to dream up imaginary, alternate lives or histories for them. I usually keep it to myself, but occasionally I’ll write a song about it. The people who inhabit this one have secret charms and powers, and they are free in ways that can only seem suggestive but elusive to the rest of us.

Let’s Adjourn To The Garden by Tangled Star

?Someone’s Walking?

Now and then I get tired of juggling personal pronouns, so with this one I decided just to stick to ?someone.? Plus I like the anonymity, and I guess the universality, of this everyday event – ?someone? walking home. And I think it’s a good thing if I don’t know how to feel about it – simple, repetitive human actions seem to express something beyond our familiar emotional worlds.

Let’s Adjourn To The Garden by Tangled Star

?Hasn’t Made for Freedom?

For me, there’s a prankish and uncooperative spirit at work in creative processes, a diabolical principle that will only abide the emergence of something beautiful or meaningful if it can then wreck it and twist it out of shape. So, though they probably seem pretty straightforward, my songs for me remain problematic in terms of what they are about. This one may have something to do with the way people wear themselves out in the effort to adjust to the madness that the world’s been trapped in for some time now (capitalism).

Let’s Adjourn To The Garden by Tangled Star

?Painted Days?

You may know the story of the man who fell asleep and woke up 20 years later. Something of that nature seems to have occurred in this song – not sleep as such, but a kind of catalepsy. It’s as though a strange shadow fell over life, or an abyss emerged in the middle of life, dividing a self into reclusive strangers who can now only make assumptions about the limited world in which they find themselves.

Let’s Adjourn To The Garden by Tangled Star

?Attic Space Conversion #1?

This track seems to be composed of heterogeneous elements tacked together, like a medley of several different songs. So I guess you could say that even at the level of its musical structure and arrangement it expresses a desire to overcome itself, to keep searching, to reinvent itself. And lyrically my songs are always trying to do this too. They must do this, because for some reason they always emerge in some kind of altered or post-apocalyptic world where nothing can ever be the same again. I imagine my songs expressing the mysterious plight of molecularised, dispersed minorities in a world beyond identity.

Let’s Adjourn To The Garden by Tangled Star

?99% OK?

Again, this track seems like a couple of different things thrown together. The main body of it has a valedictory quality to it, but as usual any sort of stance is undercut or compromised. By the way, I’m not trying to make all this sound very complicated – if I put a lot of work or care into this and all my songs, it’s in an effort to achieve a lightness of touch that doesn’t burden a listener. Hopefully he or she simply likes it, and doesn’t mind having it around.

Let’s Adjourn To The Garden by Tangled Star

?Head in the Sand?

Sometimes there’s no clear reason why I feel a certain way about a particular song. This one for me is drenched with a kind of ominous exoticism that probably doesn’t exist for others. Perhaps I was reading or thinking about one of those haunting and disturbing Paul Bowles stories set in Morocco when I was writing it. In any case, if this song has a setting it’s in a spiritual desert, which is wherever you happen to find it.

Let’s Adjourn To The Garden by Tangled Star

?Theme?

Of despair that ends up failing to take itself seriously? I find the title alone somehow suggests a preparedness to accept a situation for what it is, and to move on.
