Stack Is The New Black

By Marcus Lester 2009-09-10T00:00:00+00:00

Shit stack.

With the cheap shot out of the way, here’s the serious point: Short
Stack piss on your indie. For a long time, the social norms of ?90s alternative rock have dominated the Australian music industry. The dragon of ?80s excess has not quite been slain, but it is a sickly beast that can barely raise its head, let alone breathe the cocaine flames of its glory days. These norms are a set of social attitudes – a cyncial pose towards authority and the industry, a refusal to write a straightforward love song, a dense fog of irony – enforced by aging hipsters through nothing more severe than a loose code of acceptable behaviour. Simply put, the alternative rockers of the ?90s, those heirs of punk rock, have become the establishment.

The establishment defines the outer limits of what is respectable music. Short Stack do not care. They piss on your indie, and they probably don’t even know that it’s there. Your values are torn-up ticket stubs in the laneway gutter and they are an underage drunk with a full bladder, staggering out from the Blue Light Disco after a night on the goon bag.

They write straightforward pop songs with the kind of mawkish lyrics
any self-respecting songwriter would scorn as teenage nonsense – and they don’t care, because they are teenagers, or close enough. They also spend a lot of time on their hair and makeup and outfits, their guyliner and their meggings, and they don’t try to hide it – they are proud of how they look, the freakish little morons. And their fans, all of them teenage girls, love them for all of this. Want to have their children. Probably are having their children.

It’s unlikely Short Stack will be more than a one album wonder. Their record has been marketed in classic pop style – another break with the indie schmindie orthodoxy – with a view to a quick killing rather than a long career. To hang around – and stranger things have happened – they’d need to overcome the twin handicaps of a singer who can’t sing (when he hits a high note, of course on the word “high”, it’s all autotune and it still doesn’t work) and songs that aren’t very catchy. But while their fans probably won’t be growing old along with Short Stack, buying records as they go, the general idea isn’t going to go away.

Sooner or later, we will enter the post-hipster era. Just as we now scorn on the excesses of ?80s rock, the cool kids of tomorrow will laugh at the hypocrisy of the so-called underground that has now dominated the cultural landscape for over a decade.

Yes, it will suck. Getting old does.
