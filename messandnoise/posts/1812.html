Other Side of the Street

By lawsonfletcher 2008-08-08T00:00:00+00:00

Other Side of the Street can be succinctly summarised around a few elements. Empty spaces. Archaic drum machines. Tumbleweed guitar licks. Echoing guitar stabs. All built on fairly spaced out, repetitive post-punk structures, music not so much musical as decaying around itself. A staid refusal to let things lose and rock out, like when ?Said It? only builds into busyness underneath a muffling blanket. The band also subscribe to that school of lyricism where what is sung seems far less important or meaningful than how – and how is all stinging, distant, dripping with bleakness. Yeah that’s right; words can hurt.

There’s not much here, but then again, that’s the whole point, I guess. What’s left over from a place like Willow Court Asylum, New Norfolk, where the album’s photographs were taken? The press release conveniently lists the repeated destructions of both man and building that have gone on at this site, which nevertheless survives as a hollowed out graffiti haunt. Join the dots, of course, and Insult For Injury sound out this architecture, skeletal structures that are literally immovable. The problem, of course, is that lying in all this desolation, you begin to wonder if anything might ever be built up again, if we might ever rise above our station?
