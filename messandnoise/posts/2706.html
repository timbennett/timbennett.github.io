Go Easy

By timscottt 2012-11-18T00:00:00+00:00

In Brisbane 2012, Josh Watson’s documentary on the current Brisbane experimental music scene, Blank Realm’s Dan Spencer jokes how the band still receives emails from people preferring earlier tapes to their current output. Tough shit. Of all the bands to have sprung from Brisbane’s fulsome scene of recent years – Slug Guts, Cobwebbs, STAG, Scraps, Meat Thump – Blank Realm alongside Kitchen’s Floor Matt Kennedy have probably changed things up the most.

Their third album (a co-release between Bedroom Suck and iconic Philadelphia label Siltbreeze) sees them moving ever further away from their chaotic, noisy, performance-based beginnings into a band that have reined in the sound without losing any of the looseness. The opening ?Acting Strange? busts out of the gate with a Sonic Youth ferocity to guitar and vocals but then I swear the bright but lackadaisical guitar at the beginning of ?Cleaning Up My Mess? could be a Dick Diver song. It soon drifts off with backward meandering before breaking into some Twin Infinites-era Royal Trux meanness. All up it’s seven minutes of pure ?awesome song.?

Blank Realm – Cleaning Up My Mess by bedroom suck

The album’s strongest song, ?Pendulum Swing?, revisits the heavy New Zealand pop vibe that the band first explored on 2010’s D’j? What? album, but here with more of a shimmering and rubbery haze of garage-rock psychedelia. Spencer’s lyrics nigh indecipherable below a fog of swirly guitar and synths, ?The Crackle Pt 1? and ?The Crackle Pt 2? sees a more woozy, synth-psych freakout. It’s the closest they come to the early wig-out jams when they first started in Brisbane.

I first came across the closing title track ?Go Easy? when the band performed on Brian Turner’s WFMU radio program (alongside Slug Guts). Here it retains its swaggering mid-Stones burn. It’s an ode to driving down the boulevard while listening to the radio and to the drugged-out rock of 1970s Los Angeles. Of a time when men wore large rings.

So the band are still receiving emails from people who say they dig their old stuff rather than their newer stuff. Again, after listening to Go Easy, I only have this to say: Tough shit.
