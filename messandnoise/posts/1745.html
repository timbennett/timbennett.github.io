Stable Government And Adequate Sanitation

By justlikehoney 2008-04-01T00:00:00+00:00

Some say Marcus Cook (ex-Vivian Girls) visually resembles Henry from Eraserhead* – this EP confirms that the dark ambient music he creates is conveniently as cold and as ominous as that film’s alienating soundtrack. *Stable Government And Adequate Sanitation is a sole twenty-one minute track – a stark, elongated electronic progression way more active than your run-of-the-mill hippy-dippy dronescape but 10,000 leagues less comforting than your garden variety Buddha Box. Metallic tones rise to a grinding apex and peel away during numbing lulls. Harmonics ring in and out, swathed in reverb. It’s totally sterile, but thanks to Cook’s drawn-out digital treatment it’s never static. All subtle buzzes and twisting hums are beautifully blended in the mix, ensuring that there’s just enough unsettling dissonance present at all times and a decent measure of sonic grit amongst all the airiness.

Def Jam’s got the perfect handle on doomy minimalism – underscoring fucked beaurocracies and our planet’s health crises with this kind of a ghostly, faceless symphony is totally appropriate. There are all manner of nasty particles polluting our smoggy, carcinogenic atmosphere and similarly grainy textures abound in this cloud of sick sound. This is dirty, creeping – one can feel something sinister draw nearer and nearer yet, like the gradual onset of upchucky fever or the slow realisation that a vile and evil presence lives and grows amongst us.
