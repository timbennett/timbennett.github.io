Little Animals

By andyr 2007-03-18T12:00:00+00:00

The Beasts Of Bourbon don’t work through headphones. Cheap ones are too tinny and an expensive pair are still too small. The sound is meant to hit you in the chest and shake you down. The first band Tex Perkins ever saw was AC/DC, he once told Rolling Stone, and the experience defined his whole career. ?It was incredibly loud, but a physical loud rather than an ear-splitting loud and I’ve been trying to achieve that ever since,? he said.

The Beasts perfected their gut-fucking roar long ago and now, two and a half decades after forming, their sixth studio album is being released on the label that broke AC/DC. Its opening track, used as a teaser for the album, is where Perkins proves he’s still got the requisite attitude. ?I used to give my money to the motherfucking poor/But I don’t care about nothin? anymore,? he sings, in a voice like a homeless man cutting loose. It’s as good as anything they’ve released, with the same influences as usual – The Rolling Stones? rock-star swagger mixed with the formidable bottom-end of The Stooges.

Nothing else on Little Animals hits quite the same high (or perhaps low), but it’s not a let-down either. Spencer P. Jones? title track is a cruel joke on do-goodie types, with simpleton children’s book lyrics about ?cuddly, friendly, tasty? little animals that have gone extinct. For added confusion, it’s sung over a tender, almost rueful guitar riff. His other contribution, ?Thanks? is an A-Z list of the drugs people have shouted him over the years that, apparently, inspired the band to go back into the studio. The rest of the album is the group’s signature swamp-rock growl, best played loud enough to rattle your toes. It probably sounded punchier twenty years ago, but it hasn’t lost its appeal.
