New Noise: Open Swimmer, Claws & Organs, Point Being, River Yarra

By Mess+Noise 2014-10-15T13:00:00+00:00

It’s a banner week for New Noise, including two debut singles.

? From encountering a dead miner bird in Melbourne to ?flying back to Glasgow with you,? ?Sugar Bowl? takes us on a global trot right along with Open Swimmer?s Ben TD, a WA native who indeed did a spell in Glasgow before settling in his current base of Melbourne. The dollar-download single is quite the attention-grabbing calling card, grounding TD’s streaming folk-style vocal cadences with a danceable DIY edge of stuttered drums and low-key bass throbs. The band’s current four-piece lineup includes The Harpoons? Bec Rigby, and ?Sugar Bowl? comes from the debut album Canyon*. The song features backing vocals from the ubiquitous Banoffee. Produced by the reliable Nick Huggins, the album launches tomorrow (Oct 17) at Northcote Social Club with Rowena Wise and Brendan Welch. It’s $15 at the door from 8:30pm. Three years in the making, *Canyon includes many other guests from the Two Bright Lakes camp besides Rigby, Banoffee and Huggins: Kishore Ryan (Kid Sam), Eva Popov (Hello Satellites), Chris Bolton (Seagull) and Mark Gretton (Touch Typist).

Sugar Bowl by Open Swimmer

? Evoking The Dirt of Luck*-era Helium is an easy way into our hearts, and Claws & Organs do just that right from the start of their new EP, *I Am Scum and Nobody Should Love Me*. Although the opening ?Die!? gets a bit grungier in the chorus, Heather Thomas’s glum vocals and David Crowe’s muddy guitar do Helium proud. On a more local tip, they’ve been compared to DZ Deathrays and Damn Terran, and Crowe’s own turns at the mic certainly hammer those home. The six-song EP follows last year’s *Nothing to Learn EP and is out digitally as well as on limited 12? vinyl. Recorded at Head Gap with Neil Thomason (Jen Cloher, Augie March), it’s getting launched with a six-date East Coast tour from this weekend.

Sat, Oct 18 – Rashfest, Melbourne, VIC
Sun, Oct 19 – Frankie’s Pizza, Sydney, NSW
Thurs, Oct 23 – Alhambra Lounge, Brisbane, QLD
Thurs, Nov 6 – Jade Monkey, Adelaide, SA
Sat, Nov 8 – The Eastern, Ballarat, VIC [w/Flyying Colours]
Thurs, Nov 13 – The Workers Club, Melbourne, VIC

I Am Scum And Nobody Should Love Me by Claws & Organs

? Besides laying claim to a perfectly snide name, Point Being features M+N contributor Max Easton on bass. The Sydney four-piece also includes Nite Fields? Liza Harvey on drums and two Nicks on guitar: Nick Webb and Nick Roberts. Their debut single ?Degustation? finds a rumpled halfway mark between jangly guitar-pop and bratty post-punk. It’s super catchy while still being all too pointed in the lyrics: ?You’re not invited/But you come anyway.? Recorded and mixed by Wade Gilmour (Mannheim Rocket), the song is gracing a 7? due in December through Mystic Olympic – the new label from Unity Floors? Gus Hunt, which released that great split 7? between Unity Floors and Chook Race – and follows a handful of Sydney gigs with Day Ravies, Low Life, Ruined Fortune, Lenin Lennon and Naked.

? Eight minutes of weirdo bliss: that’s the debut single from River Yarra. ?The Look? is the latest opus from Raudie McLeod, who released the blog-beloved single ?Marcus? under his own name last year. Up for free download below, this new single has been dubbed ?psych-cosmic-bush-dance? by McLeod, and it does blur a lot of genre lines – many of them warmly nostalgic – on its way to full-body ecstasy. The first release on new label Solitaire Recordings, ?The Look? began with field recordings gathered while McLeod was on holiday at a friend’s farm in regional Victoria. It’s a very inviting taste of his upcoming three-song EP as River Yarra.
