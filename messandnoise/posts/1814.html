Slow Burn

By Trevor 2008-09-01T00:00:00+00:00

First, an apology. This excellent home-grown record was issued back in September, and yet was somehow overlooked for review. Mea culpa, no offence, let’s move on.

Having been around for four years now, The Vandas feel like long-term mainstays, and while that’s true of their constant (and consistent) live efforts, this is their first full-length album.

Recorded in a day, at their usual rehearsal studio (Soundpark in Northcote, Melbourne), it has a very comfortable feel to it, like a pair of well-worn boots. There’s a distinct whiff of sticky carpet in the air and production by Joel Silbersher (Hoss, GOD) captures a very live feel.

Musically, they don’t owe a great audible debt to their namesake Harry Vanda, the legendary Easybeats songwriter/guitarist. It’s a fairly steady mid-paced chug for the most part, with a fistful of distorted chords laid over the top for colour and the odd splash of brass. A lot of the material has a country tinge – see ?Know It All? and ?Warm At Night?, for instance – but nothing veers into alt-limpness.

If you are a fan of You Am I or Spencer P Jones, you’ll find plenty to like here. And make no mistake, this is a hugely likeable album. Frontman Chris Altmann takes the lead in writing the bulk of the material and never feels the need to strive for any stunning avant-garde statements, or transcendental moments of overwrought imagery. These are mostly small personal tales, and they are told with a good eye for detail. The title track does indeed exhibit a slow, deep smoulder; a multi-part story that unfolds over a generous five minutes, alternating quiet interludes with great swathes of guitar. Closer ?See Me Round? is a classic smiling-through-tears break-up song.

The CD also comes with a voucher that allows you to download a further six songs from the same sessions for free; a great way to round this punchy collection out a little further.

Their next release will be pounced on.
