The Line For The Men?s Room

By timscottt 2012-02-26T00:00:00+00:00

In AFL circles, the word Modra evokes the flash and flamboyance of former Adelaide Crow Anthony ?Tony? Dale Modra. Perhaps it’s just a heavy dose of irony. but there’s absolutely nothing flashy or flamboyant about Modra the New York City band – three quarters of whom happen to be Aussie expats. Theirs is a mood and style heavy on the downer vibes, taking the jagged and distorted bummer blues of The Birthday Party, Chrome Cranks and Syd Barrett then retching their own take onto a dive bar floor.

This debut LP is released on Savage Quality, the label run by Kevin DeBroux (nee Failure) of Pink Reason, who in kudos of the highest/darkest order has described them as ?more depressing than Pink Reason.? It’s a harrowing listen. The grainy and blurred cover pic of a bunch of skinheads pissing in the bathroom gives some indication of the creaky feedback and noise on offer. This is what is called an ?un-nicety?.

Modra – Her Taste In My Mouth by Savage Quality Recordings

Guitars rule but like the other bands connected with Modra – Coconuts, Bogan Dust – the guitars are of the epic and noisy feedback variety. Things stumble out of the stall with ?Manifestations of You?, slow guitars that evolve into a epic and distorted free-rock squall. Album highlight ?Her Taste in My Mouth? snakes along in a dark and twisted manner before some barely audible vocals chime in. Something about forgetting to pick up the shopping.

The recording, like the tunes, is pretty ugly and at times feels like pressing weight on an already blackened and bruised fingernail. But for all the album’s snarl, songs such as ?The Restless Dream? – with its Eastern flourishes – and the aforementioned ?Her Taste in My Mouth? ascend to a beautiful, if shambolic and fractured, mess.
