Bits of Shit

By timscottt 2010-07-01T00:00:00+00:00

Bits of Shit claim to be Australia’s first ?patch band?. I’m not entirely sure what that means, but judging by the cover image and liner notes of their debut 7? it seems a mixture of denim jackets, the White Panther Party and a Bandidos chapter who prefer clubhouse ?prawn and porn? nights over amphetamine transportation, prison sentences and gunshot wounds.

Featuring former/current members of The Up Syndrome, Southernhay Orphans, The Sailors and The Ooga Boogas, these four middle-aged punks hole up in their Coburg headquarters in Melbourne and spew out demented and rough takes on early US hardcore and punk that lends an ear to The Adolescents, Black Flag and The Stooges. It actually sounds a hell of a lot better than it reads on paper.

Opening with the scuzzy ‘Dirty Bomb’, which has Bits of Shit pallbearer Danny Vanderpol shrieking like some old dude waking up, the band thrash it out in a workmanlike fashion. The meaty guitar of Andy Lang is backed by an (almost) tight rhythm section featuring Leon ?Stacky? Stackpole (bass) and Daniel Dempster (drums). They probably don’t quite match the sonic power of Damaged-era Black Flag, but they get it done.

They also get credit for naming a song after the great Tim Hemensley, although I’m not sure how much it has to do with the late member of GOD and The Powdermonkeys. It’s more like the twisted, drunken metaphysical ramblings of dudes you see on the corner of Gertrude and Brunswick streets in Fitzroy.

Closing out with ‘Gale Force’ – which I believe they were calling themselves for five minutes before settling on the more indelible Bits of Shit – the record is fast and furious and proves that you can be any age to rage.
