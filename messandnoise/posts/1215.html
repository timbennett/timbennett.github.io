Europe Photo Diary: Tiny Ruins

By Mess+Noise 2014-07-06T14:00:00+00:00

Auckland’s HOLLIE FULLBROOK, a.k.a. Tiny Ruins, did a European tour in April/May, supporting Neil Finn for a long run of dates and headlining on other dates. She shares some of her memories below.

Setting off for six weeks. Day one at Auckland International Airport.

One of the highlight Neil Finn shows for me was in an old theatre Olympia] in Dublin. It may have been influenced by the [Murphy’s, or the singing crowd, but the show ran for three hours…

This was a beautiful cello I got to play on for some of Neil’s songs. My first show with the band was performing on Jools Holland – thrown in the deep end!

Soundchecking at the Royal Festival Hall in London. On the Neil Finn leg of the tour we played some magnificent venues, and it was interesting to be able to contrast them with the smaller, dive-y but characterful places I was playing for my own shows.

Ben Anderson and Magnus Bang-Olsen, my special bandmates for two weeks of headlining European shows. This was us piling up all the gear in Berlin, about to pack up the tour van.

Soundchecking with Ben and Magnus in at Hasenschaukel in Hamburg. Of course I missed having Cass [Basil] and Alex [Freer] on tour with me, but these two were a wonderful addition to the tour which would have otherwise been solo. They both live in Berlin but Ben is a good friend from New Zealand.

Having a lark in old Parie.

Another great show of Neil’s. I think this one is from Newcastle. It was a real treat to watch from the side of stage, and I especially enjoyed some of his more obscure songs, which he’d pull out for the hardcore fans.

Wandering around Bristol, where I was born and spent my first 10 years … this is me on the bridge opposite the shambling terraced house I grew up in. We lived looking out over the city and train tracks. A magical place in my childhood imagination, and still special to me now.

Magnus, Ben and I on the last day of our tour together.

+

####Tiny Ruins? second album, ?Brightly Painted One?, is out now on CD, vinyl and digital through Spunk (Australia/NZ), Bella Union (UK/Europe) and Flying Nun (North America).
