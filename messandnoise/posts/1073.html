Track By Track: McKisko

By Mess+Noise 2013-10-06T13:00:00+00:00

Body knowledge, sleep deprivation, ?the sads? and Brisbane landmark ?The Fairy House? all informed HELEN FRANZMANN?s second album as McKisko.

?A Little Light?

Written about seven years ago, this was almost on 2009?s] [Glorio, my first album. I couldn’t make the change in the middle of the song, which worked well live, translate in a recording. Enter Kurt Read, an e-bow, some delays and many arduous hours of vocal layering. The song shifts to a single room mic at the end, which I find really satisfying.

?Good Grief?

There’s a black room and a white room in my home. This was a black room song. Weeks of sleep deprivation led to paranoia, led to song. Up-tempo melancholy. My sister Caitlin made a (/tv/4611424) for this. There’s a haggard old lady in it at one point. She looks how I felt at the time of writing.

?Turning Curving?

The music for this song we had long before the lyrics and melody. I think it existed for about a year before we finished it. The repetitive nature of the music is meant to represent the turning of the earth. The words, melody and an email to Paddy [Mann of Grand Salvo] all happened within a few days. I had played a couple of times with Paddy and I think his voice is fine. I sent him the monotone second part, he recorded it, returned it, and the song was complete.

?I stepped out and to the side?

This was a two-stringed acoustic guitar song written while in the sads. We ended up recording a few different versions on organ and electric but I kept coming back to the two acoustic strings. Sad old two strings. Simple.

?Dirt Story Howl?

There’s a house in Brisbane known as ?The Fairy House?. The man who built it sculpted these strange little figures all over the exterior. It’s on a fairly major roundabout so lots of people know it and for a while I rented the attic as a place to write.

Inspired by some words that were scribbled on a scrap of paper tacked to the door of the room: ?What stands behind, what lays there dying, what’s held within, what lays there dying?? Every time I exited the building I’d read this. It became the final phrase of the song.

?Trolling?

It’s hard to get things done when you’re love drunk. I had written this song on guitar years ago and it was just hanging around in garage band on my computer. A drum track Kurt sent me locked it in nicely. I canned the guitar. An old song with new life.

?K. Henry Sings?

The original glock part on which this entire song was built was recorded accidentally into a loop pedal while reproducing a two-note piano chord for another song, ?The Hollow Boat?. When it played back I started singing the ?Outside, outside? part. The song is about body knowledge and loneliness. My body accidentally triggered the pedal, forming the basis of what has become my favourite song on the album.

?Oh Oh Oh?

My everything in a song. Oh so sentimental.
