Track By Track: Smile

By Mess+Noise 2013-10-14T13:00:00+00:00

The first album by Melbourne’s Smile is full of buoyant, rambling guitar-pop. Frontman PETE BAXTER revisits the good vibes behind the tunes.

On the album: Our debut was only made possible by our bass player Josh Delaney, in whose shed we recorded it. He engineered it all. We started recording this album as soon as the band was formed in September 2012, recording each song as we wrote it, keeping the ones we liked. It was mixed and mastered by our good friend Andrei Eremin and the artwork was done by local hero Julian Hocking.

?Still Waiting For My Man?

This song was written about our weed dealer Sebastian. One of the finest gentlemen I’ve ever met.

Life Choices by SMILE

?Cuntry Lyf?

I wrote this song while my partner Sunni was pregnant with my son Fox and we were looking for a house we could afford to rent, which was proving difficult. I walked from home down to Round and Round on Sydney Rd. and bought a Dream Syndicate record. This song is the feeling of that afternoon. This is one of my favourite tracks on the album.

Life Choices by SMILE

?Pascoe Vale?

This song was written after I rode to Pascoe Vale to buy a pair of hexagonal Ray-Bans I found on Gumtree. Max [Turner] wrote the intro separately, inspired by Sunni and my looking for a house (Max and I lived together at the time). Max also plays the solo on this, which is a nice point of difference as all the other face-melters belong to Nick Mulhall.

Life Choices by SMILE

?Sunni Hart?

Written as a voice memo to send Sunni to brighten her day. I wrote it in between writing ?serious? songs. This was the only song of that afternoon, though, which made the record. I love Liam [Gough]?s drumming during the 7/8 change.

Life Choices by SMILE

?Born Again?

This one I originally wrote for a band Sunni and I formed called The Trapdoors. We reworked it a year or so later when SMILE formed. Max inspired the outro during a practice session, which I believe really lifts the song into something great.

Life Choices by SMILE

?Jesus Song?

I recorded this song also before SMILE was formed, out in Warrandyte with my friend Sam Tesky on sitar. Max re-envisioned it into the direction it took on the album. Nick’s guitar tone in this is my favourite tone on the record. He used a tiny old reel-to-reel amp-type thing and a Telecaster he built. This song is a personal account of my history growing up in extreme Christianity. It’s simple and sad, like all of my favourite songs.

Life Choices by SMILE

?Stoned (Get These Fucking Flies Off My Fucking Face/Shut Up and Make Money)?

This was written late at night when I was too stoned to write lyrics about anything other than being too stoned. The whole song is only two chords. Really nice build between the bass and drums, especially in the outro. Max speaks the words of wisdom at the end.

Life Choices by SMILE

?Amess St.?

This was one of the first songs written for SMILE. It came out of our first rehearsal. Max started the progression and Nick started the distorted wah and the rest is history. The lyrics are about the morning Sunni got home from Berlin and my roommate Becky was out. I didn’t have a bed at the time so we fornicated in Becky’s.

Life Choices by SMILE

?Luxury Joint?

I wrote this while a dear friend of mine was really ill in hospital and on a lot of medication. It was originally kind of a lullaby I guess. I like the way it feels when this song ends the album.

Life Choices by SMILE
