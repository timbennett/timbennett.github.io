Track By Track: Monnone Alone

By Mess+Noise 2013-05-23T14:00:00+00:00

Former Lucksmiths bassist and amateur boatman MARK MONNONE makes his full-length solo debut with ?Together at Last?. But it’s not exactly solo, featuring members of The Ladybug Transistor, Crystal Stilts, The Aislers Set, Still Flyin?, The Ruby Suns, The Clean and Architecture in Helsinki, among others.

?The Westerly Whip?

An ode to my mother, Sirpa Sisko Monnone n’e Wallenius, Miss Finland 1964 and loving mother of two. She spent the last seven years of her life in St Helens on the east coast of Tasmania, where the topsy-turvy weather conditions can turn any pleasant bayside stroll into a battle against nature’s most brutal forces.

The Westerly Whip by Monnone Alone

?Pink Earrings?

I think Tom Waits once said the three things required for a good song are a place, the weather and something to eat. Well, my formula is this: a place, some costume jewellery and a facial expression. This one takes place in London, specifically near Borough Market, and is kind of about being more impressed by some interesting accessorising than a crummy old city’s bridges and architecture.

Pink Earrings by Monnone Alone

?My Overdue Library Fines?

I made this song up while driving the two hours from the east coast of Tasmania to Launceston with no car stereo. When it came to recording this one, I was heavily flu-ridden and had almost entirely lost my voice – I wish all the vocals on the album sounded this good. Meanwhile, Gary Olson of The Ladybug Transistor] was in his element recording ridiculous harmony over ridiculous harmony in the middle-eight part. And it kills me to say, I’m still no closer to finishing Brautigan’s [The Tokyo-Montana Express.

My Overdue Library Fines by Monnone Alone

?Echoing Days?

In 2003, I tried to write lyrics for this song every time I did my laundry at the Spensley Street laundromat in Clifton Hill. It was also the first song recorded for the album in 2010, with my friend Linton [of The Aislers Set] on the drumkit. I had no intention of making a record or starting a new band; I just wanted to finish this song. The lyrics only came to mind after my girlfriend and I visited the medieval cloisters in New York City, which I still don’t believe actually exist.

Echoing Days by Monnone Alone

?When You’re Happy You Won’t Understand?

The Bartlebees were a band from Munich, Germany, and this song is one of my favourites from their hefty catalogue. I’m pretty sure I have some of the lyrics a bit wrong and some of the chords a lot wrong. Gus [Franklin of Architecture in Helsinki and The Smallgoods] came up with the great organ line that really makes our version sound like it could have been recorded at any time in the past 50 years. Hamish Kilgour [of The Clean and The Mad Scene], meanwhile, tapped and shook some exotic instruments and also had his pocket full of some amazing cashew-nut bars.

When You’re Happy You Won’t Understand (Bartlebees cover) by Monnone Alone

?Tangerine & The Bear?

Where I work, there’s a great guy named Adam. While frothing some milk recently, he was telling me how, while living in London back in the day, he used to work at a hip organics place called Spitalfields Organics. ?No way!? I said. ?Do you know a lady who used to work there – she had hair the colour of a tangerine? And a big burly guy, maybe her dad? Maybe her boyfriend or brother? With a large beard, looked like an actual bear??? Anyway, you see where this story’s going. Also, I love Kyle [Forester]?s hilarious saloon piano on this.

Tangerine and the Bear by Monnone Alone

?Sunset Video Project?

I’m glad we got to marry up some fuzz bass, motorik drums and chorused guitars somewhere on the album. This is the unofficial theme song for my friend’s video project that I still haven’t seen. She asked me to be involved and film a sunset while living in London, and it really looked like I’d shot the whole thing in black and white.

Sunset Video Project by Monnone Alone

?The Business World?

Written with my friend Andrew ?Hotdog? Kaffer of the great Portland band Kissing Book; a truly underrated artist, Marx Brothers aficionado, and the first person I ever heard use the term ?freegan?. He and I, along our pals Kellie Sutherland of Architecture in Helsinki], Pete [Cohen of Sodastream] and Dunky [Jack], put out an EP in 2001 under the fearsome name [The Best Friends Group. Check it out at your local eBay store! But yeah, this song features Connal Parsley [Your Wedding Night, Francis Plagne] ripping some nice bass.

?Ricochet?

Originally short-listed (along with several of these songs) for the last Lucksmiths album 2008?s First Frost*], but under a different title and with completely different lyrics about writing a farewell note after having been bitten on the neck by a funnel-web spider. The updated version takes its title from the proto-*Pong arcade game I played whilst on tour with [Still Flyin? in the former GDR. Bec Rigby happily braved a winter downpour and rode the Q train in to Gary’s studio in Brooklyn to sing the duet. You may know Bec from her band The Harpoons – their album is pretty high on the Most Anticipated Debut Album Of All Time list.

Ricochet by Monnone Alone

?Steep Ravine?

A love song to mother nature. Just getting high on Mo Natch, bro. Feel it!

Steep Ravine by Monnone Alone
