Track By Track: Ghoul

By Darren Levin 2011-02-02T13:00:00+00:00

Songs inspired by Triosk and Ohana, the moody beats of Burial and Four Tet’s organic electronica – Ghoul singer IVAN VIZINTIN talks us through the inspiration behind his band’s mini-album ?Dunks?.

‘Milkily’

For a while we had a drumkit set up in my bedroom, and Pav (Pavle Vizintin) would bash on it every once in a while. He plays open handed and the drum pattern sorta sprung up from his technique; Hanno Andrew Hannaford] changed it up a bit in recording. All of us were listening to a lot of [Ohana, and ?Milkily? is a bit of a nod in their direction: the on-beat/off-beat guitar and bass stabs were all derived from stuff they were exploring. The solo was done in two takes then panned hard right/left for that crazy effect.

‘Dreambeat’

I spent hours on this one; chopping drums and synth bass back in time,
adjusting the programming, etc. Initially we wanted it to sound huge, as if we were playing in a gymnasium or something. When we gave it to
Burke [Reid] to mix he changed everything up and brought everything real close and intimate. The most prominent instrument is a gumnut shaker that I found at Anthony [Warwick’s] house. His dad collects odd wooden percussion instruments. An old workmate went to Vietnam and did some field recordings, so the sample at the end is of a blind man singing into some megaphone apparatus.

‘Good Loops’

Seekae got me listening to Four Tet’s Rounds around the same time I
heard Hanging Gardens by The Necks. I chopped up a drum thing Hanno
and I did and played some acoustic guitar on top of it. After that it
was layers, layers and layers. Burke suggested we add some synth to
the backend of the song to flesh it out a bit more. I like this one – even if I can’t decide if it’s in 6/4 or 4/4.

‘The Slip’

Anthony has this banged up 12-string that is never in tune and
that’s where we got the guitar sound for ?The Slip?. I was afraid that
we were repeating ourselves, so I tried to make the vocal melody
ridiculously elastic. It was comped together from over 100 takes.
The beats are taken from a Swizz Beats sample pack and loosely played
on an MPC [sampler]. I tried to quantise all the samples but the track just ended up sounding flat.

‘Facepaint’

?Facepaint? is one of first songs we wrote together and has gone through various versions. Hanno figured out some of that faux Triosk stuff at the beginning of the track and then sampled it to make the drum pattern throughout. Most of the more ?pad? sounds are derived from running guitars through delay pedals and getting them to self oscillate.

‘3Mark’

?3Mark? was the last song we wrote, and it took about a week. Hanno and I really love Burial so this is a blatant rip-off of his stuff. The
synth, the beats, the pitched vocal. Burke whittled this one down in
mixing and simplified it a lot. This one is really Hanno’s baby. He
planned out the structure and the counterpoint. We recorded the drums
(like most of the album) with two microphones in my garage. I really
struggled for lyrics with this one, so that’s why it’s just one thing
over and over and over. And I like George Michael. A lot.

Ghoul – 3Mark by inertiamusic

‘Ouija’

This one was written in a day, when we had an open-house and people
were walking in and out of my room asking me what I was doing. Pav and
I came back from Berlin and had I bought a Waldorf Blofeld, a great
little synth. The song is made up of loops from a improvised jam we
had with two of our mates, just cut and pasted on top of each other.
My favourite track on the record.

+

####Ghoul’s ?Dunks? is out tomorrow (February 4) through Speak N Spell.
