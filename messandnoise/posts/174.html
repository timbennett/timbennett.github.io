Worst Covers of 2008

By Darren Levin 2009-01-18T13:00:00+00:00

One person’s art is another’s garbage. The same can’t be said for these covers, which are unequivocally shit.

Many thanks to Polyster Music, M+N photographers Ben Gook, Ben Butcher, Cecelia B, Boud, Joshua Morris, Kristy Milliken, Pete Ottery, Lauren Bamford and other artistic friends, who would prefer to remain nameless (the chickens) for helping us wade through last year’s releases to pick the worst of the worst.

On Friday, we reveal the best covers of 2008.


Architecture in Helsinki
That Beep
?That Beep? obviously wasn’t loud enough to protect the Smurf village from the gumboots of Gargamel.


Assassination Collective
A Million Fantastic Reasons
… to not put a band photo on an album cover.


The Datsuns
Headstunts
The album title is an anagram of the band’s name. The cover is an anagram of carp.


Nick Cave & The Bad Seeds
Dig!!!Lazarus Dig!!!
What’s the name of the album again? Wait, those glaring orange lights might be a clue.


Rekindle
The Chandelier
Edward Furlong does ?Blue Steel?.


Cog
Sharing Space
Do you really need another reason to not buy this record?


Little Red
Listen to Little Red
A brick wall. A Posca pen. A bunch of dorks that look like Sha Na Na. Oh, they make it so easy to Belittle Red.


Fabulous Diamonds
Seven Songs
We know what you’re thinking – Are those nails real?


The Presets
Apocalypso
Accepting the award for Worst Cover Art: George Hamilton and Buckethead.


Empire of the Sun
Walking on a Dream
Before they were stars, Siegfried and Roy paid the rent by starring in a gay porn parody of Dune…


Kate Miller-Heidke
Curioser
… Kate Miller-Heidke was an extra.


Wes Carr
You
?Just chuck a mugshot on there, Dicko. They’re just going to buy it off iTunes anyway.?


Van She
V
Streetparty sure has a lot to answer for.


Bliss N Eso
Flying Colours
Every time a skip-hop act wins an ARIA, a Triple J employee gets their wings.


The Holy Sea
A Beginner’s Guide To The Sea
A one-eyed monster. Seamen ready to shoot. The innuendo was probably unintended, but we couldn’t resist.


The Vines
Melodia
That Craig Nicholls – always the centre of attention.


NFA
Brain Wash
In the hands of the wrong people, Corel Draw 2.0 can be a dangerous thing.
