New Noise: Tim Shiel, Oblako Lodka, Corin, Extrafoxx

By Mess+Noise 2014-07-15T14:00:00+00:00

Fluid electronics, low-key pop, ambient piano and fuzzy indie rock make up this week’s roundup of new music.

? Tim Shiel hasn’t exactly been at loose ends since coming off a massive world tour as Gotye’s live keyboardist. He’s scored the hit mobile game Duet*, become a key presenter on the rebranded Double J and – most prestigious of all – been the subject of a *Mess + Noise* feature. He’s also been working on Georgia Fields? next record, continuing his ?future pop duo? Telling and starting new soundtrack work. Now he’s just released the name-your-price five-song EP *Time Shield*, a nod to the punning former moniker he used to sometimes employ in addition to Faux Pas. The first four tracks are versions of a fluid composition planned for an experimental app that never saw completion – they were designed to change and react depending on the user’s mood and location plus factors like the weather and time. So ?Dwn? was meant for dawn listening, while ?Vincent Cassel? was intended for transit. The whole EP picks up nicely from the *Duet OST, reminding us just how poppy and organic Shiel can make his electronic vignettes. Freed from duties of soundtracking, they can also branch out and pick up stray influences, like the hip-hop-ish vocal sample on ?Vincent Cassel?. Perfect work listening.

Time Shield by Tim Shiel

? Oblako Lodka is the low-key indie pop outlet for Melbourne’s Irit Rozenfeld. The revolving-cast band is now a trio including Alexis Hall (The Motifs, Milk Teddy) and Peter Joseph Head (t:dy t:wns, Actor Buddhists), and there’s a new single in ?A Better Way?/?Cosmic Mind?. It’s out now for $2 download through Airpunch Collective, and follows a self-titled LP from back in 2011. The new songs are more vocal-anchored than that album, with Rozenfeld’s airy exhalations coming to the fore over warm and plinking keyboards, organ and drum machine. The songs get launched Thursday, August 7 at The Curtin Bandroom, with Totally Mild, Denim Owl and Knock Yr Socks Off DJs supporting. More info here. Limited CDs of the single – designed to resemble vinyl – will be for sale on the night.

A Better Way/Comic Mind by Oblako Lodka

? When someone mentions piano and ambient in the same breath, we immediately think of Sophie Hutchings. And Corin Ileto’s debut EP as Corin has a similar transporting vibe, though it initially seems to fall more on the ambient side. Still, piano and synth melodies soon peek through to punctuate the drifting textures she establishes, and her live-to-video set for the Display Home series early this month put a decided emphasis on keys. Downloadable for any price, or for sale as a limited CD, Deluge* was self-recorded and features cover art by Jess Gibson. It showcases the Sydneysider’s classical training, but by the end of ?Glue? there’s also a twitchy play for more electronic possibilities. In other developments, EP opener ?Morning? appears on the latest Skydreams comp, and Ileto is doing a radio set soon for FBi Radio’s *Ears Have Ears show.

DELUGE EP by CORIN

? While we’re not sure how many takers Extrafoxx will get from charging $1,000 for their new album on Bandcamp, it’s a pretty funny move (for now). There’s more to It’s All About Love than a ludicrous price tag, though: the Brisbane trio led by Conwae Burrell moves through crunchy, quirky indie rock that borders on power-pop. It follows a self-titled debut from 2011 and is considerably straighter than that one. Still, there’s a nice mix of cheekiness and sincerity: love is a recurring theme in ?I Love You? and the back-to-back gems ?The Beatles Were Right? and ?10 Songs of Love? (among others), while we’re pretty sure a pastry chef factors into the fuzz-pop glory of album standout ?Obsessed?.

It’s All About Love by Extrafoxx
