12 Days To Paris

By Sam 2011-10-01T00:00:00+00:00

There’s no denying the unbridled free feeling you get when listening to well-executed powerpop. Equal parts The Breakfast Club (that wind-in-your-hair irresponsibility of a teen playing by their own rules back in the 1980s), equal parts The Pixies (dark clothes and shoegazing rubbed in dirt and fuzz), it’s something that grabs you just right and has you flipping your middle finger to the man all while kicking up your heels with reckless abandon.

In Melbourne, back in the early ?80s, this was a common occurrence, as any regular attendee at places like The Seaview Ballroom and the Prince Of Wales will tell you. Bands like Corpse Grinders and Olympic Sideburns drew heavily on ?60s garage, punk and rockabilly, and twisted it just so. Then, of course, came Huxton Creepers, and stages, eardrums and the world at large were never the same again.

These cats cut their chops live. That’s where they belonged, on a stage. Nothing they ever recorded adequately captured what that live set was like – the theatrics of frontman and main songwriter Rob Craw, the rock solid rhythm section of Matthew Eddy on bass and Arch Law on drums, Paul Thomas? guitar wrapping it all up.

When they did come to record, while the results lacked the power so evident in a live setting, it was never by half. Debut 12 Days To Paris* – originally released in 1986 and finally reissued by Fuse – caught them at their best. And yet it does give off the impression of a caged beast; a full-on live band lurking just beneath the surface. This is where this re-release tops most others: the bonus disc. Live versions of crowd favourites ?Shake Some Action? and ?Ramble Tamble? (live on 3LO’s *Sunday Night Live as opposed to at a venue), as well as ?Iceman? and ?Wishing Well? (both recorded at The Ballroom in St Kilda in late 1984), are there to remind us of those heady days when you just couldn’t keep the Creepers off stage.

Huxton Creepers – My Cherie Amour by Fuse Group Australia

Along with the aforementioned live tracks, there are demo versions of a number of the 12 Days* material, a couple unreleased demos and songs lifted from various compilations circa 1984, including ?King Of The Road? from the Au Go Go compilation *Asleep At The Wheel.

Re-mastered and included in full (with four bonus tracks), 12 Days To Paris remains to this day a cult classic. That unmistakable guitar intro courtesy of Thomas as he leads into ?My Cherie Amour? epitomises a time and place for many, while the chugging rock’n?roll of ?Autumn Leaves?, the freewheeling ?Guilty? and the punkish ?King Of The Road? (with a rockabilly twist) are as strong and catchy as when they were first released.

All told, this is as powerful and full a picture of a seminal underground band you’re likely to find. Huxton Creepers were at the top of their respective pile during the mid-?80s, and this set shows why.
