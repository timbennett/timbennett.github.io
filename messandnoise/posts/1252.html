Classics: Magic Hands

By Mess+Noise 2014-09-07T14:00:00+00:00

M+N are asking bands about classic Australian albums – both the idea of a classic and some prime examples – as well as posing a few polite questions.

Answering this week is Alex Badham, who shares the Melbourne electro-pop duo Magic Hands with Lucy Roleff. Their self-produced debut album Let Me Hold You While You Fail came out last Friday through Finger Wave Records. It launches this Friday (Sept 12) at Circle Jerk #9 at The Imperial Hotel in Sydney with Males, Snowy Nasdaq, TV Programmes and Lovely Head, and on Saturday, September 20 at The Gasometer in Melbourne with Zone Out and Alyx Dennison.

Preamble

a. Alex, you’ve directed three videos for the band so far. Was that strong visual component part of Magic Hands from the start?
We’re both pretty active in visual media type stuff, so we’re pretty self-sufficient in that sense. I just like making music videos, and I play in a band – So it seems natural that we’ve already got three videos in the can.

And we’re pretty aware of how we’re not the most riveting band to watch playing live (a common hurdle for electronic bands, I guess), so we definitely knew from the start that wanted to put a lot of work into creating other things for the audience to look at/get hypnotised by when we perform. Video projections, lo-fi LED light show, etc.

b. The album was written between Melbourne, Berlin and rural Victoria. How quickly and/or easily did it all come together, from writing to recording?
Writing and recording is basically all one process for us: we own most of the stuff we need to be able to do it without going into studios. I’m always pretty blown away by how quickly we write songs: we basically get a song 86.458% done after a day or so of writing/tinkering, then spend months and months endlessly slotting that final 13.542% in place.

c. Lucy has a great solo career going. Alex, are you doing anything musically on the side right now? What’s the status of Batman Park and Aleks & The Ramps?
Batman Park was definitely a one-time thing. As much as it was a really super fun and great thing to be involved in, I think we’re all happy to leave it at that. I’ve been intermittently working on a solo thing for the past two years or so that may never get finished. My pals at The Apiary shot a really great music video for one of the songs, which I got naked for. I know everyone’s dying to see that, so it’d be a waste not to release it.

The Ramps – that’s not really a thing anymore. Lucy’s working on her next solo record, which will be KILLER.

What Makes a Classic?

a. What are some defining qualities that make a classic record?
Good songs, well produced, released at the exact moment to be considered part of the zeitgeist, guest spot from Ghostface Killah and a Yngwie Malmsteen guitar solo.

b. Name a recent Australian record that already feels like a classic.
I love the shit outta Work (work, work) by HTRK. I haven’t properly gotten around to listening to their new one yet. It’s probably real great though.

c. What was the first classic Australian record someone introduced you to?
Hmmm – not sure if anyone considers it a ?classic? but I have vague memories of liking a Screaming Jets album when I was a kid. Looking at their Wikipedia now, I think I had Tear of Thought on cassette. That’s a good example of cover art representing the music really well – in that it’s truly fucking awful.

d. Is there a classic ?Australian sound?? If so, what is it?
If I was playing a word-association game and someone said ?Classic Australian sound? I’d probably sing the synth line from ?Great Southern Land? back at them. (You know – ?Great southern Land? bow-bow-bow.?)

e. Who’s your favourite Australian producer, past or present?
That’s a tough one. I don’t really know the careers of many producers that well. I love the production on the second Gerling record, when they went from scrappy guitar band (which was awesome) to spacey disco dance act. That was probably self-produced right? So I’m going to go with Gerling. Yeah.

Five Potential Classics

Tell us what these five contenders for classic album status mean to you:

Kylie Minogue Ultimate Kylie (2004)
It took me awhile to come around on Kylie: I used to write her off as a poor man’s Madonna (with added disco) but I am now wholeheartedly a Kylie fan. Gosh – feels good to finally get that out in the open. I’m not so into her early hits, or that weird wine-bar-chillout style production she has occasionally, but she’s got some awesome slick and overproduced pop gems. How good is ?Slow?? SO GOOD.

Tone EP by Magic Hands

Skyhooks Living in the 70s (1974)
I can recall three Skyhooks tracks off the top my head, and I hate ?em all. Mostly just because Red Symonds is a cock. I’m not going to get into it here, but I had a weird run-in with him in North Fitzroy once, from which we both emerged as massive jerks, but him more so.

The Go-Betweens: choose your favourite album of theirs
So yeah, this is an opinion that I usually keep to myself, because they seem to be one of those infallible bands that gets everyone right here taps on heart-area-chest-region-place] but I don’t really like The Go-Betweens. I actually can’t name any of their albums. They made that [Prime TV song right? Guess that’s an OK song. (I don’t speak for Lucy on this one. She likes them)

Sunnyboys Sunnyboys (1981)
Hmm – I’ve definitely seen the name before, but it’s not ringing a bell. I have no idea who this band is.

OK so I looked them up and they sing that ?alone with you toniiiiiight? song. Cool. I wonder if they named themselves after those weird triangular ice confections that were really difficult to eat? Obviously I have nothing insightful to say about this record/band.

You Am I Hourly, Daily (1996)
There was a period of time when I was kid that I thought this band was written ?U.M.I?. What a little doofus I was. All of their earlier albums meld into the same one in my head, so I’m not entirely sure what songs are on this one. Guess I could got on le interwebs and reacquaint myself with their discography, but I feel like that’d be cheating. Plus, I got shit to do.

Side note: My friend?s uncle [Wayne Connolly] won an ARIA for producing this record, and I held it in my hands. (Let’s be honest, the only time I’m going to hold an ARIA.) And sweet jesus those things are deadly blocks of heavy, sharp metal. You could hijack a plane with one, if you could get it through security.
