Holy Balm

By Shaun 2011-01-01T00:00:00+00:00

Holy Balm capture the exhaustion of revelry. Best known in Sydney for their unashamedly sloppy electronic dance music, on record this three-piece sound like the frazzled end of an all-night bender. This is dance music where all the components are bludgeoned to a pulp: narcoleptic synth sirens bleed across bars while ghostly vocals dip in and out of focus independent of tempo. If dance music is meant to be functional, then Holy Balm sound like automation equipment and robotic systems cobwebbed, rusted, and with a malignant mind of their own.

This single follows two releases the group have issued since forming in the mid-?00s, and neither their live cassette on Near Tapes nor their 2006 split CD with Vincent Over the Sink are representative of where they’re at nowadays. What’s interesting about Holy Balm in 2011 is how, despite the claustrophobic dissonance of their sound, the locked-in rhythm section compels you to move despite the surrounding melee. There’s a dearth of light in Holy Balm’s music: it’s all shades of moonlit grey and colour-cellophaned low-wattage light bulbs, but that’s what makes them so strange and – especially in a live environment – so appealingly ritualistic. What sounds joyous in a room of people becomes, on this record, menacing in solitude.
