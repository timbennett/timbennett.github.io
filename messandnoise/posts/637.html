New Noise: Brat Farrar, Painkillers, King Gizzard, March Of The Real Fly

By Darren Levin 2011-06-14T14:00:00+00:00

A noticeably garage-y edition of our regular wrap-up of the best new music on the internet.

? Music to rob banks to. This is how King Gizzard and The Lizard Wizard describe their new single ‘Black Tooth’. To be honest it’s more like music to sniff glue and surf to, but who’s going to argue with a bunch of dudes who rob banks? ?Black Tooth? is the first single from the seven-piece’s second EP, Willoughby’s Beach. It was recorded live in one ?sweat-soaked, adrenaline-fueled? session at Paul Maybury’s warehouse in the Melbourne suburb of Fairfield. They’ll be launching it across the east-coast in July.

Black Tooth – King Gizzard And The Lizard Wizzard by showoffservices

? More lo-fi skuzzy-pop from Sam Agostino whose latest incarnation Brat Farrar sounds like a confluence of all his previous bands: Melbourne pub rock mainstays Digger and The Pussycats, abrasive noise merchants Kamikaze Trio and the hard rocking Russian Roulettes. Limited edition debut 7? ?It’s On Me? is out now on German label P Trash. It’ll be launched at The Tote In Melbourne (where else?) on July 2 and at Brisbane’s The Jubilee Hotel on July 9 with Simon and Andre Fazio from YIS joining Agostino live. An international tour is also in the works.

Its On Me – Brat Farrar by Mess+Noise

? Joe Bludge’s love of Jonathan Richman really shines through on ?Tomorrow?, a track lifted from The Painkillers? third album Feel the Pain*. Boasting an ARIA Hall of Famer in drummer James Baker (The Scientists, Beasts of Bourbon, Hoodoo Gurus), the duo formed in 2005, claiming to have played more shows in their native Perth than any other act since. Out now through Off The Hip, *Feel The Pain has been described by the band as ?their finest recording yet?. The Painkillers will be playing three shows in Melbourne this weekend: an in-store at Off The Hip on June 17; a support for Midnight Woolf at Yah Yahs that evening; and a show at Lyrebird in Ripponlea on June 18.

Tomorrow – The Painkillers by Mess+Noise

? Blue Mountains four-piece March of the Real Fly recorded their EP, the appropriately titled Live at the Dungeon*, for $2 – but it sounds like at least $80 worth of weed went into it. Punctuated by some nice harmonica blasts, ?Morning Man? sounds like something off The Brian Jonestown Massacre’s *Thank God for Mental Illness, which was famously recorded for $17.36. (Take that Anton!) A new single, ?Mystery (To You)?, will be out in early July.

Morning Man – March Of The Real Fly by Mess+Noise
