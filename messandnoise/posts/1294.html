New Noise: Howard Eynon, Violet Swells, Slow Galo, Yon Yonson

By Mess+Noise 2014-11-18T13:00:00+00:00

It’s a dreamy week for new music, including a 40-year-old folk record.

? Ever heard of Howard Eynon? If so, you’re one of not very many: the Australian folkie is dubbed ?virtually unknown? in the presser for Earth Recordings? reissue of his 1974 debut album, So What If Im Standing in Apricot Jam* sic]. Born in England, Eynon moved to a Tasmanian dairy farm with his family at age 11 before hopping a motorbike to Melbourne at 17. He got work as a theatre actor and even wound up with small film roles in *Mad Max* and *The Man from Snowy River*. Originally released via Basket/Candle Records but long since out of print, his sole album sounds in some places like a lot of great 1970s folk – singsong-y and stripped-down – but in other places it’s ambitiously coloured with Mellotron, horns, piano, violin, synth and double bass. Likened to Paul McCartney’s cult-classic solo LP *[Ram)* in the presser, *Apricot Jam is indeed an oddity well worth rescuing from the dustbin of time. Check out three songs below, including the initially Leonard Cohen-esque ?Shadows & Riffs?, and grab the vinyl in black or ?apricot jam? here.



? Unabashed devotees of golden 1960s psych-pop, Hobart’s Violet Swells sound positively out of time on their aptly titled new single ?Here Comes Yesterday?. It’s all gauzy and gooey, with a whole lot of Beatles love in there. The follow-up to May’s debut EP The Soft Focus, the single comes out this Friday (Nov 21) and heralds the band’s second EP, due early next year through Mirador Records in Australia and Strong Island Recordings in the UK. For now, Violet Swells are launching the single with a fairly far-ranging East Coast tours that culminates in a slot at the Marion Bay Falls Festival, which they scored by winning the Triple J Unearthed competition.

? Two-thirds of spacey Melbourne pop trio HOY make a slight detour as Slow Galo, a slow-going dream-pop duo. Their self-titled debut EP starts off with the sighing deliberateness of ?Canadian Winter? – complete with some spoken-word recitation – and proceeds through snappy retro pop (?Broadcast?), French-language sensuality (?Lou?), whispered folk (?Sans Soleil?) and a smouldering duet (?Your Company?), among other atmospheric threads. The EP also features cameos from Daniel Twomey (Lower Plenty, Deaf Wish) and Jimmy Saunders (Colour Bomb, Gotye). It launches next Wednesday (Nov 26) at Gasometer with support from The Icypoles and Sugar Fed Leopards? David Bramble. Slow Galo also play tonight (Nov 19) in The Tote’s front bar supporting DD & The Damaged Goods along with Lieutenant Jam; more info here.

Slow Galo by Slow Galo

? Sydney trio Yon Yonson dropped their debut album Hypomantra* with little fanfare last December, and now they’ve done it again with second LP *It’s Natural. On first listen it feels just as genre-blind as the first, although there are several prominent identifiers: there are definite whiffs of Animal Collective, as well as an adaptation of the ?radiator song? ?In Heaven? from Eraserhead. But mostly it’s something like borderless psychedelic R’n?B, floating along on its own whimsical impulses. Topped off with gently mind-bending cover art by Lachie Hinton, the album closes with the August single ?No Enemy?. It’s soft-focus immersion done right.
