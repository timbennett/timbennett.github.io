Track by Track: Thee Gold Blooms

By Mess+Noise 2015-01-28T13:00:00+00:00

Perth garage quartet THEE GOLD BLOOMS unpack their self-titled debut album, mostly recorded live in a ?very small bedroom.?

?She Said No?

This poor guy is experiencing a bad case of unrequited love. This girl isn’t having a bar of him at all.

?Natalie?

Dylan takes the frogman lead vocal on this one. That croaky voice and delivery harkens back to a lot of novelty songs from the early ?60s, and Peruvian legends Los Saicos especially. We did it as a goof and a spoof then we decided to keep it in. The song itself is about a real free spirit.

?Katie-Sue?

This one has a bit of a country shuffle to it and is a favourite live. We usually end our shows with this one because people are really quick to pick up on the ?You got to be my baby? chorus and drunk people love to sing along to it.

?We Can Hide?

When we started, we wrote a bunch of songs inspired by the naivety of early-?60s pop. When all the pop bands were clean-cut and innocent, before they discovered weed and acid. Lyrically it’s about leaving the rat race and finding your own little paradise.

?Alana?

This one was written before we even formed the band, at a time when our bass player and vocalist – the amazingly orange-bearded ?Goldy? – was in the very early stages of seeing a girl. We were teasing him like primary school kids: ?lovvverrrssss.? Then Jase kinda adlibbed this whole song about how he has eyes for no one else etc. They’re still together and the real Alana (Alanna Kusin) does a lot of our artwork, including the album cover and that triumphant photo of us in front of the Emu Export sign.

?Night Time?

Another song in that late-?50s/early-?60s vibe. This is a pretty little ballad using the classic doo wop chord progression and is our first real chance to layer up some vocal harmonies on the chorus. Always goes down well live. Jase wrote and sings this one. He wrote it after he and Dylan spent the weekend camping at the Wave Rock Weekender a few years back. Poor guy missed his girlfriend. So romantic.

?My Girlfriend?

Also written after that fateful weekend at Wave Rock. It’s a great one to do live. Not many lyrics but it’s a good one to chant: ?I will return back here in summer with my girlfriend and a bottle of wine!?

?Some Girls?

This is a play on the old saying ?Some girls will and some girls won’t.? The funny thing about this song is the amount of depraved, kinky stuff that people think the song is about. People will give us a knowing wink and say ?I know what that song is about ay – it’s about @#$!%&*!@#$ isn’t it?? It’s all a bit too saucy really.

?Let Me Know?

Goldy’s big chance. This is a garage punk song about gettin? too boozed and forgetting your entire night then asking people to try and fill in the gaps the next day. Classic Goldy.

?Thee Gold Blooms? Boogie?

This is a surf guitar track with a fun, stompin? groove. We shout out some stuff about the band throughout, or maybe we’re shouting something about our godfather Jeff Goldblum.

?Do You Really Want Me??

We were going for a bit of a Motown feel with this song. If you were to pull the lyrics apart, it’s about a sexless relationship (?If you’re tired, well I’m tired too?) and ain’t nobody got time for that. We haven’t played it live for some reason.

?Please Wake Me?

On a similar theme, this is about a guy who goes out every night and comes home drunk to his patient girlfriend … then promptly passes out. This is his pledge to the girl that he will come home sober and stay awake all night and treat her the way she should be treated.
