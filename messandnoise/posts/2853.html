Amusements

By timscottt 2013-12-15T00:00:00+00:00

I can imagine Jake Robertson and Billy Gardener blasting Chrome, The Screamers and Devo on late-night drives down the Princes Highway. Heading home to Geelong after a Melbourne show from one of their many bands, which include The Living Eyes, The Frowning Clouds, Leather Towel and Hierophants. Talking about the night’s gig over an illuminated dashboard and a can of Pringles, and arguing whether Devo’s Duty Now for the Future* is a better album than *Freedom of Choice.

These late-?70s/early-?80s US New Wave punk bands have had a weighty influence on Gardner and Robertson’s synth-heavy Ausmuteants. This obsession (a ?DEVOtion?, if you will) with jittery and jerky punk was most evident on the duo’s 2012 Split Personalities* cassette released on Gardner’s Anti Fade Records, some songs of which were later re-recorded for the *100 Ausmuteants Fans Can’t Be Wrong – 100 Bon Jovi Fans Can 7?. Full of juvenile and puerile lyrics backed by a frantic fuzzed-out sound, it was as batshit as it was amazing.

Since then Jake (synth, guitar) and Billy (drums) have added guitarist Shaun Connor of Canberra hardcore mob Eye Gouge and bassist Marc Dean from The Bonniwells to flesh out a more substantial sound. Don’t worry, the frantic and breakneck yelly punk remains, but the additions help stabilise the sound. Maybe it’s just louder.

Despite the seemingly cartoony nature of some of his songs, the gangly Robertson (a fan of Peter Bagge’s Hate comic series who for his age has an impressive knowledge of music and albums of all styles) is an impressive songwriter. Bashing down on his Korg Poly-800 and screaming about how it’s been a bad day, week, month and year, he seems geeky but also very cool.

Songs such as ?Pissed Myself Twice? and ?No Motivation? come across as awkward confessionals mixed with weary observations. ?Hate This Town? takes on a slower, more introspective tone, with Jake singing about his frustration of living in a place he finds musically stagnant: ?I hate this town and everyone making sounds.? It’s not clear if the town in question is Geelong or Melbourne, but when he sings ?It all sounds the same, it all sounds so tame,? maybe he means both.

There’s no questioning the shitty situations of both ?Kicked in the Head by a Horse? and ?Stepped in Shit?, the two buzzy tracks opening the B-side. Billy takes over vocals on ?Tinnitus?, one of the strongest tracks on the album, and pushes it hard behind sold drumming and a cool guitar line from Connor.

?Pissing in Two Streams?, the band’s best track both live and on the album, is just a great driving punk song where the synth, drumming and guitars meld into one synchronised head nod.
