Track By Track: Lo!

By Mess+Noise 2013-07-07T14:00:00+00:00

The second album from Sydney quartet Lo! absorbs a healthy spectrum of heavy music, including black metal, punk, mathcore, progressive metal and stoner rock. Guitarist CARL WHITBREAD takes us through ?Monstrorum Historia?.

?As Above?

The first half of this track was actually originally written for a trailer for an Australian horror mini-series. It ended up getting rejected but I had always been a big fan of it and thought it would make the perfect opener for Monstrorum. The heavier part of this track was initially a separate idea altogether, but it just fit so well with the first half that we decided it should be used for the intro. When we recorded this, we set up a ?drum circle? and got in our good friend and fellow drummer Ben Ellingworth to bash some toms with Adrian [Griffin, drums] in the studio.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Bloody Vultures?

This was a pretty stock-standard fast song that we thought would be the perfect way to kick off the album. The riffs were a little Dillinger Escape Plan-inspired.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Ghost Promenade?

The opening riff was actually written at the end of the Look and Behold* days, but it never became a full song. We then started using it live as an intro to our set. We wanted to try and include it on *Monstrorum somewhere and it fit perfectly at the beginning of this track. Some of the influences for this track came from Mastodon, Refused and Breach. The repetitive riff in the middle of the song is probably the hardest guitar part to play on the whole album and the timing of the bass and drums was quite tricky for all of us, so this required a bit more practice than the other tracks.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Caruncula?

This is one of our more traditionally structured songs. Most of it was written by [vocalist Jamie-Leigh Smith]. The main riff reminds me a little of Queens of the Stone Age, which is always a good thing. We also had to chuck a few gallops in the bridge for good measure. Definitely one of the more ?rock?-sounding songs on the album.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Haven, Beneath Weeping Willows?

This piece of music was the last song written for Monstrorum. We felt the album needed a bit of breathing space in the form of a quieter track. I had that bass riff lying around for a while which I hadn’t used for anything, so we basically jammed it out in the recording studio and all the layers built up from there. Once again we got Ben Ellingworth to help out with the extra percussion pieces.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Fallen Leaves?

This was one of the first tracks written for the album and is a personal favourite of mine, as it has a little bit of everything in it. The main heavy riff at the end was written first; then we decided to work backwards from that, stripping it down for the intro and having elements of the same motif appear throughout the song, but not actually bringing in the full riff until the very end. This is probably our most dynamic song on the album.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Crooked Path: The Strangers Ritual?

We were pretty keen to follow the same path as our last album and have some ambient sections on this one too, which is how this song came about. The main ?violin? sounds your hear are actually an acoustic guitar being strung by a bow, which Jamie recorded at home. The rest of the sounds were built around that. The voice you hear is a transmission of a female cosmonaut who apparently burnt up during reentry, which we found absolutely terrifying.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Lichtenberg Figures?

Another personal favourite, this happy accident managed to incorporate elements of black metal, punk and rocky sing-a-longs all in the one track. The fast parts on this song were mainly Griffin’s ideas. He’s a bit of a blast beat connoisseur, so it was nice to hear him let loose. Tom Lyngcoln from The Nation Blue/Harmony also lent his voice to this. We had already recorded it and were doing the backup vocals ourselves for the ?It carries on? part, and we thought it had a bit of a Nation Blue vibe to it and that Tom’s voice would work perfectly for it. So we got in touch with him and he recorded his parts in his home studio back in Melbourne and sent them to us.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Bleak Vanity?

This track was also mostly written by Jamie and it definitely one of the more progressive songs on the album. It reminds me of The Ocean in parts. The beat at the beginning was a nice last-minute touch.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?Palisades of Fire?

This song was written months before we had even planned on releasing a new album. We’d also been playing it live, as we wanted something new in the set. The call-and-response chant at the start was always in mind when writing the song and Jamie finessed it into the final result. [Bassist Adrian] Shapiro also lent his voice to the first word of each line, which we thought gave it a nice contrast. The weird solo at the end (which some people thought was a keyboard) is a guitar running through a shitload of reverb and a Micro POG pedal.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!

?So Below (Before We Disappear)?

This is the slowest and heaviest track on the album, and we thought it would make the perfect close. We normally tune to B standard, but this track is all in drop A, which is something we did on the previous album (for ?Hued Tarantula?) and wanted have on this album also. There is a nice combination of riffs from both myself and Jamie in this song, drawing more from our stoner rock influences. We also wanted to have Lo’c Rossetti from The Ocean sing a part on the album and we thought the end of this track was the perfect part for him. It really helped bring the album to a climatic close. He did his parts back home in his studio in Switzerland and sent them to us.

Monstrorum Historia by Lo!
