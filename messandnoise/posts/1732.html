Lights & Sirens

By David 2008-03-01T00:00:00+00:00

To be blunt, taking a leaf from Martin Hughes’ Slow Guide To Melbourne book, I am heading down the path of the slow record review. This would most likely take the form of bottling a record up, like a preserved fig, for a year or two and slowly letting it converse with my head. Rather than a listen or two in a distracted state, and then scrawling out “sounds like Iggy”, I would prefer to let an album become a friend, and then crucify it in the twenty-teens.

That is plainly not going to happen this time, but can it please serve as a caveat that I think I will really need a lot longer with Lights & Sirens than the paltry two weeks I’ve had so far to do it justice. E-Wah Lady makes stuff go, with her excellent command of tunes and what sounds like quite a mature and realistic view of life. That’s where I am at. She drives the songs a lot more with her voice and its melody, rather than with the tasteful and interesting backing which is as often a stab in the back to the song as it is a gentle soothing hand upon it.

Sometimes, also, E-Wah Lady switches from singin’ to stern talking, like on ‘(It’s a) Lovely Night To Be Alone’, which is the kind of song Patti Smith should be writing but can’t, anymore, from what I can gather, and would anyway put some shonky longhaired band behind her to do it. Take out Patti Smith’s name and put Chrissie Hynde’s, same story. Though I only mention those ladies not so much to dis their present-day work as to give you a context for EWL if you have none.

This song in particular is strident and aggressively appealing, but with the emphasis on the appealing bit, and some great weird whistling at the end or is that outside. The best song of the bunch, if it was a competition, would be the lilting, lazily cataclysmic ‘Someone Else’s Daughter’ and it’s something you have to hear.

I will tell you why it’s the best song in March 2010 OK.
